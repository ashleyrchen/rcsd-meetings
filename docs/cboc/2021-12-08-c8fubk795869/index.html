Citizens' Bond Oversight Committee Wednesday, December 8, 2021 1. Opening Items 1.1 Call to Order The meeting was called to order at 5:32pm by Shirley Cantu, committee chair. 1.2 Roll Call X Shirley Cantu - Chair Taxpayer Organization X Paul Cavagnolo Community-at-Large Karen DiLullo Business Organization X Brigit Espinosa &ndash; Vice Chair Community-at-Large X Mike Foulkes District/College Support Organization X Bill Hult Senior Organization X Travis Ginn WVC Student Organization 1.3 Public Comments (three minute limit per person ) None. 2. Regular Order of the Agenda 2.1 Approval of the Order of the Agenda The order of the agenda was approved as presented. (6/0/0, with DiLullo absent and not voting) 2.2 Approval of the June 9, 2021, meeting minutes The June 9, 2021, minutes were approved as presented. (5/0/1, with DiLullo absent and Espinosa abstaining) 3. Action Items 3.1 Review and Acceptance of the FY 20/21 Measure C and Measure W Financial and Performance Audit Reports Mr. John Dominguez, from CWDL Public Accountants, presented the Measure C and Measure W Financial and Performance Audits. The purpose of the financial audit is to verify that the numbers presented by staff are accurate. The financial audits for Measure C and Measure W both &ldquo;present fairly&rdquo; and were issued an &ldquo;unmodified opinion&rdquo; meaning that they were clean audits. The purpose of the Performance audit is to ensure that expenditures are in line with bond language and are being spent correctly. Mr. Dominguez reported that, for Measure C, the firm selected a sample of expenditures totaling approximately $25 million, and that the sample was selected to provide a representation across specific construction projects, vendors and expenditure amounts. The sample represented 56.5% of the total expenditure value. The performance audit found no exceptions and reported that funds for Measure C are being spent appropriately. For Measure W, the firm selected a sample of expenditures totaling approximately $17 million, and that the sample was selected to provide a representation across specific construction projects, vendors and expenditure amounts. The sample represented 64.7% of the total expenditure value. The performance audit found no exceptions and reported that funds for Measure W are being spent appropriately. For context, industry standard is that 25% of the total expenditure value is sampled for performance audits. Mr. Dominguez thanked management for the secure access to electronic documents and the ease and transparency in which they were presented. The firm felt very comfortable with the results of the audits. There was a motion by Hult, seconded by Espinosa, to &ldquo;accept the Measure C and Measure W Financial and Performance Audits as presented.&rdquo; The motion passed 6/0/0 with DiLullo absent and not voting. 3.2 Election of Chair and Vice Chair for calendar year 2022 There was a motion by Cantu, seconded by Hult, to elect Espinosa as chair and Foulkes as vice chair for calendar year 2022. The motion passed 6/0/0 with DiLullo absent and not voting. 4. Reports 4.1 Chancellor's Report The Chancellor was not in attendance. No report was provided. 4.2 Construction and Financial Update Ms. Dabalos presented the Project Progress Report for West Valley College and Mr. Castruita presented the Project Progress Report for Mission College. Mr. Hult thanked staff for the excellent, well-developed information and the beautiful visual presentation. Mr. Castruita reported that the Measure W Project Priority List has been revised twice since the last meeting. In September, the following revisions were made: DS-03 Alternative Energy Projects at WV & MC. The budget for this project will be increased by $500,000 to reflect a new Bay Area Air Quality Management District (BAAQMD) grant. WV-06 Theater Renovation and Expansion. The budget for this project will be increased by $9,970,000 to reflect the new State Capital Outlay allocation. In October, the following revisions were made: Changes were made to five District Services projects to support the West Valley Baseball Complex Renovation project. Funding from these projects will be used to supplement the bid award of all the bid packages as follows: DS-06 Utility & Electrical Upgrades funding: $ 500,000 DS-07 Underground Water & Sewer Upgrades funding: $ 500,000 DS-12 Parking Lots, Roads & Sidewalk Replacement funding: $ 500,000 DS-13 Storm Water Control Upgrades funding: $ 250,000 DS-14 Security, Safety & Monitoring Upgrades funding: $ 250,000 Total: $2,000,000 WV-4 Outdoor Physical Education Facilities Upgrades. The budget for this project will be increased by $7,425,000 to cover awarding all bid packages, a change order contingency, furniture and equipment, and an audio/video system. Funding for this increase is derived from a combination of District Capital Outlay funds and Measure W funds as follows: DS-06 Utility & Electrical Upgrades funding: $ 500,000 DS-07 Underground Water & Sewer Upgrades funding: $ 500,000 DS-10 Administration Building: $2,000,000 DS-12 Parking Lots, Roads & Sidewalk Repl. funding: $ 500,000 DS-13 Storm Water Control Upgrades funding: $ 250,000 DS-14 Security, Safety & Monitoring Upgrades funding: $ 250,000 WV-01 West Valley Program Contingency funding: $3,425,000 Total: $7,425,000 Mr. Castruita and Ms. Ryan reviewed the Measure C and Measure W financials. DS-04 &ndash; Police Building and Yard at West Valley College was highlighted during the review of the Measure W financials. 5. Closing Items 5.1 Staff Member Comments None. 5.2 Committee Member Comments None. 5.3 Adjournment Meeting adjourned at 6:46pm.
