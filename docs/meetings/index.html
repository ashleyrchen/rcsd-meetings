Want to speak at a board meeting?
Anyone can address the Board during the Public Comment period at the start of each meeting. You get 3 minutes to speak on any topic within the Board's jurisdiction.

In person: Pick up a speaker card at the entrance and hand it to the Board Secretary before the meeting starts.
On Zoom: Submit a digital speaker card using the links in the agenda, or use the "Raise Hand" feature. Speaker card links are posted with each meeting's agenda.

Comments in Spanish are welcome — an interpreter is available at every meeting.
Want a topic discussed at a future meeting?
There are several ways to get an item on a future Board agenda:

Submit a proposed agenda item: The most direct way. At least 10 days before a meeting, email your proposed item to the Board President and Superintendent. They set the agenda collaboratively.
Public Comment: Raise the topic during Public Comment at any meeting — Board members often follow up on community concerns.
Contact a Board Member: Email any Trustee directly. Contact info is on the district website.
"Other Business" section: Near the end of each meeting, the Board discusses suggested items for future agendas.
Written communication: Send a letter or email to the Board Secretary at the District Office (750 Bradford St, Redwood City, CA 94063).
