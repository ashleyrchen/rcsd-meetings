CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE AGREEMENT BETWEEN MISSION COLLEGE AND THE BAY AREA COMMUNITY COLLEGE CONSORTIUM TO ENTER INTO CONTRACT FOR STRONG WORKFORCE PROGRAM FUNDING, REGIONAL SHARE FROM ROUND 5 - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees accept the Strong Workforce Program subgrant to Mission College and authorize the Associate Vice Chancellor of Finance and Administration for the West Valley- Mission Community College District to execute and make all necessary arrangements in relation to this grant agreement and any future amendments and augmentations on behalf of the College.

Prepared By: Jackie Escajeda and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Daniel Peck Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Mission College incurs no cost to participate. Funding is provided by the Bay Area Community College Consortium (BACCC) in conjunction with the California Community College Chancellor&rsquo;s Office and is administered by the Cabrillo Community College District, which serves as the fiscal agent. Reference(s) As part of its strategic goals, Mission College is charged with aligning courses and programs with labor market data and industry demands and opportunities. The Strong Workforce Program gives Mission College the freedom to be innovative and explore new ways to address this issue by updating existing Career Education programs to ensure students are competitive and successful in their field of study and generating new programs that target specific labor shortages in our region. It is the work of the College to increase student retention and graduation rates while offering developed industry-informed pathways that prepare students for high paying jobs. It is also an opportunity to create supportive programs and outreach campaigns to serve more disproportionately impacted students and to provide students with cutting edge technology to enhance student learning outcomes. Background/Alternatives The Strong Workforce Program allocates $248,000,000 per year for the purpose of expanding the availability of quality community college career technical education and workforce development courses, programs, pathways, credentials, certificates, and degrees. The legislation provided for 40% of the funds to be distributed through and allocated by the regional consortia to their member colleges for the purpose of funding regionally prioritized projects and programs that meet the needs of local and regional economies. Mission College&rsquo;s Strong Workforce Regional Round 5 Allocations: Expenditure Period for Funds Regional Direct-to-College Base Allocation Regional Incentive Fund Allocation Funds Redirected to Another College Total Contract Funds 7/1/2020-6/30/2022 $385,065 $80,311 $45,000 $420,376 This contract also includes the transfer of funds from Round Five BACCC Regional Joint Ventures (RJV) Projects: Project Funds to be Transferred Transferring Institution Cannabis RJV $45,000 Santa Rosa Junior College The Cannabis RJV effort is led by the Santa Rosa Junior College and the goal of the project is to explore the workforce and entrepreneurial opportunities in one of the fastest growing industries in California. In 2021, the North American legal cannabis market is predicted to reach $24.5 billion with California having the largest state percentage of combined medical and recreational cannabis sales at $7.4 billion. Therefore, the main objective is to bring the California Community College system to the table by exploring workforce pathways and issues around the industry from the perspective of Agriculture, Water and Environmental Technologies, Culinary, Hospitality and Tourism, Health Occupations, and Business/Entrepreneurship. The legislation requires that funds target labor markets where the number of job openings meets or exceeds the supply of candidates from community colleges and from other education and training providers. It also requires that funds be utilized to facilitate alignment of Career Technical Education (CTE) programs across multiple colleges to better serve regional scale industries and labor markets. The Strong Workforce Program is a significant indication of the Governor and the legislature&rsquo;s interest in supporting the Career Technical Education mission and of their desire to build the system&rsquo;s capacity to become important contributors to the regional economies that are essential to California&rsquo;s long-term economic vitality. Coordination The academic deans who oversee CTE programs work collaboratively with the faculty co-chairs of the Mission College CTE Committee on the final list of projects. The CTE committee develops, approves, and oversees an application process for SWP proposals. Successful proposals are then recommended by the CTE Committee to the President. The fiscal reporting agent for the college will be responsible for entering project templates into the State Chancellor&rsquo;s fiscal reporting software (NOVA), submitting quarterly reports and a final report, monitoring the progress of the project s, and informing the CTE committee and college president of that progress. Follow-up/Outcome On behalf of the District, Associate Vice Chancellor Ngoc Chim will sign the agreement and the district&rsquo;s participation in the approved programs will be official. The academic deans will coordinate the College&rsquo;s participation in the projects and will provide progress reports to the CTE committee and the College President. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE PURCHASE ORDER FOR CREDENTIA NURSE AIDE LLC - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approves the creation of an open purchase order to Credentia Nurse Aide LLC for the amount of $200,000 on behalf of the Health Workforce Initiative (HWI) Northern Regional Testing Center (NRTC).

Prepared By: Anita Roman, Cynthia Harrison, and Danny Nguyen Reviewed By: Daniel Peck Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact This purchase order is to procure the Credential, National Nurse Aide Assessment Program (NNAAP) testing materials and services from July 2021 through June 2022. The nurse assistant candidates pay fees that are determined by the California Department of Public Health, which in turn pay for NNAAP testing materials. The fees and the testing materials are managed by the Health Workforce Initiative, Northern Regional Testing Center. There is no fiscal impact to Mission College or the West Valley-Mission Community College District, as costs are fully funded by the nurse assistant candidate fees. Reference(s) As fiscal agent for NNAAP, Mission College is obligated to procure the NNAAP testing materials and services on behalf of the Northern California nurse assistant programs. Background/Alternatives The HWI NRTC was Board of Trustees approved in 2011 and continues to be hosted at Mission College. The HWI NRTC works with the California Department of Public Health and Credentia to manage the HWI NRTC for nurse assistant certification testing. The HWI NRTC Director contracts with multiple community colleges and independent nurse assistant programs to coordinate the nurse assistant certification testing for Northern California. The $200,000.00 will accommodate the estimated 16,000 nurse assistant candidates requiring nurse assistant certification testing for fiscal year 2021/2022. Coordination This purchase order has been reviewed by the HWI NRTC Director and the Vice President of Administrative Services at Mission College. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board of Trustees approval, a purchase order of $200,000 will be submitted to Credentia Nurse Aide LLC for the nurse assistant certification testing materials and services. §

RESOLUTION NO. 21090701 TO ESTABLISH THE 2021-2022 APPROPRIATION LIMIT - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 21090701 to establish the 2021-2022 appropriations limit at $206,362,856.

Prepared By: Linda Wilczewski Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Prior to adoption of its final budget, each state governmental entity must calculate its appropriations limit. The limit that was first calculated in 1979 may be modified each year by a factor composed of a change in student population (FTES) and the lesser of the change in the Consumer Price Index or California per capita income. Although the state is approaching its Gann Limit, few school districts or community colleges have approached theirs. West Valley-Mission CCD&rsquo;s appropriations limit, as calculated according to the provisions of Article XIII-B, is much larger than the District&rsquo;s actual appropriations. The District&#39;s 2021-2022 appropriations subject to limitation is $141,075,115. The District&#39;s 2021-2022 appropriations limit, based on population growth/decline, has been determined to be $ 206,362,856 (worksheet attached). The District&#39;s appropriations subject to the limit for 2021-2022 do not exceed the appropriations limit. Reference(s) Article XIII-B of the California Constitution, commonly referred to as the Gann Limit, was approved by the voters in 1979. Proposition 4 was passed by the voters of the state of California in November of 1979. Later, implementing legislation in the form of SB 1352 was passed in the California legislature and signed by the Governor. SB 1352 requires the following: &quot;Government Code Section 7910: Each year the governing board of each local jurisdiction shall by resolution establish its appropriations limit for the following fiscal year pursuant to Article XIII-B at a regularly scheduled meeting or at a special meeting. Fifteen (15) days prior to such meeting, documentation used in the determination of the appropriations limit shall be available to the public. The determination of the appropriations limit is a legislative act.&quot; Background/Alternatives In accordance with the provisions of Government Code Section 7910, any action to set aside or annul the establishment of the appropriations limit must be filed with the State Chancellor within forty-five (45) days of the adoption of the resolution. Coordination According to law, the &quot;documentation used in the determination of such limit shall be made available to the public fifteen (15) days prior to the actual adoption of such resolution.&quot; In compliance with the requirement, the documentation was made available to the public on August 23, 2021 Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the Gann Limit information will be reported on the CCSF-311 Report, which is due on or before October 10, 2021. §

Reference(s) California Code of Regulations, Title 8, Section 3203 California Constitution, Article IX, Section 14 Education Code, Section 70902 Education Code, Section 76020 Education Code, Section 76403 Education Code, Section 87408 United States Code Title 29 Section 654(a)(1) United States Equal Employment Opportunity Commission Guidance California Community Colleges Chancellor&rsquo;s Office 2021-01 Advisory Background/Alternatives The West Valley-Mission CCD is committed to protecting the health and well-being of students, faculty, staff, administrators, and the communities we serve, as well as maintaining higher education access and attainment for our students. As we work toward the safe resumption of increased on-campus learning, working and other activities, we embrace a comprehensive strategy designed to reduce the likelihood of transmission of the COVID-19 virus. This approach contributes to the overarching goal of achieving population-level immunity throughout the West Valley-Mission CCD. In light of the evidence established to date regarding the safety and effectiveness of available COVID-19 vaccines, the District would require all individuals to be vaccinated against COVID-19 unless approved as exempt due to verified medical or religious reasons. Those who do not receive a COVID-19 vaccination shall wear face coverings and comply with twice weekly mandatory testing. The vaccine requirement would be effective on each campus and in District Services Coordination The proposed Board Policy 3960 titled COVID-19 Vaccination Policy is intended to reduce the likelihood of transmission of the COVID-19 virus. This policy contributes to the District&#39;s overarching goal of achieving population-level immunity through the West Valley-Mission CCD.
