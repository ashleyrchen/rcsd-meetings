Assembly Bill 2449 Remote Teleconference Attendance Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may be eligible to attend a Board of Education meeting remotely with Just Cause or with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance. To ensure compliance with the notice requirements of AB 2449, the Board will maintain an AB 2449 standing agenda item for each meeting. Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Just Cause submissions received for the current Board meeting. Consider and take action on Emergency Circumstance requests, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance and Board approval. Emergency Circumstance means a physical or family medical emergency that prevents the trustee from attending in person. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. Disclosure of individuals present at remote locations, if any, pursuant to AB 2449 . A trustee attending a Board meeting remotely under AB 2449 must publicly disclose whether any other individuals 18 years of age or older are present in the room at the remote location with the trustee, and the general nature of the members relationship with any such individuals. Any trustee attending the current Board meeting remotely under 2449 will make this public disclosure.

Prepared By: LINPING YU, MEHRAN KARIMI, GUY SHANI, VALERIE JENSEN, AND LORRIE RANCK Reviewed By: SEHER AWAN Approved By: BRADLEY DAVIS Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The General Fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. Reference(s) Curriculum development and approval is an ongoing activity overseen by the Academic Senate, and its subordinate Curriculum Committee, and the Office of Instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Degrees developed in compliance with State-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the District Chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between Mission College and West Valley College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. ACCEPTANCE BY WEST VALLEY COLLEGE OF THE EARLY EDUCATION TEACHER DEVELOPMENT GRANT FROM THE SANTA CLARA COUNTY OFFICE OF EDUCATION FOR EDUCATOR PREPARATION PROGRAM - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees authorizes the administration to accept the $200,000 grant from the Santa Clara County Office of Education Educator Preparation Program to provide courses to 40 participants to acquire their Assistant Teacher Permit to enter the workforce and become teachers.

Prepared By: Joshua Moon-Johnson Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact West Valley College will be provided with a $200,000 grant out of an Early Education Teacher Development Grant received by the Santa Clara County Office of Education (SCCOE) to offer teacher training in the 2024-25 academic year. Reference(s) The Santa Clara County Office of Education received an Early Education Teacher Development Grant to increase the quality of instruction in the California State Preschool Programs and Transitional Kindergarten classrooms within Santa Clara County. To that end, the County's Office of Education partnered with higher education institutions, such as West Valley College, by providing them with grant opportunities. Background/Alternatives The Child Studies Department had seven Adult Education students complete the Pathway to Permit Program this past year. This important program reaches students who have either faced employment obstacles due to the pandemic, are English language learners, or are transitioning careers. WVC aims to expand this outreach in partnership with community educational agencies. Coordination Laurie Dotson will be the lead faculty member overseeing the implementation of the Early Education Teacher Development Grant. The grant leadership team will also include the provost of academic affairs, Joshua Moon-Johnson, and the dean of professional studies, Eric Grabiel. Follow-up/Outcome By accepting the Early Education Teacher Development Grant, West Valley College is agreeing to provide instruction for four child studies classes with an IBEST model and ESL co-teaching support. WVC will also assist students in obtaining their child development assistant teacher credentials, as well as their associate teacher permit credentials from the California Teaching Credentialing Department. Students will be provided with job opportunities to further assist with workforce entry. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF FACILITY USE AGREEMENT FEE UPDATES FOR FY 24/25 - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the updated facility use fees as presented.

Prepared By: Terrance DeGray Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The district received approximately $1.3 million in facilities rental revenue in the current fiscal year. As the district applies the new facility use fees, the rental revenue may increase depending on the type of rental. Reference(s) BP 6700 Civic Center and Other Facilities Use AP 6700 Civic Center and Other Facilities Use Education Code 82542 Fees for Use of College Facilities Background/Alternatives This annual review updates the facility use agreement rates for District facility fees charged to both for-profit and non-profit entities. The updates and processes adhere to Education Code 82542 - Fees for Use of College Facilities. This code allows the governing board of a community college district to charge fees for the use of their facilities and grounds by external organizations to ensure costs are covered. The revised fees account for the direct cost increases associated with staff members who support the external rentals of College Facilities, Athletic Fields, and Outdoor spaces. Education Code 82542 requires the governing board to set fees. Coordination The updated rates were coordinated with the event coordinators, vice presidents of administrative services at both colleges, executive director of intercollegiate athletics, vice chancellor of health and public safety, vice chancellor of finance and administration, and vice chancellor of facilities development and operations. Follow-up/Outcome Staff will post the updated rates on the District and College websites and apply them to future Facility Use Agreements, effective July 1, 2024. §

APPROVE CONTRACT INCREASE WITH SMITH-EMERY SAN FRANCISCO FOR SPECIAL TESTING AND INSPECTION SERVICES ON THEATER RENOVATION PROJECT AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve a contract increase with Smith-Emery San Francisco in the amount of $184,479.96 for special testing and inspection services for the Theater Renovation project at West Valley College.

Prepared By: Nini Reyes-Bolinger and Rosa Huynh Reviewed By: Terrance DeGray Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The funding for this project comes from a combination of State Capital Outlay Funds and Measure W projects WV-06 of the West Valley College project priority list. Reference(s) This item is associated with the implementation of the WVC Facilities Master Plan, the WVMCCD 5-Year Construction Plan, and the Measure W Project Priority List. Title 24 – Administrative Regulations requires that a Division of the State Architect (DSA)-approved and qualified Special Testing and Inspection Laboratory be hired for school construction projects. Smith-Emery meets the Title 24 requirements and qualifications. Background/Alternatives On December 19, 2022 the District executed a contract with Smith-Emery San Francisco in the amount of $100,000 for special testing and inspection services for the Theater Renovation project at West Valley College. The new building code requires additional special testing and inspection services to complete the project per DSA requirements. The District had intended to do a voluntary seismic upgrade for the project, however the Division of State Architect informed the District on January 19, 2022 that a full seismic upgrade would be required in compliance with CEBC Section 319.2 per ASCE 41-17 Section 6.2.2. The code section requires a comprehensive level of material testing to be performed as defined in each applicable material chapter of ASCE 41 to determine strength of materials not specified in the original drawings to satisfy the DSA Interpretation of Regulation of Existing Buildings Bulletin #3 (IR EB-3). Additional structural studies and reports assessing the building resulted in additional testing scope and overtime to complete the work during the school breaks to not impact classes. The schedule of work was also impacted due to unforeseen conditions with lead paint that had to be abated in the testing areas. The contract increase will be as follows: Description Fee Original Contract Amount: $100,000.00 Previous Approved Contract Increase: $191,935.00 This Contract Increase: $184,479.96 New Contract Amount: $476,414.96 Coordination The vice chancellor of facilities development and operations, executive director of bond construction, executive director of general services, and Kitchell CEM worked on this item. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, a contract increase will be issued to Smith-Emery San Francisco in the amount of $184,479.96. §
