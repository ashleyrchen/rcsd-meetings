CONSIDER APPROVAL OF CURRICULUM CHANGES AT MISSION COLLEGE Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the curriculum changes at Mission College as presented.

Prepared By: Linping Yu, Mehran Karimi, Guy Shani, Valerie Jensen and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The General Fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. These costs are offset, to varying degrees, by apportionment received for credit-based instruction and other associated fees. Reference(s) Curriculum development and approval is an ongoing activity overseen by the Academic Senate, and its subordinate Curriculum Committee, and the Office of Instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the Mission College Program Review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per District policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and approved by the Curriculum Committee and the Articulation Officer. Each of the curriculum changes and the foregoing approvals has been further reviewed by the chief instructional officer, the college president, and the district chancellor. The Office of Instruction further certifies that the programs and courses described herein satisfy all applicable requirements of Title 5 and have also been submitted to the Associated Students Government of Mission College. Degrees developed in compliance with State-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the District Chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between Mission College and West Valley College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcomes Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the College catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the College catalog and from the state Curriculum Inventory. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF WEST VALLEY COLLEGE PARTICIPATION IN THE 2026 INTERNATIONAL PLANETARIUM SOCIETY CONFERENCE IN JAPAN - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve travel costs of $8,684 for one faculty member and one instructional learning technician to attend the 2026 International Planetarium Society Conference in Fukuoka, Japan, June 18–27, 2026.

Prepared By: Anthony Djedi Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The International Planetarium Society Conference will take place in Fukuoka, Japan, from June 18–27, 2026. The estimated cost of attendance for two college employees is $8,684 Item Cost Registration $1,584 Airfare $2,700 Lodging $1,300 Meals $2,100 Ground Transportation $1,000 ESTIMATED TOTAL COST - 2 ATTENDEES $8,684 Funding will be provided by the West Valley College faculty conference leave committee fund and the general fund. Reference(s) BP7400 states: “Prior Board approval shall be required for any travel at District expense if the total travel expense exceeds or will exceed $10,000 for any one event or purpose, without regard to the number of individuals traveling. ” It further states that " All travel outside the United States must be approved in advance by the Board." Background/Alternatives West Valley College’s Kvamme Planetarium serves as a regional center for astronomy education, immersive STEM learning, and community engagement. Attendance at the International Planetarium Society Conference provides valuable professional development opportunities focused on planetarium operations, educational programming, astronomy outreach, immersive media systems, and emerging presentation technologies. Conference participation will support continued improvements to instructional programming, operational effectiveness, and public engagement at the Kvamme Planetarium while strengthening staff expertise in advanced planetarium systems and immersive technology infrastructure. Attendance also provides access to current industry standards, technical best practices, and professional collaboration opportunities identified as important through a recent external technical evaluation of the planetarium control environment. Coordination The Kvamme planetarium coordinator will facilitate attendance at the conference. Further support will be provided by the dean of the school of science and math and the vice president of administrative services. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, travel arrangements for two attendees at the conference will be finalized. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF AWARD OF RFP 03-2526 FOR FACILITIES USE OF MISSION COLLEGE’S TENNIS COURTS - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve entering into a non-exclusive Facilities Use License Agreement with PNW Tennis, LLC d/b/a Gorin Tennis Academy for facilities use of Mission College’s tennis courts. The term of the agreement will be for a five year term.

Prepared By: John Vlahos and Michael Robins Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact In accordance with the proposal submitted in response to RFP 03-2526, the district will receive use fees from the selected awardee, generating a guaranteed annual tennis court rental revenue of $400,000, along with an additional $20,000 in scholarship donations. Total revenue may exceed the guaranteed base amount depending on facility usage. Reference(s) Pursuant to California Education Code Sections 82537, 82542 and Board Policy/Administrative Procedures 6700, the Civic Center Act allows the district to issue facility use agreements when it does not interfere with educational use. Background/Alternatives The district publicly solicited proposals for RFP 03-2526 for the use of the Mission College tennis courts. Proposals were sent to seven local tennis academies and schools, and three proposals were received and found to be responsive: Name of Bidder Hourly Rate Percentage increase Scholarships/ Gift in Kind PNW Tennis LLC d/b/a Gorin Tennis Academy $40.00 ($400,000 base yearly total proposed) 5% Increase per year $20,000 Tennis Development, LLC. d/b/a/ Eagle-Fustar Tennis $37.00 (With modified hours, $210,000 base yearly total proposed) No increases $0 Ultra Performance Academy $30.00 (With modified hours, $ 78,300 base yearly total proposed) 0% increase first year, after that, a 5%increase, conditional on facility improvements $0 Coordination All three proposals were evaluated by the district’s Contracts Compliance Manager, Buyer, and Administrative Specialist of Athletics, who served as the evaluation committee for this RFP. The Associate Vice Chancellor of Intercollegiate Athletics and the Executive Director of General Services also reviewed and approved the recommendation. PNW Tennis LLC d/b/a Gorin Tennis Academy submitted the proposal with the highest fee remuneration and was therefore selected by the evaluation committee for recommendation to the Board. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the executive director of general services will execute a Facilities Use License Agreement with awardee PNW Tennis LLC d/b/a Gorin Tennis Academy to enter into a non-exclusive five year contract term. §

14.3 · Action | Discussion · Board

Second Reading - BOARD POLICY 5015 on RESIDENCE DETERMINATION - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct a second reading of Board Policy 5015 on Residence Determination, close public comment, and adopt proposed changes.

Prepared By: Chris Dyer Reviewed By: Cade Story-Yetto Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact If approved, the revised BP 5015 would help remove a financial barrier for eligible veterans, active-duty military members, and other qualifying military-connected students by allowing them to receive resident tuition treatment or a nonresident tuition exemption as provided by state and federal law. To the extent this increases enrollment or retains students who might otherwise not enroll, WVMCCD may also generate apportionment-eligible FTES for those students. Reference(s) Title 5 Sections 54000 et seq.; Education Code Sections 68040 , 68086 and 76140 Background/Alternatives This policy language was provided by the Community College League of California’s (CCLC) Policy & Procedure Service, aligning with federal and state law for military-affiliated students . The federal Veterans Access, Choice, and Accountability Act and later amendments, now reflected in 38 U.S.C. § 3679(c) and California Education Code, require public colleges to provide in-state tuition treatment to qualifying veterans and other “covered individuals,” including certain GI Bill and Chapter 31 beneficiaries, or risk VA disapproval of covered programs. BP 5015 makes clear that the normal one-year California residency rule has exceptions for qualifying active military, veterans, and certain military-connected students for tuition purposes. Coordination This policy went through the district’s participatory governance process and was reviewed by the Academic Senates and College Councils of both colleges prior to review by District Council on March 10 and April 14, 2026, and by the Legislative Policy & Oversight Committee on April 22, 2026. Each body voted to recommend full approval by the Board. Follow-up/Outcome At the June 16, 2026, Board of Trustees meeting, approval of Board Policy 5015 on Residence Determination and the conclusion of the public comment period will be considered on the agenda. If adopted, this policy will be publicly available on the WVMCCD website. This policy and the corresponding Administrative Procedure will be incorporated into the websites of both Mission College and West Valley College. Both college’s Enrollment Services offices with adjust residence procedures and fees to align with this adopted language. §
