The grant award period is July 1, 2023, through June 30, 2025. Mission College will prepare to collect and report the information outlined below for the ELL Grant funds in forthcoming annual progress reports. PROPOSITION 30 EDUCATION PROTECTION ACCOUNT (EPA) SPENDING PLAN - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the spending plan for the Education Protection Account (EPA) for fiscal year 2024-25.

Prepared By: Ngoc Chim Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The State continues to provide Proposition 30 revenues in FY 2024-25 to support local schools and community colleges. The funding amount is $100 per FTES. The District anticipates receiving approximately $1,171,400 from the State and intends to use the money for instructional salaries and benefits. A separate sub-fund has been established in the general ledger to track the revenue and expenditures for the EPA funding. After the close of the fiscal year, the District will publish the actual revenue received and the actual expenditures on the District’s website. Reference(s) On November 8, 2016, voters approved Proposition 55, The California Children’s Education and Health Care Protection Act of 2016, which is an extension of Proposition 30 extending the personal income tax increase through 2030-31. The accounting and reporting requirements for Proposition 55 are exactly the same as the requirements for Proposition 30. The language of Proposition 30 requires that each local educational agency”…shall have sole authority to determine how the money received from the EPA are spent…provided, however, that the appropriate governing board or body shall make these spending determinations in open session of a public meeting of the governing board or body and shall not use any of the funds from the EPA for salaries or benefits of administrators or any other administrative costs.” Each local educational agency will be required to annually publish on its website an accounting of how much money was received from the EPA and how that money was spent. ADOPT RESOLUTION NO. 24071601 ENDORSING ASSEMBLY BILL 2093 (SANTIAGO) COMMUNITY COLLEGES: CALIFORNIA COLLEGE PROMISE: FEE WAIVER ELIGIBILITY AND FUNDING FORMULA. (2023-2024)—Recommendation: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 24071601 endorsing Assembly Bill 2093, which would expand the California College Promise Program by allowing qualifying students to pursue a Bachelor&#039;s degree at California community college campuses tuition-free.

Prepared by: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed by: Bradley Davis Approved by: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact California Education Code § 76300 mandates that community college districts charge students an enrollment fee of $46 per unit per semester. AB 2093 proposes to expand the California College Promise, which currently waives tuition for two years for full-time students pursuing an associate’s degree, to include all full-time students pursuing a bachelor's degree at community colleges. The fiscal impact on the district includes the administrative costs of implementing the expanded program and the potential reallocation of existing resources to support the initiative. This expansion aligns with the district's commitment to providing affordable education and supporting students in achieving their educational goals. The impact will only occur if the district receives final approval to offer bachelor's degrees. The legislation grants the district flexibility to determine eligibility requirements and sustain the program without additional state funding. Reference(s) The California Community College Board of Governor’s Fee Waiver (BOGFW) waives enrollment fees ($46 per unit) for students who (1) meet residency criteria; (2) maintain minimum academic and progress standards; and (3) demonstrate financial need via various methods (Education Code § 76300). AB 19 (Santiago, 2017) established the California College Promise, which enables California Community Colleges (CCC) to waive enrollment fees for one academic year for all first time, full-time students (Education Code § 76396). AB 2 (Santiago, 2019) expanded the California College Promise by enabling CCCs to waive enrollment fees for two academic years for first-time full-time students. It also extended the eligibility to include students in the disabled student services program enrolled in fewer than 12 units (Education Code § 76396.3). AB 183 (2022) expanded the eligibility for the California College Promise to full-time returning community college students (Education Code § 76396.3). Background/Alternatives This resolution authorizes the chancellor to work closely with state legislators to champion Assembly Bill 2093. This bill would expand the California College Promise to enable community colleges to waive enrollment fees for all full-time students (12 units or more) pursuing a bachelor’s degree. It aims to provide greater financial support to students pursuing four-year degrees at community colleges, aligning with the district’s mission to make higher education more accessible and affordable. This expansion is expected to encourage more students to enroll in and complete baccalaureate programs, thus contributing to a more educated workforce and supporting California's economic needs. Currently, the WVMCCD has been tentatively approved to offer a baccalaureate degree at Mission College in Emergency Services Administration and at West Valley College in Park Management (subject to final approval). Key provisions of the bill include: Extending the California College Promise to cover an additional two academic years for first-time community college students and returning community college students who matriculate into upper-division coursework of a community college baccalaureate degree program. Requiring participating students to complete identical requirements to the existing California College Promise, including completing the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) or the California Dream Act application. Allowing students enrolled in fewer than 12 units to be deemed "full time" if certified as such by a staff person in the disabled student services program at the institution. Ensuring that students who have previously earned a degree or certificate from a postsecondary educational institution are not eligible for the fee waiver. Permitting students who are members of the Armed Forces and called to duty to withdraw from participation in the program and resume upon their return without losing eligibility. Considering high school pupils who enroll in community college courses and subsequently enroll in community college after high school graduation as first-time community college students. Coordination Resolution No. 24071601 was reviewed by District Council on July 8, 2024, and by the Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on June 25, 2024. AB 2093, if passed, will require community colleges to comply with the updated provisions of the California College Promise. This includes reporting requirements for the Chancellor’s Office of the California Community Colleges and ensuring that participating students meet the eligibility criteria as outlined in the bill. Follow-up/Outcome If adopted, the resolution will be publicly posted on each campus and available on the West Valley-Mission Community College District website. In addition, the approved Resolution No. 24071601 will be provided to the Chancellor’s Office of the California Community Colleges, the Department of Finance, and the appropriate members of the Legislature. The district will ensure compliance with the annual reporting requirements as specified in AB 2093, which include documenting the use of funds and demonstrating adherence to the program’s conditions. §

This policy is being updated to incorporate recent changes in California Education Code and Title IX changes. Major changes include: Title IX Exception: A notable addition is the exception for conduct constituting sexual harassment under Title IX. Such cases are now addressed separately under AP 3433 and AP 3434, rather than AP 5520. Permission from District Employee: The requirement for obtaining written permission to possess certain objects from the District Police and Vice President of Student Services is replaced with obtaining permission from any District employee, concurred by the college president. Expanded Definitions: The revised statement expands the definitions of prohibited behaviors to include sexual assault, sexual exploitation, harassment based on additional categories (disability, gender identity, gender expression, nationality, race or ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation), and bullying, including cyberbullying. Inclusion of Intimidating Conduct: Intimidating conduct or bullying through various means, including direct physical contact, verbal assaults, social isolation, manipulation, and cyberbullying, is explicitly addressed. Clarification on Smoking: The smoking prohibition is maintained but specifies that it is in areas prohibited by law or college/District regulations. Inclusion of Sexual Assault: Sexual assault is explicitly mentioned as a separate category of misconduct. Clarification of Reporting: The reporting of misconduct involving injury or death now specifies the involvement of District personnel. These changes enhance the clarity, specificity, and inclusivity of the conduct that may lead to disciplinary action while also addressing Title IX cases separately.
