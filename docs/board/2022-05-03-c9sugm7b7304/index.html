PURCHASE OF A TCOM (Trailer Compressor) TRAILER FOR MISSION COLLEGE 2021-2022. Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve expenditures by Mission College up to $160,000 to purchase a TCOM (Trailer Compressor) Trailer, which is a high-pressure mobile breathing air recharging system.

Prepared By: Jackie Escajeda and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Norma Ambriz-Galaviz Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact This amount is allocated in Mission College&rsquo;s Strong Workforce Regional funds. This purchase will help the Fire Protection Technology (FPT) faculty provide realistic drills to the FPT and Firefighter Academy students, which prepares them for state exams and ultimately a job with a fire agency. Reference(s) n/a Background/Alternatives The TCOM Trailer provides air refilling capability for the self-contained breathing apparatus or air tanks (SCBA). The SCBA provides air for Firefighters who enter an Immediately Dangerous to Life and Health (IDLH) situation. The cadets will become familiar with and practice using the SCBA during the Firefighter 1 Academy. This TCOM Trailer (Air Trailer) allows the SCBA tanks to be refilled immediately, providing more opportunities for the cadets to become proficient in their use. It is portable and can be used at any training location and does not require installation. Currently, FPT faculty and some students travel to fire agencies to refill the air tanks (SCBA). Thus, purchasing the TCOM Trailer in the long run will save time and money and each student will have an opportunity to learn how to refill a SCBA. Coordination The Career Education Division and the FPT Department will work with Purchasing to negotiate final costs, maintenance agreements, and delivery and storage of the TCOM Trailer. Follow-up/Outcome The expected outcome is to provide essential instructional materials to enhance the pedagogy and sustainably supply FPT with resources to maintain and utilize required tools. §

Reference(s) Education Code 76004 (Assembly Bill 288) establishes the College and Career Access Pathways Act to authorize California Community College districts to enter into formal partnership agreements with local school districts. The goal is to create easier pathways from high school to community college and to offer or expand dual enrollment opportunities for under-represented higher education students or for students who may not be college bound. This partnership aligns with the goals of West Valley College&#39;s Educational and Facilities Master Plan and its Student Equity Plan related to improving the student pathways to degrees and transfer, strengthening collaborative partnerships with K-12 institutions, and increasing access and success for underrepresented groups. This agreement is also being presented to the CUHSD Board and may undergo changes before it is returned. Background/Alternatives Students who complete college credit while enrolled in high school, are more likely to earn high school diplomas, to enroll in community colleges and four-year colleges, to attend post-secondary education on a full-time basis, and to complete degrees in those institutions, than students without these experiences. CUHSD is a public-school district serving grades 9-12 with students whose residences are located within the regional service area of WVMCCD and these students deserve an opportunity to participate in an accelerated process that can further ensure academic success. This CCAP Agreement allows students to pursue this dual enrollment opportunity. Courses offered as part of this CCAP Agreement shall be community college courses acceptable towards a career technical education credential or certificate, or preparation for transfer, or appropriate to improve high school graduation rates or help high school pupils achieve college and career readiness. Coordination The operational and instructional aspects of this agreement will be administered and monitored by the West Valley College vice-provost and dean of instruction. An educational administrator to be specified in the appendix to the CCAP Agreement will serve as point of contact to facilitate coordination and cooperation between the college and CUHSD, in conformity with WVMCCD policies and standards. Follow-up/Outcome After the proposed Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Dual Enrollment Partnership Agreement is approved by the WVMCCD Board of Trustees and the CUHSD Board, West Valley College will submit the agreement to the CCC Chancellor&rsquo;s Office for review and coordinate with CUHSD staff to implement the pathway in preparation for the next cohort in the fall 2022 semester. §

Prepared By: Rebecca Alvarez Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) Periodically, the District evaluates its mission statement to ensure that it reflects the students served, the programs offered, and the ways the community is strengthened by its presence. If the mission statement is found in need of revision, a cooperative process of development and review has begun. The District&#39;s mission statement was last updated on September 6, 2007. Following a serie s of open sessions with District Council , the draft Mission Statement was shared with the academic senates, classified senates, and student government at both colleges a nd approved on April 11, 2022, by di strict c ouncil for recommendation to the Board of Trustees. The new mission statement is as follows: The West Valley-Mission Community College District unites our colleges in a shared vision of educational excellence where students representing diverse experiences and identities feel connected and supported in pursuit of their goals and aspirations. Coordination Th e process for the development of the ne w District Mission Statement was coordinated by Chancellor Bradley Dav is through di strict council. Input was received from across the District , and members of District Council partnered with their constituents to identify language to be included. The final statement was drafted by English department faculty and district council member Bill Davis and approved by d istrict c ouncil on April 11, 2022. Follow-up/Outcome Following adoption by the Board of Trustees, staff in respective areas will publish the new District Mission Statement in all required locations, including the college catalog and online. First Reading: UPDATED BOARD POLICY 6250 BUDGET MANAGEMENT - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct the first reading of the updated board policy on budget management which will set the minimum reserve to be no less than 10% and no more than 17% (equivalent to two months’ worth of expenditures) of total unrestricted general fund expenditures and consider public comment.

Prepared By: Ngoc Chim Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The district currently sets aside a 5% reserve, which is approximately $8.3 million, and a 3% contingency reserve, which is approximately $5 million. The district intends to add an estimated $14.4 million to the reserve totaling $27.7 million which is equivalent to two months&rsquo; worth of expenditures. The amount will fluctuate based on the operating expense in the unrestricted general fund. Due to current challenges facing the system brought on by the global pandemic and the uncertainty in the economy, having a sufficient unrestricted reserve is critical to a district&rsquo;s ability to operate effectively and maintain fiscal resiliency in difficult times. Reference(s) The State Chancellor&rsquo;s Office issued memorandum FS 22-03 on February 1, 2022, providing an overview of the Fiscal Forward portfolio for monitoring districts fiscal health and recommendations related to districts budget architecture and development. The State Chancellor&rsquo;s Office is recommending that districts adopt formal policies to maintain sufficient unrestricted reserves with a suggested minimum of two months of total general fund operating expense. Coordination The board policy was reviewed with the Fiscal Workgroup on April 4, 2022, and presented to the District Council for review on April 11, 2022. The Audit and Budget Oversight Committee reviewed the policy on April 11, 2022. The Legislative and Policy Oversight Committee reviewed the policy on April 13, 2022. Follow-up/Outcomes After the first reading and review of the policy by the various committees, the district will take the policy back to the same committees and the Board for the second reading in May. §

Second Reading: Board Policy 3721 on Information Security. Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct a first reading of Board Policy 3721 on Information Security and close public comment.

Prepared By: Rose Nelson Reviewed By: Dan Borges Approved By: Brad Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) Section 502(c) PC and 530.5 PC; California Civil Code 1798.29, 1798.82, and 1798.84; California Government Code &sect;6250-&sect;6270.5; Family Education Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA); California Community Colleges Information Security Standard; California Community Colleges Data Security Standard; NIST Special Publication 800-171 Revision 1 Background/Alternatives This procedure will establish an Information Security framework that allows district personnel to take appropriate measures to safeguard personally identifiable information, including, but not limited to, employee and student records, from inadvertent or unlawful disclosure. Coordination The plan for this policy was coordinated by the Associate Vice Chancellor Information and Educational Technology with key technology staff as well as with members of the Technology Advisory Coordination (TAC) team. The policy was reviewed by District Council on March 7,2022 and April 11, 2022, and by LPOC on April 13, 2022. Follow-up/Outcome At the May 17, 2022, meeting of the Board of Trustees, approval of the BP 3721 Information Security and closure of the public comment period will be considered. If the policy is adopted it will be publicly posted on the WVMCCD website. §
