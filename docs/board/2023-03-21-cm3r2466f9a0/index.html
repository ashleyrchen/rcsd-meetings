Draft by: Manny Cappello Reviewed by: Funding Source/Fiscal Impact West Valley-Mission Community College District would use District contributions of local funds along with potential SB 169 State funding. Reference(s) Board Policies 2200 (Board Duties and Responsibilities), 2210 (Officers), 2220 (Committees of the Board), 2305 (Annual Organizational Meeting), 2310 (Regular meetings of the Board), 2340 (Agendas), 2345 (Public Participation at Board Meetings), 2360 (Minutes), 2365 (Recording), 2410 (Board Policies and Administrative Procedures), 2430 (Delegation of Authority to the Chancellor), 2710 (Conflict of Interest), 2716 (Political Activity), 2720 (Communications Among Board Members), Title 5 sections 53000 et seq., and 59300 et seq.; and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and Senate Bill 893. Background/Alternatives A recent study showed that California community college students are increasingly housing insecure with 1 in 5 students currently homeless. Roughly 400,000 students in the system and 60 percent of community college students in California have experienced recent housing insecurity and 50 percent have struggled with food insecurity in the last year. The mission and values of the West Valley-Mission Community College District are firmly grounded in equity and reflect support for all students in reaching their goals regardless of their background. Housing and food insecurity can seriously impact the mental and physical health and well-being of community college students, especially those already at risk, thereby reducing their opportunities for success. Thirty-one percent of black students reported being without permanent shelter, while 34% of transgender students and 27% of gay and lesbian students lacked stable homes. While our region has seen, over the years, a tech boom that has fueled demand for housing, unfortunately, the necessary supply of housing is well below the demand and is grossly unaffordable to students. In response, the West Valley-Mission Community College District has made provisions for overnight emergency parking for students and community members on our campus parking lots. The Bay Area housing crisis has impacted us all in many ways but, the impact to our students has been devastating. Coordination This resolution would require the West Valley-Mission Community College District to use local unrestricted general funds to provide waivers of tuition and fees. This bill would require the governing board of the West Valley-Mission Community College District to submit a report to the Chancellor&rsquo;s Office of the California Community Colleges, the Department of Finance, and the appropriate committees of the Legislature on the implementation of these provisions, as specified. Follow-up/Outcome If adopted, the resolution will be publicly posted on each campus, available on the West Valley-Mission Community College District website.
