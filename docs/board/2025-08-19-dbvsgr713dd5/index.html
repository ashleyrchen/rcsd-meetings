1. Assembly Bill 2449 Remote Teleconference Attendance Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may be eligible to attend a Board of Education meeting remotely with Just Cause or with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance. To ensure compliance with the notice requirements of AB 2449, the Board will maintain an AB 2449 standing agenda item for each meeting. Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Just Cause submissions received for the current Board meeting. Consider and take action on Emergency Circumstance requests, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance and Board approval. Emergency Circumstance means a physical or family medical emergency that prevents the trustee from attending in person. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. Disclosure of individuals present at remote locations, if any, pursuant to AB 2449 . A trustee attending a Board meeting remotely under AB 2449 must publicly disclose whether any other individuals 18 years of age or older are present in the room at the remote location with the trustee, and the general nature of the members relationship with any such individuals. Any trustee attending the current Board meeting remotely under 2449 will make this public disclosure.

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF CURRICULUM CHANGES AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the curriculum changes at West Valley College as presented.

Prepared By: Kristen Jackanich, Luis Portillo, Doonu Barife, Chris Dyer and Joshua Moon-Johnson Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is n o fiscal impact. Reference(s) Curriculum development and recommendation is an ongoing activity overseen by the West Valley College Academic Senate, and its subordinate curriculum committee, and the office of instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the West Valley College program review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per district policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and recommended by the curriculum committee and the articulation officer. Each of the curriculum changes and the foregoing recommendations has been further reviewed by the chief instructional officer, the college president, and the district chancellor. The office of instruction further certifies that the programs and courses described herein satisfy all applicable requirements of Title 5 and have also been submitted to the Associated Students Government of West Valley College. Degrees developed in compliance with state-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the district chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees, and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between West Valley College and Mission College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the college catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the college catalog and from the state curriculum inventory. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF BOND LIST REBASE OF THE MEASURE C AND MEASURE W PROJECT PRIORITY LIST - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the revisions and rebase of the Measure C and Measure W bond list, as presented.

Prepared By: Jessica Fassler Reviewed By: Terrance DeGray Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Funding for these projects is derived from a combination of State Capital Outlay funds, Measure C, and Measure W. This rebase accomplishes two actions, interest recognition for Measure Cand Measure W, and the allocation of overhead. The interest recognition increases the overall available budget of the bond and does have a fiscal impact. The overhead allocation is a redistribution of costs to projects for the purposes of capitalization and thus has a net zero impact. Reference(s) This item is associated with the implementation of the West Valley College and Mission College facilities master plans, the WVMCCD 5-Year Construction Plan, and Measure C and Measure W bond programs. Background/Alternatives As part of the accountability measures for the Measures C and W bond programs, the management team has put into place a formal process for managing, tracking and presenting to the Board all revisions to the Measure C and Measure W individual project names, scopes and budgets. This process adheres to the accountability standards outlined by Proposition 39 and provides transparency in the evolution of the project list. On December 10, 2024, the Board of Trustees approved the rebased Measure C and W project priority lists. Since then, programs and budgets for active projects have been updated and names for active projects have been changed by the district. This Bond List Rebase Approval Request reflects the following significant revisions: Measure C interest earnings allocation to West Valley project 415020 – Learning Resource Center Renovation in the amount of $16,318.41 Measure W interest earnings allocation to District project 435000 – Program Contingency in the amount of $4,824,323.12 Budget transfers from District project 400000 – Bond Administration in the amount of $23,951.09 and District project 435006 – Program Management in the amount of $295,209.17to active projects to accommodate the allocation of program management, legal, and related expenses paid July 1, 2024 through May 31, 2025 for capitalization purposes. This rebase will be effective as of August 20, 2025. Please refer to the attached bond list rebase approval request report for details on each project revision included on the Measure C/Measure W Rebase #29/16, dated August 19, 2025. Coordination The vice chancellor of facilities development and operations, executive director of bond construction, facilities administrative specialist, A Kennedy Group and construction managers worked with district and college administrators on preparing this report. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the rebased Measure C and Measure W project priority list will be implemented by district administration. §

Prepared By: Justin Tran Reviewed By: Melissa Johns Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The Foundation has been offered a $37,000 planning grant from the Carol S. Larson Fund, a donor advised fund at the Silicon Valley Community Foundation. The grant will fund 0.2 FTE release time for a West Valley College faculty member as well as expenses related to marketing, outreach, and planning supplies. Reference(s) Board Policy 3820 Gifts: Governs acceptance of donations exceeding $10,000, which require prior Board approval. Background/Alternatives This grant supports a planning process, scheduled from September 2025 to December 2025, to evaluate the feasibility and design of a new Early Childhood Teacher Preparation Program at West Valley College, specifically tailored for learners aged 50 and older. Led by Child Studies instructor Michelle Francis, the planning effort will include a market analysis, community outreach through focus groups and interviews, and the development of structural models. The primary goals are to assess community interest, identify effective program delivery models, and create a detailed implementation roadmap. Donor Carol S. Larson remains actively involved throughout the planning process. Coordination The grant was coordinated through Carol S. Larson's donor-advised fund at the Silicon Valley Community Foundation, in collaboration with West Valley College dean of professional studies Eric Grabiel and associate vice chancellor of advancement Melissa Johns. Follow-up/Outcome The Office of Advancement and the WVMCCD Foundation will be responsible for managing receipt of funds, disbursing grants, and overseeing all reporting requirements. Key deliverables will include a feasibility report, program model, and comprehensive implementation plan. §

FIRST READING: MISSION COLLEGE FUTURE FACILITIES PLAN - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct a first reading of the Mission College Future Facilities Plan (FFP) and consider public comment.

Prepared By: Danny Nguyen Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no direct fiscal impact from the approval of the Future Facilities Plan. The plan serves as a strategic guide for future campus development in support of the college’s Strategic Education Master Plan (SEMP) and in alignment with available fiscal and physical resources. Implementation of specific projects identified in the plan will be subject to separate Board approvals as funding becomes available. Reference(s) Board Policy 3250 requires a broad-based, comprehensive, and integrated system of planning that involves appropriate segments of the college community. Facilities planning is also required by Title 5 regulations and supports compliance with ACCJC Accreditation Standards. Background/Alternatives The Future Facilities Plan provides a long-range framework to guide capital improvements, campus development, and facilities-related investments over the next five to ten years—ultimately enhancing the student experience, learning environment, and institutional success. Developed in alignment with Mission College’s Strategic Education Master Plan (SEMP) which was board approved in June 2024, the FFP reflects extensive input from students, faculty, classified professionals, administrators, and community stakeholders. The plan includes: An assessment of current facility conditions and space utilization. A planning framework guided by key principles and priorities. Integration of campus concepts related to zones and axes, access and circulation, equity and community, and sustainability and technology. Recommendations for future studies and projects, including renovations, new construction, and infrastructure improvements. This inclusive and collaborative planning process provides a shared foundation for decision-making and long-term campus development. Coordination The Future Facilities Plan was developed by the Mission College Facilities Master Plan workgroup in collaboration with district facilities and Gensler. It has been presented to and approved by campus governance groups, the college council, and the executive cabinet. Follow-up/Outcome Implementation of the Future Facilities Plan will be a coordinated districtwide effort, guided by the college president with planning support from the executive cabinet, participatory governance bodies, and district facilities department. The plan will serve as a foundational tool for prioritizing projects, pursuing and aligning funding opportunities, and informing capital improvement efforts. Revisions to the plan are anticipated in future cycles to reflect updates to the college’s educational goals and strategic priorities. §
