It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Background/Alternatives As per District policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and approved by the curriculum Committee and the articulation officer. Degrees developed in compliance with state-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the district chancellor acting as designee for the board of trustees and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between Mission College and West Valley College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcomes Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. AGREEMENT BETWEEN MISSION COLLEGE AND EAST SIDE UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT (ESUHSD) – COLLEGE AND CAREER ACCESS PATHWAYS (CCAP) Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Agreement between East Side Union High School District (ESUHSD) and West Valley-Mission Community College District (WVMCCD) to offer dual enrollment pathway courses for the 2026–2028 academic years.

Prepared By: Julian Branch Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact: The General Fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. If approved, Mission College will provide community college coursework to eligible ESUHSD students during summer and/or academic term offerings at agreed-upon ESUHSD sites and/or Mission College. Mission College will include students enrolled under this CCAP Agreement in the District’s report of Full-Time Equivalent Students (FTES) for purposes of receiving state apportionment, consistent with Education Code section 76004. References: Education Code 76004(b) (Assembly Bill 288) establishes the College and Career Access Pathways Act, which allows California Community College districts to form partnerships with local school districts. This agreement aligns with Mission College’s Strategic Education Master Plan and Student Equity Plan goals, which focus on enhancing student pathways to degrees and transfer, strengthening collaborations with K-12 institutions, and increasing access and success for underrepresented groups. On October 4, 2019, Assembly Bill 30 and Senate Bill 5861 were signed into law, simplifying the CCAP agreement process by requiring each district to present the proposed agreement to their governing board only once, rather than twice. Background/Alternatives: Students who earn college credit while enrolled in high school are more likely to graduate, enroll in postsecondary education, attend full-time, and complete certificates and degrees. The proposed two-year CCAP Agreement (July 1, 2026 – June 30, 2028) establishes a formal collaboration between Mission College and ESUHSD to provide structured dual enrollment pathways. Initial program offerings include: MTT 010 – Introduction to Technology and Career MTT 012 – Electronic Systems Measurement and Troubleshooting Courses may be offered at Mt. Pleasant High School and/or Mission College during Summer 2026 and Summer 2027, subject to final scheduling coordination. This agreement replaces prior agreements and formalizes compliance with current Education Code requirements. Both districts will seek governing board approval prior to execution. The agreement has been reviewed by Mission College administration and ESUHSD leadership. Coordination: The director of educational partnerships will oversee implementation of the agreement in coordination with the vice president of instruction, vice president of student services, participating academic departments, and ESUHSD leadership. Both districts will coordinate student eligibility verification, release documentation (if required), data reporting, and ISA approval consistent with state regulations. Follow-up/Outcome: Upon approval by both boards, the executed agreement will be filed with the California Community Colleges Chancellor’s Office and the California Department of Education as required by Education Code section 76004. Mission College and ESUHSD will finalize course scheduling, student recruitment, and faculty assignment in preparation for Summer 2026 implementation. Annual reporting will be completed in compliance with Chancellor’s Office requirements, including enrollment, course completion, FTES generation, and demographic reporting. §
