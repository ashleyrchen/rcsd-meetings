Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. APPROVAL OF MISSION COLLEGE TO ATTEND THE 2025 ASIAN PACIFIC AMERICANS IN HIGHER EDUCATION (APAHE) NATIONAL CONFERENCE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve 15 attendees comprised of classified professionals, faculty, administrators, and students to attend the 2025 Asian Pacific Americans in Higher Education (APAHE) National Conference, April 9 - 11, 2025 in Oakland, CA.

Prepared By: Omar Murillo, Jennifer Nguyen, Ken Songco Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Funding to attend this conference, held at the Marriott Oakland City Center, will be provided by the Mission College Title III Asian American Native American Pacific Islander Serving Institution (AANAPISI) grant and the State Chancellor’s Office Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) Student Achievement Program Funding. Below are the estimated costs of attendance for this conference: Item Cost/Person Registration Fee (Early Bird) $600 Ground Transportation/Mileage Reimbursement $200 Hotel Accommodations ($275/Night) $825 Parking ($55/Night) $220 Meals $200 Estimated cost per participant $2,045 TOTAL COST - $2,045 X 15 PARTICIPANTS $30,675 Reference(s) AP6311 states: “Prior Board approval must be obtained for any travel at district expense if the total travel expense will exceed $10,000 for any one event or purpose, without regard to the number of individuals traveling.” Background/Alternatives APAHE is a national gathering of Asian American/Pacific Islander higher education educators, students, and allies to share their best practices, including other AANAPISI-designated institutions. Mission College classified professionals, faculty, administrators, and students have attended the APAHE Conference annually. APAHE’s mission is to enhance “the educational opportunities for Asian and Pacific American students; promoting and supporting the hiring, retention, and advancement of qualified Asian and Pacific American faculty, staff, and administrators; and creating a better understanding of issues in the public affecting Asian and Pacific Americans in higher education.” This vision and mission align with Mission College’s vision, mission, and institutional goals, as well as the objectives the AANAPISI grant and the college's new AANHPI SAP funding to develop strategies and programs for culturally responsive learning communities. The theme for the APAHE 2025 National Conference is Choosing Humanity: Co-Creating Joy, Justice, and Leadership. In a time increasingly divided by challenges and disparities, Choosing Humanity is the intentional act of centering people, community, and culture in practice as educators and leaders. As co-creators, participating institutions commit to affirming the knowledge, experiences, ancestry, and intersectionality of the community, ensuring that every voice is heard, and every story is valued. The conference will provide opportunities to explore how equitable practices transform educational spaces, promoting inclusivity and dismantling systemic barriers. Leadership as a shared responsibility will also be a key focus, encouraging individuals to step into their power and contribute meaningfully to the well-being of others. Coordination AANAPISI program analyst, Jennifer Nguyen, will take the lead in coordinating and recruiting Mission College classified professionals, faculty, administrators, and students to attend the 2025 APAHE conference pending Board approval. Follow-up/Outcome Upon approval of this agenda item, travel arrangements and registration will be confirmed for 15 Mission College staff, faculty, administrators, and students to attend the conference in Oakland, CA. Conference participation will inform the work of the AANAPISI/AANHPI SAP grant staff to develop more culturally-relevant programming for AAPI students at Mission College, such as enhancing our pilot AAPI learning community, an AAPI peer navigator program, and development of Asian American Studies curriculum to meet the demands of the new CA Community College and Ethnic Studies requirements. Best practices gleaned from this conference will be shared with the Mission College Asian Pacific Islander Desi American (APIDA) Affinity Group, which acts as an advisory body in developing Mission College’s culturally-relevant AAPI programming. Participation in the APAHE conference will help strengthen community amongst the college's APIDA students, faculty, staff, and administration. Finally, Mission College students who participate in the conference will participate in feedback sessions focused on the designing of new APIDA programming. §

ADOPT RESOLUTION NO. 25012102 INCREASING THE MONTHLY COMPENSATION OF STUDENT TRUSTEES - Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 25012102 to increase the monthly compensation for student trustees from $200 to $400.

Prepared By: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed By: Cade Story-Yetto Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Approval of this item will increase the monthly compensation provided to student trustees from $200 to $400, paid for out of the unrestricted general fund. Reference(s) Education Code § 72023.5, § 72024(a)(4). Per Board Policy 2725, "the district shall provide compensation to Board members for attending Board meetings at the maximum rate authorized by statute. Compensation shall be paid according to the formula set by statute. Student members are provided compensation at half the rate of that paid to Board members." Per Board Policy 2015, the district grants student trustees "the privilege to receive compensation for meeting attendance at a level of half the rate for publicly elected trustees." Background/Alternatives Education Code § 72023.5 gives local governing boards the discretion to grant specified privileges to student trustees. Under Education Code § 72024(a)(4), in community college districts with an annual FTES between 10,000 and 25,000, each trustee may receive up to $400 per month in compensation if they attend all meetings. The district’s prior-year FTES fell within this range; thus, per BP 2725, the current monthly compensation for non-student members of the Board of Trustees is $400, while student trustees receive $200. This item requests the Board invoke its statutory authority to bring student trustee compensation to $400 per month—on par with non-student members—effective February 1, 2025. This item also requests the Board to revise Board Policy 2725 and Board Policy 2015 to remove any references specifying that student trustees be compensated at half the rate of non-student trustees, ensuring alignment with the provisions of Resolution No. 25012102. Coordination The executive assistant to the board of trustees surveyed compensation practices across California community college districts to better understand current norms regarding student trustee stipends. Following this review, the chief of staff worked with legal counsel to confirm alignment with Education Code provisions and Board policy, culminating in the preparation of this agenda item and resolution. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the signed resolution will be published on the district website. BP 2725 and BP 2015 will be updated accordingly to reflect the new student trustee compensation level. The finance and human resources departments will implement this change beginning with February payroll. §
