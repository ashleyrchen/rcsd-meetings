CONSIDER APPROVAL OF CURRICULUM CHANGES AT MISSION COLLEGE

Prepared By: Linping Yu, Aram Shepherd, Guy Shani, Melissa Stewart, and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Daniel Peck Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The General Fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. These costs are offset, to varying degrees, by apportionment received for credit-based instruction and other associated fees. Reference(s) Curriculum development and approval is an ongoing activity overseen by the Academic Senate, and its subordinate Curriculum Committee, and the Office of Instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the Mission College Program Review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per District policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and approved by the Curriculum Committee and the Articulation Officer. Each of the curriculum changes and the foregoing approvals has been further reviewed by the Chief Instructional Officer, the College President, and the District Chancellor. The Office of Instruction further certifies that the programs and courses described herein satisfy all applicable requirements of Title 5 and have also been submitted to the Associated Students Government of Mission College. Degrees developed in compliance with State-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the District Chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between Mission College and West Valley College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcomes Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the College catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the College catalog and from the state Curriculum Inventory. §

First Reading - West Valley-Mission Community Colleges District Resolution 20220118 Redefining Trustee Boundary Areas from which Districting Governing Board Members will be elected. Recommendation: That the Board of Trustees conduct a first reading of Resolution No. 20220118 and consider public comment regarding adjusted trustee election areas from which district governing board members will be elected.

Prepared By: Manny Cappello Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The contract amount for demographic research and redistricting assistance is $54,000 and will be performed by Lapkoff and Gobalet Demographic Research, Inc. The final report will be the basis for filing adjusted trustee election areas with the Santa Clara County Registrar of Voters in compliance with all rules including six criteria the new districts must meet including near equal populations, compliance with the Voting Rights Act, geographically contiguous districts, communities of interest sharing social and economic interests, and geographically compact areas. Reference(s) Board Policies 2010 (Board Membership), 2100 (Board Elections), 2110 (Vacancies on the Board), 2200 (Board Duties and Responsibilities), 2210 (Officers), 2220 (Committees of the Board), 2305 (Annual Organizational Meeting), 2310 (Regular meetings of the Board), 2340 (Agendas), 2345 (Public Participation at Board Meetings), 2350 (Speakers), 2355 (Decorum), 2360 (Minutes), 2365 (Recording), 2410 (Board Policies and Administrative Procedures), 2430 (Delegation of Authority to the Chancellor), 2710 (Conflict of Interest), 2716 (Political Activity), 2720 (Communications Among Board Members), Title 5 sections 53000 et seq., and 59300 et seq.; and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964. Background/Alternatives Signed by Governor Gray Davis in 2002, the California Voting Rights Act (CVRA) generally provides that &ldquo;an at-large&rdquo; method of election may not be imposed or applied in a manner that impairs the ability of a protected class to elect candidates of its choice or its ability to influence the outcome of an election, as a result of the dilution or the abridgment of the rights of voters who are members of a protected class. California Elections Code Section 10522 stipulates that at least 125 days prior to the day fixed for the general district election, the secretary of a resident voting district shall deliver to the county elections official of each affected county a map showing the boundaries of the district within that county and a statement indicating in which divisions a director is to be elected at the next general district election. Education Code Section 5019.5(a) requires roughly equal population within trustee areas in a by-trustee area election process and to adjust the boundaries of those trustee areas consistent with the results of the most recent census. Boundaries need to be adjusted to satisfy at least one of the following conditions: a) The population of each area is, as nearly may be, the same proportion of the total population of the district as the ratio that the number of governing board members elected from the area bears to the total number of members of the governing board. For example, if a district has seven trustees and elects one trustee from each of seven trustee areas, each district should have approximately one-seventh of the total population in each trustee area; or b) The population of each area is, as nearly as may be, the same proportion of the total population of the district as each of the other areas. The general guideline is that the largest trustee area should be no more than 10% more populous than the smallest trustee area in the district. Coordination The Chancellor and other staff worked with Lapkoff & Gobalet Demographic Research, Inc, District counsel, and state and local officials on this project. The Legislative and Policy Oversight Committee placed this on their agenda providing public participation and inviting public comment at each of four meetings in which redistricting was on the agenda. In addition, the Board of Trustees have been provided two updates during publicly noticed meetings and will consider a first and second reading during upcoming board meetings. A redistricting website was created allowing easy access to updates, current information, and public comment. District Council and other participatory governance groups have been and will continue to be apprised of the status of this project. Also, there were numerous discussions and correspondence with County of Santa Clara Registrar of Voters and Santa Clara County Office of Education. These included Bob Nguyen, GIS Manager of the office of Registrar of Voters and Suzanne Carrig, Director of Policy Development & Administrative Programs at the Office of Education. These discussions pertained to confirmation of boundary maps on file with the Registrar of Voters and determination of their accuracy. Other discussions pertained to confirmation of potential adjustments of boundary lines with the San Jose-Evergreen CCD which were confirmed to never be completed and the maps never adjusted. Resolution 20220118 was last reviewed by the District Council on January 10, 2022 and Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on January 12, 2022. Follow-up/Outcome At the February 15, 2022 meeting of the Board of Trustees, adoption of the resolution and closure of the public comment period will be considered. If the resolution is adopted, it will be publicly posted on each campus, available on the WVMCCD website and Resolution 20220118 provided to the County of Santa Clara Registrar of Voters. §
