There are 3 taskforces: Explore Taskforce The Program Mapper website was shown Connect and Support Taskforce Enhance meaningful connections The hidden curriculum Learn Taskforce Integrated Student Experience, Active Learning and Careers and Experiential Learning. Guided Pathways is part of the equity work. Next steps: Launch program mapper Deepen engagement of students in career resource tools Launch 16 week engagement plan Scale classroom support for career exploration Questions? CONSIDER APPROVAL OF AWARDING REP# 01-2223 FOR WVC AND MC AUDIO-VISUAL PROJECT TO COIT COM SATELLITE SERVICES, INC. Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees approve awarding Coit Com Satellite Services, Inc as the most responsive and responsible vendor whose proposal is most advantageous to the District for WVC and MC Audio-Visual Project.

Prepared By: Joel Bennett and Don Mackessy Reviewed By: Michael Robins and Dan Borges Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The funding for the West Valley College and Mission College Audio-Visual Project will come from the State Instructional Equipment grant. Reference(s) Per Public Contract Code Section 20651, the district competitively bid contract services, under a &ldquo;Request for Proposals&rdquo; (&ldquo;RFP&rdquo;) process to meet the California Code for bid process requirements, including competitive bidding of non-construction professional services. Under this RFP process proposers were asked to submit a single proposal integrating professional services/design, equipment, and public works installation/construction. The district sought to retain one or two qualified technology firms to provide classroom audio visual systems. The Project shall provide, install, program, integrate, and test Audiovisual Systems (AVS) for the Gillmor Building at Mission College and the Science and Math Building at West Valley College. Background/Alternatives RFP 01-2223 was prepared and sent to four prospective proposers. The district received four proposals, two were found to be responsive: Proposers Base Price Add Alternate Total Avidex Industries, LLC $1,102,410.33 $286,433.96 $1,388,844.29 Coit Com Satellite Services $848,631.54 $222,056.21 $1,070,687.75 Coordination All proposals were reviewed and approved by the District&rsquo;s Executive Director of General Services and IT Director of Education Technology. The RFP evaluation committee was composed of three district staff members: IT Director, IT Architect, and IT Analyst. The committee selected the proposal by Coit Com Satellite Services as the most responsive proposal. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, two, or possibly three, contracts&mdash;one for professional services, one for equipment (or one that combines professional services and equipment), and one for public works installation/construction -- will be executed between the district and Coit Com Satellite Services for WVC AND MC Audio-Visual Project and a purchase order will be issued for a combined total not to exceed $1,070,687.75. §

PROPOSITION 30 EDUCATION PROTECTION ACCOUNT (EPA) SPENDING PLAN. Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees approve the spending plan for the Education Protection Account (EPA) for fiscal year 2022-23.

Prepared By: Ngoc Chim Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The State continues to provide Proposition 30 revenues in FY 2022-23 to support local schools and community colleges. The funding amount is $100 per FTES. The District anticipates receiving approximately $1,041,600 from the State and intends to use the money for instructional salaries and benefits. A separate sub-fund has been established in the general ledger to track the revenue and expenditures for the EPA funding. After the close of the fiscal year, the District will publish the actual revenue received and the actual expenditures on the District&rsquo;s website. Reference(s) On November 6, 2012, voters approved Proposition 30, The Schools and Local Public Safety Protection Act. This proposition temporarily raises the sales and use tax by 0.25 cents for four years and raises the income tax rate for high income earners ($250,000 for individuals and $500,000 for couples) for seven years to provide continuing funding for local school districts and community colleges. The sales and use tax expired on January 1, 2017. The income tax was expected to expire on December 31, 2018. The revenues generated from Proposition 30 were deposited into a newly created state account called the Educational Protection Account (EPA). On November 8, 2016, voters approved Proposition 55, The California Children&rsquo;s Education and Health Care Protection Act of 2016, which is an extension of Proposition 30 extending the personal income tax increase through 2030-31. The accounting and reporting requirements for Proposition 55 are exactly the same as the requirements for Proposition 30. The language of Proposition 30 requires that each local educational agency&rdquo;&hellip;shall have sole authority to determine how the moneys received from the EPA are spent&hellip;provided, however, that the appropriate governing board or body shall make these spending determinations in open session of a public meeting of the governing board or body and shall not use any of the funds from the EPA for salaries or benefits of administrators or any other administrative costs.&rdquo; Each local educational agency will be required to annually publish on its website an accounting of how much money was received from the EPA and how that money was spent. Revenues from EPA funds are unrestricted and should be accounted for separately. Background/Alternatives The District is required to comply with the language in Proposition 30. The District&rsquo;s auditor&rsquo;s will be reviewing for compliance at year end. Coordination District Staff will work with college staff to record the revenue and expense to ensure the District is in compliance with Proposition 30 and the State Budget and Accounting Manual (BAM), which has the authority of regulation in accordance with Title 5 Section 59011 of the California Code of Regulations. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the spending plan will be implemented. §
