CONSIDER APPROVAL OF MISSION COLLEGE STUDENT TRAVEL FOR TROPICAL ECOLOGY CLASS TO COSTA RICA. Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees approve the students’ travel costs related to the Tropical Ecology Field Studies Class, BIO30L/BIO31L.

Prepared By: Jean Replicon and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Mission College will once again offer a Study Abroad Program for its Tropical Ecology Field Studies class, BIO30L/BIO31L. Students bear the responsibility of financing the trip. The cost of the program will be $2617.00 per student which will cover airfare, accommodations, meals, and all program costs. As in past years, student scholarships will be provided dependent on available funding. Student scholarships will be awarded based on student need and merit. The Tropical Science Center Program fee is $1500 and covers the following 10 items: Ground transportation throughout the program Lodging at La Casona de Monteverde Lodge Lodging at San Miguel Biological Station Skywalk Hanging Bridges Tour Guided night hike at Monteverde reserve Day hike to Continental Divide at Monteverde Reserve Butterfly Garden Visit Research equipment All educational activities at Monteverde and Cabo Blanco All meals while in Costa Rica The airfare is $1117 per person. Reference(s) Board Policy 7400 indicates that colleges are required to seek Board Approval for any travel at District expense if the total travel expense exceeds $10,000 for any one purpose. Background/Alternatives Tropical Ecology credit classes in Costa Rica have been offered by Mission College since 1999. Tropical Ecology Field Studies is a one-unit class, transferrable to UC and CSU. The current offering is planned for March 25-April 1, 2023 and is expected to include between 12-20 participants. Students visit wildlife reserves, a biological field station and laboratory where they carry out surveys and field research. This is a unique program among the community colleges as it allows students of any discipline and experience level to participate in field work in the tropics. As an immersive experience, it in inherently transformational and gives students first-hand knowledge of how Costa Rica leads the way in conservation efforts. Biological Sciences Instructor, Dr. Jean Replicon, will be the faculty lead for the trip. Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 22081601 supporting Assembly Bill 1456 making Cal Grant reforms that simplify qualification criteria targeting awards for the most vulnerable students statewide.

Prepared by: Manny Cappello Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Background/Alternatives Lawmakers and community college administrators recognize Cal Grant programs are too complex and students with financial need are often excluded.
