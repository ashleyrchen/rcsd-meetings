Assembly Bill 2449 Remote Teleconference Attendance Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may be eligible to attend a Board of Education meeting remotely with Just Cause or with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance. To ensure compliance with the notice requirements of AB 2449, the Board will maintain an AB 2449 standing agenda item for each meeting. Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Just Cause submissions received for the current Board meeting. Consider and take action on Emergency Circumstance requests, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance and Board approval. Emergency Circumstance means a physical or family medical emergency that prevents the trustee from attending in person. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. Disclosure of individuals present at remote locations, if any, pursuant to AB 2449 . A trustee attending a Board meeting remotely under AB 2449 must publicly disclose whether any other individuals 18 years of age or older are present in the room at the remote location with the trustee, and the general nature of the members relationship with any such individuals. CONSIDER APPROVAL OF STUDENT TRAVEL EXCEEDING $10,000 IN COST - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the students’ travel costs related to the Tropical Ecology Field Studies Class, BIO30L/BIO31L.

Prepared By: Jean Replicon , Clement Lam, and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Mission College will once again offer a Study Abroad Program for its Tropical Ecology Field Studies class, BIO30L/BIO31L. The cost of the program will be approx. $2330.00 per student which will cover airfare, accommodations, meals, and all program costs. Students may bear the responsibility of financing the trip. Through the program review process, Mission College approved $12,000 to help support operational expenses and reduce the student course fee for Tropical Ecology Field Studies class. This will help cover in-country transportation and field activities costs. The President’s office has allocated $15,000 of the Land Corp funds it received for international travel to the Tropical Ecology Field Studies program and this will be used to sponsor 6 student participants. The Chancellor’s office has generously identified funds to sponsor 4 student participants. All scholarships will be awarded based on both need and merit. As in past years, student scholarships will be provided dependent on available funding. Reference(s) Board Policy 7400 indicates that colleges are required to seek Board Approval for any travel at District expense if the total travel expense exceeds $10,000 for any one purpose. Background/Alternatives Tropical Ecology credit classes in Costa Rica have been offered by Mission College since 1999. Tropical Ecology Field Studies is a one-unit class, transferrable to UC and CSU. The current offering is planned for March 30-April 7, 2024, and is expected to include between 12-20 participants. Students visit wildlife reserves, a biological field station and laboratory where they carry out surveys and field research. This is a unique program among the community colleges as it allows students of any discipline and experience level to participate in field work in the tropics. As an immersive experience, it in inherently transformational and gives students first-hand knowledge of how Costa Rica leads the way in conservation efforts. Biological Sciences Instructor, Dr. Jean Replicon, will be the faculty lead for the trip. Dr. Replicon has been taking Mission College Tropical Ecology students to Costa Rica since the inception of this program, which has included over 225 students, faculty and staff as participants. Coordination Details of the program are coordinated between the Executive Director of General Services, the Study Abroad Program faculty, the area Deans, the Vice President of Instruction, the Vice President of Administrative Services, and the President of Mission College. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, students will be able to travel and participate in the Study Abroad Program. As in prior years, students will showcase their experience at various forums across campus . In addition, students will be invited to present to the board on their experiences upon completion of the program. §

PARTNERSHIP WITH SUCCESSFUL AGING SOLUTIONS &amp; COMMUNITY CONSULTING (SASCC) - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees authorize the District to formalize a $250,000 partnership with Successful Aging Solutions &amp; Community Consulting (SASCC). This partnership aims to expand educational services for older adults and increase senior enrollment within both colleges.

Prepared By: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed By: Michael Robins Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The District has allocated $250,000 from its unrestricted general fund in the FY'2023-24 budget to facilitate this strategic partnership with SASCC, targeted at increasing older adult enrollment in credit and non-credit courses at both colleges. Through this partnership, the District expects to increase enrollment by a minimum of 200 Full-Time Equivalent Students (FTES) over 12 months. On a twice-annual basis, the District will reassess this program, its fiscal impact, and its return on investment to determine whether to continue or discontinue this partnership. Reference(s) In recent years, the District has recognized the immense potential of tailoring services to the senior community to enrich our campuses and the broader community. In 2018, WVMCCD worked with SASCC to become the first community college district to earn an "age-friendly" designation by the World Health Organization for showing a commitment to incorporating people of all ages into the campus communities. In early 2023, the District and SASCC conducted surveys of the senior population in Santa Clara County, revealing a significant need for enhanced senior services from community colleges. Beyond identifying service needs, the surveys provided insights into curriculum changes and strategies to foster a more welcoming environment for older adults on both campuses. Additionally, the data underscored the potential of expanding educational access for seniors as a strategic approach to counter the declining enrollment trends observed in all Bay Area community colleges. The survey's results showed the District that the decision to partner with SASCC and expand its programs aligns with the District's commitment to cultivating an inclusive educational landscape, aiming to diversify and boost enrollment to best serve our community's needs. Background/Alternatives In collaboration with the District, SASCC will develop and implement several consulting services to advance District goals. These include: Strategic enrollment drives and registration programs targeting the over 3,000 seniors in SASCC's membership program, aiming for a minimum increase of 200 FTES within the senior population registering for courses at either of the District’s Colleges. Dramatically enhanced marketing toward the senior population by leveraging The Outlook Community Newspaper, with monthly spreads featuring the Colleges, alongside programs on KCAT Public Access television and social media. Collaboration with the District to explore the feasibility of offering certificate programs supporting the aging services sector. Certificates may include Eldercare, Home-based Caregiving, Community Caregiving, and others. SASCC will also offer several direct services to enhance community involvement and student accessibility. These include: The RYDE program, providing complimentary transportation for seniors to and from the campuses of both West Valley College and Mission College. The Outlook Community Newspaper, sponsored by the District, to be mailed monthly to homes within each city in the WVMCCD service area, with designated space for District announcements. Access to the Saratoga Senior Center, allowing volunteer, paid internship, and employment opportunities for WVMCCD students. SASCC will also utilize the center to introduce a Senior Dual Enrollment program for its members and offer an Onsite Student Success Coach Program to assist older adults with online academic activities. Access to the Saratoga Adult Care Center, a licensed adult day care facility, allowing volunteer, paid internship, and employment opportunities for WVMCCD students. This partnership is considered the first step toward creating the Center for Aging at the District, which will serve as a hub for each of the Santa Clara County Senior Centers and their population of 14,000 seniors, meeting our District's educational, civic, and recreational goals. The Center for Aging will serve seniors in a manner akin to the College of Adaptive Arts' service to our community's disabled members. Coordination This item saw close coordination between district and college leadership. The WVMCCD's Executive Director of General Services and the Chancellor thoroughly reviewed and endorsed the agreement. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the District will formalize its partnership agreement with SASCC. Immediate next steps include: establishing a timeline with key milestones alongside SASCC staff; commencing targeted marketing and outreach initiatives in November 2023; forming a task force for program development in aging services; integrating the RYDE transportation program into existing campus safety and transportation plans; and initiating the planning for the Center for Aging. §
