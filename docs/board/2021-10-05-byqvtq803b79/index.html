CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE AGREEMENT BETWEEN MISSION COLLEGE AND THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA, CALIFORNIA WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT BOARD

Prepared By: Jeff Pallin and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Daniel Peck Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Mission College will receive $ 124,999.68 for the duration of Phase I of the project which is January 2022 - September 2022. The funding originates from the State of California Workforce Development Board. Budget Line Item Amount Instructional $30,000.00 Administrator $23,000.00 Employee Benefits (Instructional + Administrator) $15,900.00 Supplies & Materials $21,292.00 Operating Expenses $30,000.00 Indirect Cost Rate $4,808.00 Total $125,000.00 From January 2022 to September 2022, quarterly reports will be given to the California Workforce Development Board showing actual expenditures against the budget. In addition, the college would be able to access funding available through Mission College&rsquo;s Regional Allocation of Strong Workforce Funds, if needed. Reference(s) As part of its strategic goals, Mission College is charged with aligning courses and programs with labor market data and industry demands and opportunities. The Workforce and Economic Development Program gives Mission College the freedom to be innovative and explore new ways to address this issue by updating existing Career Education programs to ensure students are competitive and successful in their field of study and generating new programs that target specific labor shortages in our region. It is the work of the College to increase student retention and graduation rates while offering developed industry-informed pathways that prepare students for high paying jobs. It is also an opportunity to create supportive programs and outreach campaigns to serve more disproportionately impacted students and to provide students with cutting edge technology to enhance student learning outcomes. More specifically, our proposed project includes: 1) Public Transit Leadership Certificate to serve as a career advancement pathway for public transit workers and be developed as a regional asset: The Career Technical Education Certificate of Achievement in Public Transportation Leadership. 2) Public Transit Education Hub to serve as a One-Stop Shop for industry-specific expertise. The Hub will function as a service resource and be built out using the HRTP standards. This will enable Mission College to function as a lead training facility to help other colleges in the region, the state, and the nation to build their own Transportation Apprenticeship (and Pre-Apprenticeship) programs. 3) New Academic / Industry Department to ensure the sustainability of the existing apprenticeship programs, including the newly proposed certificate program. MC&rsquo;s ability to move to Phase II ($500,000) is evidenced by our capability to sustain and grow both the current HRTP partnership and its outcomes (VTA Apprenticeship Program), an integrated system of DAS certified public transit apprenticeships. Additionally, we will demonstrate that we are part of a sustainable HRTP by: Documenting formalized long-term commitments from our partners Creating a plan to sustain the partnership over time Making concrete investments made to sustain the partnership; and Taking specific steps to grow jobs, including the workforce training These will be supported through formalized letters of commitment, memoranda of agreement, sustainability plans, strategic plans, and our past successes and plans for the newly proposed project. Background/Alternatives The High Road Training Partnerships (HRTP) initiative started as a $10M demonstration project designed to model partnership strategies for the state. Ranging from transportation to health care to hospitality, the HRTP model embodies the sector approach championed by the Board &mdash; industry partnerships that deliver equity, sustainability, and job quality. Along with these program investments, the Board is producing a body of policy and principle to guide related undertakings across the workforce system. Indeed, the initiative was designed as a campaign &mdash; to advance a field of practice that simultaneously addresses urgent questions of income inequality, economic competitiveness, and climate change through regional skills strategies designed to support economically and environmentally resilient communities across the state. The industry-based, worker-focused training partnerships build skills for California&rsquo;s &ldquo;high road&rdquo; employers &mdash; firms that compete based on quality of product and service achieved through innovation and investment in human capital, and can thus generate family-supporting jobs where workers have agency and voice. The Strong Workforce Program allocates $248,000,000 per year for the purpose of expanding the availability of quality community college career technical education and workforce development courses, programs, pathways, credentials, certificates, and degrees. Coordination Jeffrey Pallin, Dean of Business, Technology, and Kinesiology will be working with Rob Gamble, Director of Workforce and Economic Development to coordinate faculty, administrators, supplies, materials, and operations. They will also include an evaluation plan to gauge the success of the partnership and its impact on students and the region, and be comprised of both formative and summative components, quantitative and qualitative methods, student-worker and industry outcomes, and leading and lagging indicators. Follow-up/Outcome Upon approval, the signed agreement will go into effect January 2022-September 2022. §
