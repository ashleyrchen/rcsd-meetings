CONSIDER APPROVAL OF CURRICULUM CHANGES AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees approve the curriculum changes at West Valley College as presented.

P repared By: Kristen Jackanich, Stacy Gleixner, Chris Dyer, Luis Portillo and Doonu Barife Reviewed By: Stephanie Kashima Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The general fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. These costs are offset, to varying degrees, by apportionment received for credit-based instruction and other associated fees. Reference(s) Curriculum development and recommendation is an ongoing activity overseen by the West Valley College Academic Senate, and its subordinate curriculum committee, and the office of instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the West Valley College program review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per district policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and recommended by the curriculum committee and the articulation officer. Each of the curriculum changes and the foregoing recommendations has been further reviewed by the chief instructional officer, the college president, and the district chancellor. The office of instruction further certifies that the programs and courses described herein satisfy all applicable requirements of Title 5 and have also been submitted to the Associated Students Government of West Valley College. Degrees developed in compliance with state-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the district chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees, and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between West Valley College and Mission College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the college catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the college catalog and from the state curriculum inventory. §

STUDENT CHILDCARE SUPPORT - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the District’s plan to reduce and eliminate student childcare fees and utilize the unrestricted general fund to supplement the operation of the Child Development Center (CDC) starting in Spring 2023 through Spring 2024

Prepared By: Anne Horgan and Michael Robins Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact On September 6, 2022, the Board of Trustees approved the Final Budget which allocates the budget from the Unrestricted General Fund to support student fees. The estimated amount set aside to support the Child Development Center is $617,136 and $1,360,708, respectively, for FY 22/23 and 23/24. The funding will allow the District to maintain childcare services at the current level. Reference(s) Title V sets the requirements for the teacher-to-child ratio for children in different age groups. It also sets eligibility for students earning less than $95,000 annually. Background/Alternatives The District is currently exploring options to support student equity and success. This initiative will assist with removing financial barriers for students and reduce the cost of education. In the past, both colleges operated the CDC under two separate Directors. The District has initiated the consolidation process to move the operation under the District Executive Director of General Services. The District is currently reviewing the operations at both sites and is developing a plan which includes the timeline, services provided, and eligibility criteria. The plan will include a phased-in approach to allow for time to plan and consider all aspects of the operation in order to enable the District to provide free childcare to students. The District intends to initiate the first phase of providing free childcare to students in Spring 2023 and the second phase in Fall 2023. The District currently charges between $1,550 to $2,650 each month per child. These rates have been in place since 2020. Children enrolled in the program range from one month to six years old. The State currently provides subsidies for students making less than $95,000. Under the proposed plan, the District will cover the cost for students who do not qualify for state subsidy and have a family income of less than $150,000. Coordination This item was coordinated with the Executive Director of General Services, the Program Director of CDC, college staff including the College Presidents, and the AVC of Finance and Administration. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the District will implement phase one of the plan in Spring 2023 to provide free childcare to students who do not qualify for State subsidy and are enrolled in at least three units/hours (for non-credit) with an education plan. Staff will continue to work on the phase two implementation plan for Fall 2023. Communications will be sent to current and new students to notify them of the free services. The Finance Department will coordinate the transfer of funds necessary to support the CDC operation. §

ADOPT RESOLUTION NO. 22111501 AFFIRMING SUPPORT OF FREE COMMUNITY COLLEGE AT THE WEST VALLEY-MISSION COMMUNITY COLLEGE DISTRICT - Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution NO. 22111501 authorizing the West Valley-Mission Community College District to waive tuition and enrollment fees and use existing local funds to reduce costs for students furthering their education.

Prepared by: Manny Cappello Reviewed by: Bradley Davis Approved by: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact California Education code 76300 requires each Community College District to collect tuition and enrollment fees for students enrolled in courses. Recently, the San Mateo Community College District worked with legislative partners to enact SB893 which amends the education code to waive tuition and enrollment fees for students residing in the district. The District endeavors to enact legislation that would permit the same. Upon the change in law, the District would be permitted to waive (or charge reduced) tuition and enrollment fees and use its local funds to provide students with free community college courses on each of our two campuses. The West Valley-Mission Community College Board of Trustees is required to adopt appropriate and necessary resolutions relating to qualifications for students who would be eligible for reduced fees. This change to the California Education code would also reconcile any concerns relating to potential gifts of public funds. This program would not increase any state funding for the District or commit the state to allocate any additional resources. Importantly, the resolution grants the District flexibility to determine all eligibility requirements. Reference(s) California Education code 76300, Board Policies 2200 (Board Duties and Responsibilities), 2210 (Officers), 2220 (Committees of the Board), 2305 (Annual Organizational Meeting), 2310 (Regular meetings of the Board), 2340 (Agendas), 2345 (Public Participation at Board Meetings), 2360 (Minutes), 2365 (Recording), 2410 (Board Policies and Administrative Procedures), 2430 (Delegation of Authority to the Chancellor), 2710 (Conflict of Interest), 2716 (Political Activity), 2720 (Communications Among Board Members), Title 5 sections 53000 et seq., and 59300 et seq.; and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and Senate Bill 893. Background/Alternatives This resolution would authorize the Chancellor to work closely with state legislators on authoring, introducing and championing legislation that uses District unrestricted general funds to provide fee waivers to residents of the District. There are several additional transparency requirements and safeguards in the resolution, including: &bull; The West Valley-Mission Community College District Board of Trustees must establish a framework and adopt policies to determine eligibility and students must demonstrate financial need (as determined by the District). &bull; Students must reside in the West Valley-Mission Community College District boundary, thus preventing any real or perceived competition with neighboring community college districts. &bull; West Valley-Mission Community College District must publicly provide financial impact information at a public Board meeting showing that the District has the funds to reduce fees for students and the District is prohibited from receiving reimbursement from the state. The resolution incorporates the U.S. Department of Education&rsquo;s definition of a student&rsquo;s Total Cost of Attendance (COA), which includes: COA is the estimate of tuition and fees, cost of room and board (or living expenses), cost of books, supplies, transportation, loan fees, and miscellaneous expenses (including a reasonable amount for the documented cost of a personal computer), allowance for childcare or other dependent care, costs related to a disability, and reasonable costs for eligible study-abroad programs. Existing law authorizes a community college that receives funding under the program to, among other things, waive some or all fees for up to 2 academic years for first-time students who are enrolled in 12 or more semester units or the equivalent at the college and complete and submit either a Free Application for Federal Student Aid or a California Dream Act application. This resolution would authorize the District to use local unrestricted general funds, in addition to funding received under the California College Promise, to help students with total cost of attendance. The bill would define total cost of attendance for a student attending West Valley-Mission Community College as including the student&rsquo;s tuition and fees, books, and supplies, living expenses, transportation expenses, and any other student expenses used to calculate a student&rsquo;s financial need for purposes of federal Title IV student aid programs. Coordination This bill would require the District to use local unrestricted general funds to provide waivers of tuition and fees. This bill would require the governing board of the West Valley-Mission Community College District to submit a report to the Chancellor&rsquo;s Office of the California Community Colleges, the Department of Finance, and the appropriate committees of the Legislature on the implementation of these provisions, as specified. Follow-up/Outcome If adopted, the resolution will be publicly posted on each campus, available on the West Valley-Mission Community College District website. In addition, the approved Resolution No. 22111501 provided to the Chancellor&rsquo;s Office of the California Community Colleges, the Department of Finance, and the appropriate members of the Legislature . §
