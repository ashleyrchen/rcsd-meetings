MISSION COLLEGE-SANTA CLARA VALLEY TRANSPORTATION AUTHORITY (VTA) AGREEMENT RENEWAL - Recommendation: That the Board of Trustees approve the agreement with Santa Clara Valley Transportation Authority (VTA) to continue the Mission College Smart Pass 2019-2020 program for Academic year 2020-2021.

Prepared By: Liz Pelayo and Connor Keese Reviewed By: Omar Murillo Approved By: Daniel Peck Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The direct costs of the VTA Smart Pass (formerly known as Eco Pass) program at Mission College are funded through mandatory transportation fees as approved by the Board of Trustees on January 19, 2016. The Smart Pass transportation fee of up to $11.00 per semester for full-time students was first implemented in Fall 2016. A prorated discount of approximately $31,450 will be applied to Mission College&rsquo;s Fall 2020 invoice due to VTA effectively suspending the collection of fares April 1, 2020 &ndash; July 31, 2020, while safety measures to protect passengers and drivers were put into place. Reference(s) California Education Code, Section 76361 and Mission College&rsquo;s Election Ballot Language. Background/Alternatives Following the favorable vote in Fall 2015 of a majority of the Mission College student body to establish the program and assess fees to Mission College students, the Board of Trustees approved the mandatory Eco Pass Transportation Fee on January 19, 2016. On April 09, 2016, the Board took action to approve the first year agreement with VTA. In Fall 2016, Mission College implemented the Eco Pass (now known as Smart Pass) Program with the Santa Clara Valley Transportation Authority (VTA). This agreement continues the program for the fifth year. This is an important program for Mission College students, as it drastically reduces the cost of student transportation within the greater Bay Area and allows our students to ride VTA buses and light rail at a fraction of the traditional consumer cost. Mission College students currently pay a maximum of $12.00 for seven months in spring/summer and five months in the fall semester for unlimited access to both the VTA bus and light rail lines. Mission College part-time students currently pay up to $10.50 for seven months in spring and five months in the fall semester for unlimited access to both the VTA bus and light rail lines. Mission College summer students pay $5.00 for unlimited access to both the VTA bus and light rail lines. Payment is then made to VTA via purchase order based on fall and spring semester headcounts. The Smart Pass (formerly known as Eco Pass) program is an ongoing transportation agreement with VTA, and there are no alternatives to this program that could provide similar services. Coordination Coordination for the VTA contract renewal involved Mission College&rsquo;s Office of Student Activities, Vice-President of Student Services, Vice-President of Administrative Services, and the District&rsquo;s General Services Executive Director. Follow-up/Outcome On approval of the agreement renewal, the Office of Student Activities will issue a purchase order for VTA Smart Pass based on the fall and spring semester headcount. §

MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING BETWEEN WEST VALLEY COLLEGE AND THE FEDERAL AVIATION ADMINISTRATION UNMANNED AIRCRAFT SYSTEMS COLLEGE TRAINING INITIATIVE - Recommendation: That the board approve the three-year Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between West Valley College and the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS) College Training Initiative.

Prepared By: Chris Cruz and Renee Paquier Reviewed By: Stephanie Kashima Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There are no direct fiscal impacts to West Valley College regarding this MOU with the Federal Aviation Administration UAS College Training Initiative. The West Valley College UAS Technology program will be working under current funding parameters. Reference(s) This MOU relates directly to the established Unmanned Aircraft Systems Technology Program which was previously approved by the Board of Trustees. Also see BP 3910: Use of Unmanned Aircraft Systems; Federal Aviation Administration Modernization & Reform Act of 2012; Federal Aviation Administration Small UAS Rule, 14 CFR &sect;107 (Part 107); California Civil Code &sect;1708.8; and Special Authority for Certain Unmanned Systems (49 USC &sect;44807). Background/Alternatives This year, the FAA has established a UAS (Unmanned Aircraft Systems) Collegiate Training Initiative. The FAA&#39;s 2018 Reauthorization Act required the FAA to establish a collegiate training initiative program relating to UAS. It also called for a process to designate an association of public, two-year institutions of higher education as Community and Technical College Centers of Excellence in small UAS technology training. The Unmanned Aircraft Systems Collegiate Training Initiative (UAS-CTI) program addresses the Section 631 requirement of the FAA 2018 Reauthorization Act. Any public two-year institution of higher education that participates in the UAS-CTI program will also be designated as members of the Consortium for Small Unmanned Aircraft Systems Technology Training. The FAA will invite members of this consortium to participate in annual meetings and other events with the agency, and will facilitate the sharing of best practices through this consortium. The Unmanned Aircraft Systems Collegiate Training Initiative (UAS-CTI) is a new program designed for universities, colleges, and technical schools by the FAA to recognize institutions that prepare students for careers in unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) or drones. Post-secondary institutions with UAS curricula that want to be recognized as UAS-CTI participants will now have the opportunity to apply for this distinction. The result of this collaborative working relationship will include a continuous dialogue with stakeholders to connect universities and colleges with general industry, local governments, law enforcement, and regional economic development entities to address labor force needs. The West Valley College UAS Technology program completed a detailed application to the FAA UAS College Training Initiative, which was approved by the FAA. In that application, the college had to demonstrate that it met the basic guidelines for instruction and training. Institutions of Higher Learning will meet criteria for unmanned aviation training programs in accordance with Section 631 of the FAA Reauthorization Act of 2018. These UAS-Specific training objectives include: Training on UAS platforms including multirotor and fixed-wing small unmanned aircraft. Knowledge of the operation of UAS components and the capabilities and limitations of the aircraft. Maintenance and inspections program training, insurance and awareness of privacy and security systems. Simulation and/or hands-on flight training in using UAS, including practical flight assessments. Training in functional areas including: a. agriculture, b. environmental services, c. infrastructure and utility inspections, d. petroleum, oil and gas inspections, and e. public safety. Remote pilot certification requirements and training on applicable federal policies and regulations. Training related to flying with sensors and processing data. The FAA expects all UAS-CTI schools to encourage diverse and inclusive participation in the program. The FAA promotes diversity and inclusion by: &bull; Diversity: recruiting from a diverse, qualified group of potential applicants to secure a high performing workforce drawn from all segments of American society. &bull; Inclusion: fostering a culture that encourages collaboration, flexibility, and fairness to enable individuals to contribute to their full potential and supporting further retention in the program. On July 21, 2020, the college received notification that our UAS Technology Program at West Valley College was approved by the Federal Aviation Administration to be part of the FAA-UAS CTI program. Coordination The local coordination of this program will be done through the Park Management, Geospatial, and UAS Technology department chair Chris Cruz and faculty Benjamin Mendelsohn. Both are current FAA Part 107 certified pilots. They maintain their certification per Board Policy/Administrative Procedure 3910. This MOU will be in effect for three years from the date of signature. Follow-up/Outcome On behalf of the District, Chancellor Bradley Davis will sign the MOU with the FAA and the District&rsquo;s participation will be official on September 1, 2020. Department chair, Christopher Cruz, will coordinate the District&rsquo;s participation and will provide progress reports as requested. This signed document will then be sent to FAA administrator Diana Robinson for Joe Morra, Division Director, Safety & Integration, Unmanned Aircraft Systems Integration Office. §

FOR THE ELECTRIC VEHICLE CHARGING PROJECT AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommendation: That the Board of Trustees approve the procurement of electric transformer and switching equipment for West Valley College in the amount of $136,483 in order to facilitate the interface of the new electric vehicle charging stations with West Valley College’s existing infrastructure.

Prepared By: Monet Payton and Mina Hernandez Reviewed By: Javier Castruita Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The funding for this project comes from a combination of the Bay Area Air Quality Management District Grant and Measure W, Alternative Energy Projects, line item DS-03 of the District Services Project Priority List. Reference(s) California Public Contact Code Section 20652 allows the governing board of any community college district, without advertising for bids, to acquire equipment, materials, and supplies by utilizing an existing contract from another public corporation or agency. OMNIA Partners, formerly U.S. Communities, assists Participating Public Agencies to reduce the cost of purchased goods through strategic sourcing that combines the volumes and the purchasing power of public agencies nationwide. This is accomplished through an award of competitively solicited contracts for high quality products and services by large and well recognized public agencies (herein "Lead Public Agencies"). The contracts provide for use by not only the respective Lead Public Agency, but also by other Participating Public Agencies. On December 20, 2017, The City of Kansas City, MO (herein "Lead Public Agency") on behalf of itself and all states, local governments, school districts, and higher education institutions in the United States of America, and other government agencies and nonprofit organizations (herein "Participating Public Agencies") entered into a Master Agreement for a complete line of Electrical, Lighting, Data Communications, and Security Products and Related Products, Services and Solutions (herein "Products and Services") with Graybar Electrical Company, Inc. for the provision of said equipment to participating agencies. This partnership seeks to combine the volumes of Participating Public Agencies to achieve cost effective pricing. Utilizing this Agreement will benefit the District in achieving added price savings through volume discounting. Therefore, this purchase will be procured pursuant to OMNIA Partners Contract EV2370, currently active through January 31, 2023 . Background/Alternatives This procurement is for electric transformer and switching equipment which will connect the new electric vehicle charging station system at West Valley College parking lots to the existing electrical infrastructure. Quotes were obtained from Graybar Electrical Company, PowerFlex EDF Renewables and Siemens USA. The proposals were reviewed by the project management team and Graybar Electrical Company was the lowest responsive quote that matched the specifications required. This is for equipment only, being purchased in advance in order to expedite procurement for long lead time electrical equipment. Installation is part of Bid CA07-2021 WVC EV Charging Stations. Coordination The District’s bond buyer, executive director of general services, manager of parking & traffic operations, director of facilities construction for West Valley College, and the associate vice chancellor of facilities development and operations have reviewed and approve of this purchase. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, a purchase order will be issued to Graybar Electrical Company, Inc. §
