Special Board of Trustees Meeting | Housing Study Session Meeting Minutes (Saturday, December 3, 2022) - approved Generated by Rebecca Alvarez on Friday, December 16, 2022 Members present 1. Meeting Opening 1.1 Roll Call - called to order at 10:06 AM Present: Susan Fish, Randi Kinman, Robert Owens, Adrienne Grey, Anne Kepner Absent: Karl Watanabe, Jack Lucas Non voting members: Bradley Davis 1.2 Pledge of Allegiance The pledge of allegiance was recited. 1.3 Approval of the Order of the Agenda Motion to approve the order of the agenda by Robert Owens, second by Susan Fish Aye: Adrienne Grey, Robert Owens, Randi Kinman, Anne Kepner, and Susan Fish Final Resolution: Motion Carries Regular Order of the Agenda 2.1 Presentation introduction - Brad Davis, WVMCCD Chancellor Chancellor Davis stated that Mr. Risher with Scion is with us today to present and review financing options related to the potential housing project and answer questions that the Board may have. This has been a deliberate process that began with a needs assessment, it was determined that there is a need so we have moved on to phase 2, the financial plausibility and what it would mean for the district in terms of expenditures. Next would be possible partnership with the state and collaboration with the entire district, should this move forward. We are still in the analysis phase, Chancellor Davis stated that he encourages the colleges through participatory governance structures to facilitate discussions and stated that he is available during District Council to answer questions. Ted Risher with Scion was introduced. 2.2 West Valley-Mission CCD Housing Study Report / Fiscal Analysis - Presented by: Ted Risher, The Scion Group Ted Risher stated that he encourages questions to learn more about this process. The following was highlighted during his presentation: Strategic Objectives - what is most and least important Demand Findings - there is plenty of demand Programs modeled - 352 beds, 300 bed and 500 beds. Single students, students with families, employees with families SRJC Program Summary - pros and cons #1 Program Summary - Preferred program with 300 units/students best balance of cost and convenience for students Rent ceiling $1474 #2 Program Summary - Less preferred program as it is more per student than option #1. P3 funding structure was explained $737 2 BR | $1082 4 BR SB 169 funding is an amazing resource #3 Program Summary - Significantly more expensive rents for students and likely too expensive to receive SB 169 funding. #1032 Studio | $1,179 1 BR | $11,327 2 BR #4 Program Summary - Employee Housing Very expensive and not eligible for SB 169 Notional Program for WVMCCD - Program 1 200 double beds and 100 single beds with full kitchens 28,820 Square Feet Fits demand profile in market and demand analysis Financial Analyses Approach 100$ P3 50% P3 / 50% SB 169 100% SB 169 One campus site will be selected Sources and Uses - described in detail 100$ P3 50% P3 / 50% SB 169 100% SB 169 Rents Based on 12 month leases The average rent of $786 per month represents 53% of the maximum annual rent of $1474 for Santa Clara County. Total cost of Ownership FY 2028 Cash Flow Element The value of the land was discussed. Site location was discussed Discussion ensued regarding the operations of people living on campus. Discussion ensued regarding the location of the housing project. Program Rental schedule Budget Cash Flow Discussion ensured regarding weather or not the project is worth moving forward with. Support was shown for the project to move forward. Chancellor Davis stated that the next step is for the board to decide weather or not to proceed. Perhaps LPOC will present a resolution once the board comes to a decision. It was stated by Trustee Grey that housing is at the top of the barriers for students attending college. Discussion took place amongst the Trustees. The timeline was discussed of what needs to happen prior to July 2023 submission. A decision to move forward needs to be made by March 15, 2023 to submit the application for the SB 169 application submission. It was recommended to have all information to the board by the end of February. January and February are key months for this project. Next steps: Site selection - decided by end of February Site walk with Trustees Chancellor Davis suggested: Staff present timeline and resolution that encapsulates next steps for this project to LPOC - then to the full Board in January. This would be utilized as a roadmap. Divvy up responsibilities amongst leadership, ensure boxes are being checked off. In summary, a document should be given to the Board for consideration of what will take place between now and March 15. Ted Risher closed stating that the project is feasible and that there is a demand for housing. $25 million is set aside in the capital outline fund. 2.3 Consideration and possible action regarding the West Valley-Mission CCD Housing Project Motion to recommend the Housing Project forward and proceed as discussed based on information and timeline presented today, contingent upon the more detailed timeline and information that will come from the Chancellor, motion by Adrienne Grey, second by Randi Kinman. Trustee Owens is concerned for members of the public and their input on this project. He requested that there be an immediate effort to contact the cities and make them aware (if this action is taken) is contemplation making application to the State of California to construct a student residential facility (student housing) and has yet to decided on location and would appreciate comment from each organization. Amended motion: Motion to approve the schedule presented by Scion to submit a July 2023 submission for SB 169 funding. Motion by Adrienne Grey, second by Randi Kinman. Aye: Adrienne Grey, Robert Owens, Randi Kinman, Anne Kepner, and Susan Fish Final Resolution: Motion Carries The timeline has been adopted. A request was made by Randi Kinman to Place a landing page on the WVMCCD website. President Kepner stated that this is a tremendous undertaking. Chancellor Davis thanked Ted for taking his time to present today and he thanked the Board for their input. President Kepner asked about partnerships with other California public secondary institutions and if WVMCCD should engage with them. Ted stated that intersegmental is good, however the WVMCCD application is differentiating from peers, however this is something that we can be discussed. Anne Suggested that Ted attend the next LPOC meeting to discuss the scoring rubrics. 2.4 Oral Communications from the Public on Items on the Agenda. Pursuant to Government Code 54954.3(b) Oral Communication is limited during special meetings to items listed on the published agenda. There was none. Adjournment The meeting was adjourned at 12:03 PM
