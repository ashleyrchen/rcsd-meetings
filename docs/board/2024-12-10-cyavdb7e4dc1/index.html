CONSIDER APPROVAL OF MISSION COLLEGE TO ATTEND THE 18TH ANNUAL AFRICAN AMERICAN MALE STUDENT SUCCESS SUMMIT - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve up to seventeen (17) students, faculty, staff, and administrators representing Mission College to the 18th Annual African American Male Student Success Summit on March 5-7, 2025.

Prepared By: Omar Murillo & Ken Songco Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The Summit will take place at the Hyatt Regency Los Angeles International Airport in Los Angeles, CA. A combination of funds from Mission College Student Equity and Achievement Program and the Conference Leave Committee have been identified to support this effort. Below are the estimated total costs of attendance: Item Cost Early Bird Registration $8,780 Round Trip Airfare $3,875 Lodging $11,100 Meals $1,625 Ground Transportation $1,150 ESTIMATED TOTAL COST $26,530 Reference(s) AP6311 states: “Prior Board approval must be obtained for any travel at district expense if the total travel expense will exceed $10,000 for any one event or purpose, without regard to the number of individuals traveling.” Background/Alternatives The African American Male Education Network and Development (A²MEND) organization will be hosting its 18 th Annual African American Male Student Success Summit. This summit brings together Black male college students and educators to encourage and find innovative ideas to increase the success of Black male students in California’s community college system. The summit is hosted by the African American Male Education Network & Development (A²MEND), a nonprofit comprising African American male educators, who voluntarily use their professional expertise to operate a statewide mentoring program for Black male college students. There will also be a Women's Leadership Academy (WLA) on March 5 th that provides professional development and networking opportunities for higher education professionals with a special focus on women in education. This year's theme, "STANDING ON BUSINESS: Operating with excellence in engaging and serving black males," will cultivate the importance of re-establishing black male success in community colleges and their presence in the community as a whole. Coordination The Office of Student Equity and Success is coordinating this effort with members of the Mission College Black Academic Excellence (BAE) Affinity Group and the Umoja Community including Yolanda Barnes, Donnelle McGee, Dr. David Piper, Ken Songco, and Roshawn Walter.
