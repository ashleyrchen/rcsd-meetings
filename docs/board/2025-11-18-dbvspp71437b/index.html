Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. CONSIDER APPROVAL OF CURRICULUM CHANGES AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the curriculum changes at West Valley College as presented.

Prepared By: Kristen Jackanich, Luis Portillo, Doonu Barife, Chris Dyer and Joe Morello Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The general fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. These costs are offset, to varying degrees, by apportionment received for credit-based instruction and other associated fees. Reference(s) Curriculum development and recommendation is an ongoing activity overseen by the West Valley College Academic Senate, and its subordinate curriculum committee, and the office of instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the West Valley College program review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per district policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and recommended by the curriculum committee and the articulation officer. Each of the curriculum changes and the foregoing recommendations has been further reviewed by the chief instructional officer, the college president, and the district chancellor. The office of instruction further certifies that the programs and courses described herein satisfy all applicable requirements of Title 5 and have also been submitted to the Associated Students Government of West Valley College. Degrees developed in compliance with state-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the district chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees, and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between West Valley College and Mission College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the college catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the college catalog and from the state curriculum inventory. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE MISSION COLLEGE JAPAN STUDY ABROAD TRIP IN SPRING 2026 - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve thirteen attendees (9 nursing students and 4 chaperones) to participate in the Japan Study Abroad Trip in Osaka, Japan from March 27 – April 4, 2026.

Prepared By: Chigusa Katoku Reviewed By: Omar Murillo and Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The estimated total cost for 9 students and 4 chaperones to participate in the program is $36,400. Land Corporation International Travel Special Project Fund: Allocated to Mission College specifically for national and international student travel, this fund will cover the expenses for eleven (12) participants—nine nursing students, one nursing faculty member, and two administrators — totaling $33,600. Health Services Foundation Account: This fund will cover the expenses for one district nurse chaperone, totaling $2,800. Below is the estimated cost of the program per participant for 13 attendees: Item Estimated Cost Staff Chaperone Travel Expenses *Program Fee for I.S. Nursing College $1,200 Airfare $1,500 Per Staff Chaperone Total: $2,700 4 Staff Chaperones Total: $10,800 Student Travel Expenses Registration $1,200 Airfare $1,500 Per Student Total: $2,700 9 Student Participant Total: $24,300 Trip Insurance Trip Insurance Per Attendee $100 Total Trip Insurance $1,300 TOTAL COSTS: $36,400 *The program fees cover ground transportation, accommodations, and most meals Reference(s) AP6311 states: “Prior Board approval must be obtained for any travel at district expense if the total travel expense will exceed $10,000 for any one event or purpose, without regard to the number of individuals traveling.” Background/Alternatives Mission College began its collaboration with Izumisano Sennan Nursing College (I.S. Nursing College) in Japan in 2022. The partnership aims to create meaningful educational opportunities that foster intercultural understanding and global competencies, preparing students from both institutions to better serve diverse populations in their communities. Since Spring 2023, Mission College has hosted delegations of students and chaperones from I.S. Nursing College for a 7-day nursing study abroad program. These visits occurred annually in Spring 2023, 2024, and 2025, with the fourth group scheduled to visit in February 2026. In Spring 2024 and 2025, a delegation from Mission College—comprised of nine nursing students, two administrators, one faculty member, and one staff member from district health services—traveled to Osaka, Japan to participate in a one-week nursing study abroad program. The itinerary included a visit to I.S. Nursing College, hospital tours, engagement activities with I.S. Nursing College students, and cultural excursions to historical sites such as Nara. The impact of this program has been profound, as evident by testimonials from past participants: “I absolutely feel this program has both changed and enhanced my nursing school experience. It’s essentially a milestone for me in the LVN program”, and: “This trip undoubtedly left a permanent mark, shaping our professional growth and fostering lasting connections and memories.” Among other activities, a partnership liaison meeting will be held with the IS Nursing School leadership team. Coordination The program is being coordinated by the district executive director of general services and the district executive director of health services, in partnership with the Mission College president, vice president of student services, director of international programs and services, and assistant director of the vocational nursing program, along with the vice president of I.S. Nursing College. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, travel arrangements will be finalized and confirmed. In addition, presentations will be organized for participating students to share their experiences with the Mission College and district community. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE 2025-2028 MISSION COLLEGE STUDENT EQUITY PLAN - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the Mission College 2025-2028 Student Equity Plan.

Prepared By: Omar Murillo and Ken Songco Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Funding for the 2025-2028 Student Equity Plan is provided through the California Community Colleges Chancellor’s Office (CCCCO) Office of Equitable Student Learning, Experience, and Impact, under the Student Equity and Achievement (SEA) Program authorized by Education Code §78220–78222. Mission College has been awarded $2,574,694 in annual SEA funding, which began on July 1, 2025. Additional college and district resources—including Guided Pathways, Strong Workforce, AB 19/1705, and Land Corporation funds—may supplement implementation activities Reference(s) Education Code §78220–78222 require each community college to maintain a Student Equity Plan to ensure equitable educational opportunities and eliminate achievement gaps among student groups. These statutes authorize the Student Equity and Achievement (SEA) Program, which funds colleges to implement activities supporting access, persistence, completion, and transfer for disproportionately impacted student populations. Background/Alternatives The Mission College 2025-2028 Student Equity Plan outlines institutional goals and strategies to close equity gaps and improve outcomes for disproportionately impacted student groups, with particular focus on Black/African American, First-Generation, and Latine students. The plan aligns with the Mission College Equity Framework, ADES Black Star Movement, and HSI Servingness (ESCALA MTS) efforts. It addresses all required statewide metrics—successful enrollment, completion of transfer-level math and English, persistence, degree/certificate completion, transfer, and comprehensive education planning—through targeted, evidence-based interventions such as equity-based faculty development, first-year experience programs, expanded mentoring, and transfer support initiatives. The plan builds upon prior Student Equity Plans and fulfills state mandates under the Student Equity and Achievement (SEA) Program for the 2025-2028 cycle. Coordination The plan was developed under the leadership of dean of student equity and success Ken Songco in collaboration with the student equity and transformation committee, executive council, and college governance bodies. The plan will be vetted through college council and implemented through cross-functional teams beginning January 2026. Follow-up/Outcome Mission College will submit required SEA annual and term-end reports and the new Vision Aligned Reporting (VAR) to the California Community Colleges Chancellor’s Office beginning December 2025. Progress will be monitored by the student equity and transformation committee, which will coordinate evaluation, resource allocation, and reporting across all implementation teams. §
