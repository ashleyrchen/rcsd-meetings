AGREEMENT BETWEEN MISSION COLLEGE, WEST VALLEY COLLEGE, AND THE BAY AREA COMMUNITY COLLEGE CONSORTIUM TO ENTER INTO CONTRACT FOR STRONG WORKFORCE PROGRAM FUNDING, REGIONAL SHARE FROM ROUND 6. Recommended Action: It is recommended that the Board of Trustees accept the Strong Workforce program subgrant to West Valley and Mission and authorize Ngoc Chim, Vice Chancellor of Administrative Services for the West Valley Mission Community College District, to execute and make all necessary arrangements in relation to this grant agreement and any future amendments and augmentations on behalf of both colleges.

Prepared By: Jackie Escajeda and Brad Weisberg Reviewed By: Norma Ambriz-Galaviz and Stephanie Kashima Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact West Valley College and Mission College incur no cost to participate. Funding is provided by the Bay Area Community College Consortia (BACCC) in conjunction with the California Community College Chancellor&rsquo;s Office and is administered by the Cabrillo Community College District which serves as the fiscal agent. Reference(s) As part of both college&rsquo;s strategic goals, they are charged with aligning courses and programs with labor market data and industry demands and opportunities. The Strong Workforce program provides West Valley College and Mission College the autonomy to be innovative and explore new ways to develop new career technical education (CTE) programs that target specific labor shortages in our region and update existing programs to ensure students are competitive and successful in their field of study. It is the work of both colleges to increase student retention and graduation rates while offering industry-informed pathways that prepare students for high paying jobs. It is also an opportunity to create supportive programs and outreach campaigns to serve more disproportionately impacted students and to provide students with cutting edge technology to enhance student learning outcomes Background/Alternatives The Strong Workforce program allocates $248,000,000 per year to the state community college system for the purpose of expanding the availability of quality community college career technical education and workforce development courses, programs, pathways, credentials, certificates, and degrees. The legislation provided for 40% of the funds to be distributed through and allocated by the regional consortia to their member colleges for the purpose of funding regionally prioritized projects and programs that meet the needs of local and regional economies. Furthermore, these contracts include the transfer of funds from Round 6 BACCC Regional Joint Ventures (RJV) projects to the lead colleges of the RJV project based on the on the legislation requirement that funds target labor markets where the number of job openings meets or exceeds the supply of candidates from community colleges and from other education and training providers. It also requires funds to be utilized to facilitate alignment of CTE programs across multiple colleges to better serve regional scale industries and labor markets. The Strong Workforce program is a significant indication of the governor and the legislature&rsquo;s interest in supporting the CTE mission and of their desire to build the system&rsquo;s capacity to become important contributors to the regional economies that are essential to California&rsquo;s long-term economic vitality. The table below details the regional allocations to both campuses. West Valley College&rsquo;s Strong Workforce Regional Round 6 Allocations: Expenditure Period for Funds Regional Direct-to-College Base Allocation Regional Incentive Fund Allocation Regional Augmentation Allocation RJV Funds Redirected to Another College Total Contract Funds 7/1/2021-6/30/2023 256,710 53,541 65,763 50,000 326,014 Mission College&rsquo;s Strong Workforce Regional Round 6 Allocations: Expenditure Period for Funds Regional Direct-to-College Base Allocation Regional Incentive Fund Allocation Regional Augmentation Allocation RJV Funds Redirected to Another College Total Contract Funds 7/1/2021-6/30/2023 385,065 80,311 80,052 18,333 527,095 Descriptions of the Round 6 BACCC Regional Joint Ventures (RJV) Projects: The RJV that West Valley College is participating in is the Building an Equitable Entrepreneurial Ecosystem for $50,000. Through a public-private partnership with ESO Ventures, Inc, West Valley College is one of six participating community colleges that will directly target neighboring communities of color and will launch two cohorts of 20-25 students to incubate new Black and Brown businesses. Aspiring entrepreneurs will have the opportunity to complete a 10-module entrepreneurship boot camp through ESO Ventures while simultaneously completing their noncredit small business essentials certificate at West Valley College. Through targeted marketing efforts and enrollment, West Valley will establish a non-traditional enrollment pipeline of community members who are aspiring entrepreneurs. The RJV that Mission College RJV is participating in is Nurse Educators RJV Cost Share for $10,000. The lead college for this RJV is Evergreen Valley and the goal of the project is to help colleges recruit nursing faculty and train RNs to become faculty in a community college nursing program. This will help address the shortage of nursing faculty at a community college. The second RJV that Mission College is participating in is Netlab RJV for $8,333. The lead college is Cabrillo. Netlab is a hands-on lab technology providing equitable remote access for information technology students. The Netlab system is a server appliance provided by Network Development Group with software tools preloaded for institutions to host real lab equipment, virtual machines, and lab content at a location for trainees to complete labs. Coordination The academic deans who oversee CTE programs work collaboratively with the faculty on the projects. RESOLUTION NO. 2206211 APPROVING THE REQUIRED MILITARY EQUIPMENT USE POLICY IN ACCORDANCE WITH ASSEMBLY BILL 481 AND GOVERNMENT CODE SECTION 7070 – Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution NO. 2206211 approving the District Police Department’s Military Equipment Policy (709) and authorizing the funding, acquisition, collaboration, and use of specific equipment.

Prepared By: Chris Rolen Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The identified military equipment, associated supplies and training are funded by the General Fund. Reference(s) Assembly Bill 481, Law enforcement and state agencies: military equipment: funding, acquisition, and use. Government Code Sections 7070-7072. District Police Department Lexipol Policy 709-Military Equipment. Background/Alternatives On September 30, 2021, Governor Gavin Newsom signed a series of eight (8) policing reform legislation, including Assembly Bill 481 which created Government Code Sections 7070. This law requires all law enforcement agencies in the State to obtain approval of the applicable governing body, by adoption of a &ldquo;military equipment use policy,&rdquo; as specified, by ordinance at a regular meeting held pursuant to open meeting laws, prior to taking certain actions relating to the funding, acquisition, or use of military equipment as defined. The law also requires approval for the continued use of military equipment acquired prior to January 1, 2022. The governing body is required to annually review the ordinance and either approve or disapprove a renewal of the authorization for a type, as defined, of military equipment or amend the military equipment policy if it determines, based on an annual report prepared by the law enforcement agency, that the military equipment does not comply with the standards for approval. The department has complied with all requirements of Assembly Bill 481 leading up to Board approval, including creating a draft military equipment policy, and making the policy available on the department&rsquo;s website at least 30-days prior to any public hearing concerning the military equipment at issue. Coordination This item has been reviewed by the Chancellor, Associate Vice Chancellor of Public Health and Safety, the Chief&rsquo;s Community Advisory Committee, the Legislative and Policy Oversight Committee and held an open public forum. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the Associate Vice Chancellor of Public Health and Safety-Police Chief, will issue Military Equipment Policy 709 and post the policy on the department&rsquo;s website. The Chief and/or designee will submit to the Board of Trustees an annual military equipment report for each type of military equipment approved and in use within one year of approval, and annually thereafter for as long as the military equipment is available for use. The next annual report will be submitted to the Board of Trustees in May 2023. §
