Assembly Bill 2449 Remote Teleconference Attendance Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may be eligible to attend a Board of Education meeting remotely with Just Cause or with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance. To ensure compliance with the notice requirements of AB 2449, the Board will maintain an AB 2449 standing agenda item for each meeting. Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Just Cause submissions received for the current Board meeting. Consider and take action on Emergency Circumstance requests, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance and Board approval. Emergency Circumstance means a physical or family medical emergency that prevents the trustee from attending in person. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. Disclosure of individuals present at remote locations, if any, pursuant to AB 2449 . CONSIDER APPROVAL FOR THE MISSION COLLEGE HOSPITALITY MANAGEMENT DEPARTMENT STUDY ABROAD PROGRAM TO ITALY - SUMMER 2025 - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve up to thirty (30) students and two (2) faculty chaperones from the Hospitality Management Department to participate in Course FDR 076-Hospitality Sales &amp; Marketing study abroad program in Italy from June 24, 2025 to July 7, 2025.

Prepared By: Daniel Arias and Haze Dennis Reviewed By: Lorrie Ranch and Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Students will bear the responsibility for financing the trip. Student scholarships will be provided depending on available funding and will be awarded based on student need and merit. Multiple funding sources and fundraising will be used to further support student expenses. The College will not provide financial assistance for this trip. Below is the estimated cost of attendance per participant for the FDR 076 study abroad trip: ITEM Cost Program Fee $2,800 Airfare $2,000 Meals $700 Travel Insurance $250 ESTIMATED COST PER PARTICIPANT $5,750 TOTAL COST - $5,750 x 30 PARTICIPANTS $172,500 The Program fee of $2,800 includes: Transportation to and from airport in Italy 14-day itinerary with ground transportation Lodging at a hotel upon arrival and lodging at student apartments for the duration of the trip Six to eight meals Tours, hands-on activities, workshops, tastings, markets, wine and cheese production, restaurants, and entrance fees. Constant, onsite support representative for all excursions, and 24-hour emergency access. Classroom access varied across the itinerary, internet access, and SIM card assistance. Hybrid format with instruction, orientation and debrief scheduled onsite at Mission College. For every 15 students, Italy Abroad Program (IAP) will provide each faculty funding that will cover an estimate of 75-80% of faculty expenses. These expenses are applied to the travel and housing needs of the faculty. For additional funding support, faculty will apply for conference funds to cover expense differences and engage in fundraising. The range of cost for the FDR 076 course of fifteen students with one faculty chaperone is $87,500. The range of cost for thirty students with two faculty chaperones is $172,500. Reference(s) AP6311 states: “Prior Board approval must be obtained for any travel at District expense if the total travel expense will exceed $10,000 for any one event or purpose, without regard to the number of individuals traveling.” Background/Alternatives Building on the success of the previous summer, this initiative aims to enhance students’ understanding of international hospitality, sustainability and the dynamics of hospitality marketing, while providing firsthand experience in a vibrant cultural setting. In the summer of 2023, Hospitality Management offered Introduction to Hospitality and Sustainability courses in Florence, Italy and partnered with The Italy Abroad Program (IAP). During the 2022/2023 academic year, other colleges collaborated with IAP, offering courses in fields such as Communication, Kinesiology, Philosophy, Public Health, and Sociology in Ischia, which is proposed as a site for the 2025 program. In Florence, alongside the Hospitality courses, four to five other colleges provided classes in Accounting, Civil Engineering, Finance, Health and Science, Global Marketing, and Philosophy, Ethics, and Social Psychology. The IAP’s effective programming and orientation contributed to an enjoyable experience, and the partnership aims to continue offering unique travel opportunities for students. Travel programs are scheduled to begin in May and run through the summer, with Florence and Ischia as the primary locations. In the winter months, Milan and Naples will host additional programs. The Hospitality Management Department will travel to Ischia, Italy from June 24, 2025 to July 7, 2025, pending board approval. This upcoming program will mark the second opportunity for the Hospitality Management department to engage students in a dynamic and transformative experience. With high demand for these programs, early registration is recommended to secure spots for future travel opportunities. Explored Study Abroad Opportunities The Italy Abroad Program (IAP) was selected as it provides a customized experience that aligns with the program goals, course outlines, and student learning outcomes of the Hospitality Management courses. IAP also offered personalized planning, with Vince Amalfitano revising the itinerary to include more hands-on activities at no extra cost. Additionally, this partnership presents the potential to develop a formal and ongoing "culinary" abroad program for students. Other study abroad providers were also considered: EF Educational Tours: Although well-recognized in the U.S., feedback from participants in their Florence programs was mixed. API Tours (Academic Programs International): Popular but tends to be more expensive and has static itineraries, offering less flexibility. AIFS (American Institute for Foreign Study) Abroad: Offers unique 3–9-week programs globally, but they are significantly more expensive per person. Coordination Upon board approval, the logistics will be coordinated between the executive director of general services, the department chair of hospitality management, the dean of business, education, and industry services, the vice president of instruction, the vice president of administrative services, participating faculty members Haze Dennis and/or Eve Little, and/or Chair Daniel Arias, the associate vice chancellor, advancement, and the administrator of the Italy Abroad Program. Follow-Up/Outcome Upon board approval, hospitality management students will participate in the department’s second travel abroad effort. The department hopes to make this initiative an ongoing and expanding program. This trip will include foundation planning for a possible exclusive culinary experience for the future. FIRST READING - BOARD POLICY 5900 ON FREE COMMUNITY COLLEGE PROGRAM. Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct a first reading of Board Policy 5900 on Free Community College Program and consider public comment.

Prepared By: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed By: Cade Story-Yetto Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact In September 2023, the Board of Trustees authorized the district to utilize unrestricted general funds to provide tuition and enrollment fee grants to students living in the service area of Mission College and West Valley College. To cover the anticipated expenses of the Enrollment Fee Grant for Fall 2024 - Spring 2025, the district set aside approximately $3.4 million from its unrestricted general fund in the FY 24/25 budget. With approval of this policy, the district can replace the Enrollment Fee Grant with the Free Community College Program, as codified in Assembly Bill 3158 (Berman; AB 3158). The district anticipates that the amount of funding needed to administer the new program will remain unchanged. As required by AB 3158, the district shall prepare a fiscal impact statement, including a three-year projection of the fiscal impact of the fee waiver on the district. This fiscal impact statement shall be presented at a public meeting of the Board of Trustees and made available to the public. Reference(s) CA Education Code Sections 76302, 76303, 68000, 68130.5, 76140, 76140.5, 66025.3, 72246(c) (d.1) (d.2), 72252 and 76060.5. Assembly Bill 3158 (Berman; AB 3158). Background/Alternatives Over the past two years, the district has been pursuing various initiatives to utilize local funds to remove financial barriers for students. These efforts include eliminating fees for tuition, parking, healthcare, textbooks, childcare, and other miscellaneous costs. As part of this endeavor, the district established the Enrollment Fee Grant program in September 2023 to provide fee waivers to eligible students. To legally codify and expand upon the waivers administered by the Enrollment Fee Grant program, the district worked with Assemblymember Marc Berman to introduce AB 3158, co-authored by Senators Josh Becker and Dave Cortese, along with Assemblymembers Evan Low and Gail Pellerin. This bill was signed by the Governor on September 22, 2024, and will become effective on January 1, 2025. Consequently, the Free Community College Program will replace the Enrollment Fee Grant, offering broader support under the new legislation. Coordination The policy was reviewed for a first reading by District Council on October 7, 2024, and will be reviewed for a second reading by District Council on November 4, 2024. The policy will also be reviewed by the Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on October 30, 2024. Follow-up/Outcome At the November 12, 2024, Board of Trustees meeting, approval of this policy and the conclusion of the public comment period will be considered on the agenda. If adopted, this policy will allow the Free Community College Program to go into effect by January 1, 2025, coinciding with the effective date of AB 3158. While the Chancellor collaborates with the colleges to develop the related administrative procedure, the district will utilize the language in Education Code Section 76303 in lieu of an administrative procedure to operationalize the Free Community College Program. §
