Santa Clara County continues to move in a positive direction. The County Health Department and the STate are expressing optimism. Metrics are moving in the right direction. The State formally announced that Santa Clara County is moving from the Orange Tier to the less restrictive Yellow tier on May 19, 2021. The Yellow tier is identified as minimal risk. The positivity rate and the number of new cases continue to decline. The District continues to take COVID seriously. Teh District continues to have meetings to discuss all the issues; getting back to campus in a safe manner, focusing heavily on cleaning practices, ensuring the HVAC and ventilation systems are operating at the necessary effectiveness. THE CDC recently released the updated guidance on the wearing of face coverings. The new guidance recommended that authorities consider reducing face cover requirements for vaccinated individuals. However, the state still requires that we use face coverings and maintain social distancing requirements on our campuses. An email was sent out yesterday via the District's COVID-19 response email. The taskforce will continue to review State and County guidance our primary concern is the safety of our employees and students, As a reminder, continue visiting the COVID-19 website for the most current district-wide guidelines and information. The colleges will continue to communicate on college-specific information. Chancellor Davis added that Santa Clara County issued a new Health Order today that takes effect at 12:01 am. Santa Clara County is now requiring businesses and governmental agencies to ascertain the vaccination status of all employees and provide updated reports to the county every 14 days as to the number of people who are unvaccinated. The order also reinforces that although there has been recent CDC guidance to the contrary, Face coverings are still required by the county with this order. This county order backs away from working from home and leans toward returning persons to work outside and encouraging governmental entities and businesses to engage in as much outside activities as possible. We will be discussing how we will go about ascertaining the vaccination status of all of our employees. The County order is silent as it relates to students. We have been working under the presumption that we will engage in the process of discussion with all of our collective bargaining groups as it relates to vaccination protocols for our employees but we have begun at both campuses putting together a survey and talking to student leaders about the prospect of student vaccinations as is being developed at the UC's and the CSU is and most private schools. We have received some updates already from our participatory governance who've done some surveying we expect that we will get a pretty sizable student response. After we have that data, the plan is to begin a discussion as to whether or not we should pursue a policy. That policy discussion will begin at the legislative and policy oversight committee (LPOC) and then makes it way, if necessary to the full board. 3.9 Written or Oral Communication from the Public A number of faculty provided explanations of their concerns regarding WVMFT negotiations and asked for the Board's support of WVMFT and the Faculty. CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE PEARSON VUE OPEN PURCHASE ORDER-Recommendation: That the Board of Trustees approve the increase of an open purchase order #P2100158 to Pearson Vue from $80,000.00 to $125,000.00 on behalf of the Health Workforce Initiative (HWI) Northern Regional Testing Center (NRTC).

Prepared By: Cynthia Harrison Reviewed By: Danny Nguyen and Daniel Peck Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact This purchase order is to procure the Pearson Vue National Nurse Aide Assessment Program (NNAAP) testing materials and services from July 2020 through June 2021. The nurse assistant candidates pay fees that are determined by the California Department of Public Health, which in turn pay for NNAAP testing materials. The fees and the testing materials are managed by the HWI NRTC. There is no fiscal impact to Mission College or West Valley Mission Community College District, as costs are fully funded by the nurse assistant candidate fees. Reference(s) As fiscal agent for NNAAP, Mission College is obligated to procure the NNAAP testing materials and services on behalf of the 220 partnering northern California nurse assistant programs, which includes 40 new programs. Background/Alternatives The HWI NRTC is a Board of Trustees approved regional center hosted at Mission College since 2011. HWI NRTC has been contracted by the California Community College Chancellor&rsquo;s Office and the California Department of Public Health to manage the HWI NRTC for nurse assistant certification testing. The HWI NRTC Director contracts with multiple community colleges and independent nurse assistant programs to coordinate the nurse assistant certification testing for northern California. The request for increase of the open PO is because the HWI NRTC director recruited and trained additional testing sites to accommodate the increased number of nurse assistant candidates. The $125,000.00 will accommodate the estimated 7,500 nurse assistant candidates requiring nurse assistant certification testing for fiscal year 2020/2021. Coordination This purchase order has been reviewed by the HWI NRTC Director, the VP of Administrative Services, and the President of Mission College. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board of Trustees approval, the amended purchase order from $80,000.00 to be increased to $125,000.00 will be submitted to Pearson Vue for the nurse assistant certification testing materials and services. §

AGREEMENT BETWEEN WEST VALLEY COLLEGE AND CAMPBELL UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT FOR A COLLEGE AND CAREER ACCESS PATHWAYS DUAL ENROLLMENT PARTNERSHIP 2021-2022

Prepared By: Chris Dyer Reviewed By: Stephanie Kashima Approved By: BRADLEY DAVIS Funding Source/Fiscal Impact If approved, West Valley College will offer community college pathway courses exclusively to CUHSD students at West Valley College or the Campbell Union High School District campuses. West Valley-Mission Community College District (WVMCCD) will claim apportionment for enrolled students under this CCAP Agreement. These classes will be in addition to course sections offered on the West Valley College campus and will generate full-time equivalent students (FTES). Reference(s) Education Code 76004 (Assembly Bill 288) establishes the College and Career Access Pathways Act to authorize California Community College districts to enter into formal partnership agreements with local school districts. The goal is to create easier pathways from high school to community college and to offer or expand dual enrollment opportunities for under-represented higher education students or for students who may not be college bound. This partnership aligns with the goals of West Valley College&#39;s Educational and Facilities Master Plan and its Student Equity Plan related to improving the student pathways to degrees and transfer, strengthening collaborative partnerships with K-12 institutions, and increasing access and success for underrepresented groups. On October 4, 2019, the Governor signed Assembly Bill 30 and Senate Bill 5861 both of which amended the law in the same way to make pupil participation in the College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) program and the CCAP agreement process less burdensome for colleges. These changes include: The elimination of the requirement that each district present the purposed CCAP agreements to their respective governing boards twice. Each district is now only required to present the purposed CCAP agreement once to their respective governing boards. This agreement has been reviewed by the West Valley College President, West Valley College Vice President of Academic Affairs, West Valley College Dean of Instruction, West Valley College Vice-President of Student Services, Superintendent of the Campbell Union High School District and the Assistant Superintendent of the Campbell Union High School District. This CCAP template has been reviewed by the District&rsquo;s Attorney, Liebert Cassidy Whitmore. This agreement is also being presented to the CUHSD Board and may undergo changes before it is returned. Background/Alternatives Students who complete college credit while enrolled in high school, are more likely to earn high school diplomas, to enroll in community colleges and four-year colleges, to attend post-secondary education on a full-time basis, and to complete degrees in those institutions, than students without these experiences. CUHSD is a public-school district serving grades 9-12 with students whose residences are located within the regional service area of WVMCCD and these students deserve an opportunity to participate in an accelerated process that can further ensure academic success. This CCAP Agreement allows students to pursue this dual enrollment opportunity. Courses offered as part of this CCAP Agreement shall be community college courses acceptable towards a career technical education credential or certificate, or preparation for transfer, or appropriate to improve high school graduation rates or help high school pupils achieve college and career readiness. Coordination The operational and instructional aspects of this agreement will be administered and monitored by West Valley College, Vice Provost and Dean of Instruction. An Educational Administrator to be specified in the Appendix to the CCAP Agreement will serve as point of contact to facilitate coordination and cooperation between the college and CUHSD, in conformity with WVMCCD policies and standards. Follow-up/Outcome After the proposed Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Dual Enrollment Partnership Agreement is approved by the WVMCCD Board of Trustees and the CUHSD Board, West Valley College will submit the agreement to the CCC Chancellor&rsquo;s Office for review and coordinate with CUHSD staff to implement the pathway in preparation for the next cohort in the Fall 2021 semester. §

RESOLUTION 21051801 SUPPORTING ASSEMBLY BILL 927, COMMUNITY COLLEGES: STATEWIDE BACCALAUREATE DEGREE PROGRAM, INTRODUCED BY ASSEMBLY MEMBER MEDINA.

Prepared By: Manny Cappello Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) Board Policies 4020 (Curriculum Development), 4021 (Hours and Units), 4022 (Curriculum Approval), 4050 (Articulation), 4260 (Prerequisites and Co-Requisites) and Education Code Sections 66030, 66250 et seq., 87100 et seq., and 87100 et seq.; Title 5 sections 53000 et seq., and 59300 et seq.; and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964. Background/Alternatives Lawmakers are recognizing that a growing number of workforce fields and employers require a bachelor’s degree to qualify for employment. However, obtaining these degrees are high cost and can result in significant student debt. Community colleges are able to provide high quality, low cost and easily accessible programs students need to obtain the bachelor’s degree necessary to meet job qualifications. AB 927 makes the existing baccalaureate pilot program permanent and expands these programs to all California community colleges. Coordination Resolution 21051801 was reviewed by the Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on April 28, 2021, and District Council on May 10, 2021. Follow-up/Outcome The Chancellor will publicly post this resolution on each campus and the WVMCCD website and email the approved Resolution 21051801 to the office of Assembly Member Medina and the California Community College League. §
