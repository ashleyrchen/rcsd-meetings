CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE ACCREDITATION FOLLOW-UP COMPLIANCE REPORT FOR WEST VALLEY COLLEGE

Prepared By: Stacy Gleixner Reviewed By: Stephanie Kashima Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no cost of submitting the follow-up report online. Reference(s) In accordance with the requirements of Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges (ACCJC) Standard IVB.1.i &ldquo;The governing board is informed about and involved in the accreditation process,&rdquo; ACCJC eligibility requirements 20 and 21, and WVMCCD District board policies and administrative procedures 3200 &ldquo;Accreditation,&rdquo; the follow-up report is being presented for Board of Trustee for approval. Background/Alternatives The Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges (ACCJC) of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC) requires each of its member colleges to complete a comprehensive self-evaluation every six years and to undergo an ACCJC evaluation team site visit as part of the accreditation process. The last full accreditation process for West Valley College took place in 2020. It resulted in several recommendations emanating from the internal self-evaluations and the ACCJC evaluative process. ACCJC reaffirmed accreditation for both colleges following the completion of the site visit and follow-up reports. The follow-up report must contain information pertaining to two district compliance requirements: Standard III.A.5 (District Requirement 1) : In order to meet the Standard, the Commission requires that the District systematically evaluate all personnel at stated intervals in accordance with college policies. Standard III.C.2 (District Requirement 2) : In order to the meet the Standard, the Commission requires that the District continuously plan technology updates and replacements with the colleges to ensure quality and capacity of technology are adequate to support the College&rsquo;s mission, operations, programs, and services. In the interim, West Valley has responded to the recommendations and have completed the follow-up report which is due October 1, 2021 to ACCJC. Coordination West Valley created a taskforce to provide general oversight and sharing of ideas and information for the follow-up report. This work was led by the faculty accreditation liaison officer, John Hannigan, and the classified professional accreditation liaison officer, Ana Lobato. The task force coordinated proper timelines to ensure accurate information for each recommendation response. The task force also integrated the logistics of editing and distribution. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval, the follow-up report will be submitted to ACCJC for distribution to the ACCJC Evaluation Teams October 1, 2021. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE AGREEMENT BETWEEN WEST VALLEY COLLEGE AND THE BAY AREA COMMUNITY COLLEGE CONSORTIUM TO ENTER INTO CONTRACT FOR STRONG WORKFORCE FUNDING, REGIONAL SHARE, ROUND FIVE.

Prepared By: Brad Weisberg Reviewed By: Stephanie Kashima Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact West Valley College incurs no cost to participate. Funding is provided by the Bay Area Community College Consortium in conjunction with the California Community College Chancellor&rsquo;s Office and is administered by the Cabrillo Community College District which serves as the fiscal agent. Reference(s) As part of the college&rsquo;s strategic goals, West Valley is charged with aligning courses and programs with labor market opportunities. The Strong Workforce program gives the college the freedom to explore new ways to address this issue by bringing old programs up to date and generating new programs that target specific labor shortages in our region. It is the work of the college to develop industry-informed pathways that prepare students for high paying jobs and increase student retention and graduation rates. It is also an opportunity to create supportive programs and outreach campaigns to serve more disproportionate students and to provide students with cutting edge technology to enhance student learning outcomes. Background/Alternatives The Strong Workforce program allocates approximately $248 million per year for the purpose of expanding the availability of quality community college career technical education and workforce development courses, programs, pathways, credentials, certificates, and degrees. The legislation provided for 40% of the funds to be distributed through and allocated by the regional consortia to their member colleges for the purpose of funding regionally prioritized projects and programs that meet the needs of local and regional economies. West Valley College&rsquo;s expenditure obligation for round five of the strong workforce regional funds total $337,289. The total includes our involvement in a regional joint venture project with Santa Rosa Junior College which will incur an outgoing cost of $25,000, and another joint venture in collaboration with the Bridge to Recovery program with an increase of $52,038. Coordination In coordination with the college provost, the academic deans in areas that produce FTES in CTE will oversee the project funding. The fiscal reporting agent for the college will be responsible for entering project templates into the state chancellor&rsquo;s fiscal reporting software (NOVA), submitting quarterly reports and a final report, monitoring the progress of the projects, and informing the college provost of that progress. Follow-up/Outcome The Associate Vice Chancellor of Finance and Administration will execute the agreement on the District&rsquo;s behalf. The academic deans will coordinate the college&rsquo;s participation and will provide progress reports as requested. §

13.1 · Action · Board

SECOND READING OF THE NEW POLICY TO BE ADDED TO BOARD POLICY MANUAL REGARDING COVID-19 VACCINATIONS – CHAPTER 3: GENERAL INSTITUTION – SECOND READING OF BOARD POLICY 3960 COVID-19 VACCINATION POLICY - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees review and approve the Second Reading draft of Board Policy 3960 of Chapter 3. This policy addition protects the health and well-being of students, faculty, staff, administrators, and the communities served.

Prepared By: Manny Cappello Reviewed By: Legislative and Policy Oversight Committee Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Costs for the service and those that may be related to consideration of a new policy addition are contained in the General Fund Budget and Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund (HEERF). Reference(s) California Code of Regulations, Title 8, Section 3203 California Constitution, Article IX, Section 14 Education Code, Section 70902 Education Code, Section 76020 Education Code, Section 76403 Education Code, Section 87408 United States Code Title 29 Section 654(a)(1) United States Equal Employment Opportunity Commission Guidance California Community Colleges Chancellor&rsquo;s Office 2021-01 Advisory Background/Alternatives The West Valley-Mission CCD is committed to protecting the health and well-being of students, faculty, staff, administrators, and the communities we serve, as well as maintaining higher education access and attainment for our students. As we work toward the safe resumption of increased on-campus learning, working and other activities, we embrace a comprehensive strategy designed to reduce the likelihood of transmission of the COVID-19 virus. This approach contributes to the overarching goal of achieving population-level immunity throughout the West Valley-Mission CCD. In light of the evidence established to date regarding the safety and effectiveness of available COVID-19 vaccines, the District would require all individuals to be vaccinated against COVID-19 unless approved as exempt due to verified medical or religious reasons. Those who do not receive a COVID-19 vaccination shall wear face coverings and comply with twice weekly mandatory testing. The vaccine requirement would be effective on each campus and in District Services Coordination The proposed Board Policy 3960 titled COVID-19 Vaccination Policy is intended to reduce the likelihood of transmission of the COVID-19 virus. This policy contributes to the District&#39;s overarching goal of achieving population-level immunity through the West Valley-Mission CCD. The proposed Board Policy was reviewed at the Legislative and Policy Oversight Committee on August 25, 2021, and shared at District Council for a first reading on August 30, 2021 and a second reading on September 13, 2021. Follow-up/Outcomes Following the Board approval of board policy, the District will post the revised policy on the District website. §
