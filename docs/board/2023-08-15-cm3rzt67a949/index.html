ACCEPT CA COMMUNITY COLLEGES CHANCELLOR&#039;S OFFICE CULTURALLY RESPONSIVE PEDAGOGY &amp; PRACTICES (CRPP) INNOVATIVE BEST PRACTICES GRANTS AWARDED TO MISSION COLLEGE AND WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees authorize the administration to accept the one-time $298,372 Mission College grant award and the one-time $299,919 West Valley College grant award from the CA Community Colleges Chancellor&#039;s Office to be distributed over a two-year period (2023-2025).

Prepared By: GRETCHEN EHLERS & KEN SONGCO Reviewed By: SEHER AWAN AND JOE McDEVITT Approved By: BRADLEY DAVIS Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The 2021 Budget Postsecondary Education Trailer Bill (AB 132 ) Reference(s) The 2021 Budget Postsecondary Education Trailer Bill (AB 132) appropriated $20 million in one - time funding for allocation to community college districts to support a systemwide effort to provide culturally competent professional development, with an emphasis on improving learning outcomes. A total of $5.8 million was provided to districts through apportionment during the 2021 -2022 academic year First Principal Apportionment (P1). The remaining $14.2 million is being made available to districts as Culturally Responsive Pedagogy & Practices Innovative Best Practices Grants . Background/Alternatives The core goal of the Culturally Responsive Pedagogy & Practices (CRPP) Innovative Best Practices Grants is to develop Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Accessibility (DEIA) -focused professional development opportunities to upskill faculty and successfully expand their c apacities to serve students, improving students experiences and outcomes. The CRPP Innovative Best Practices Grants are competitive, one-time grants to aid institutions that develop innovative DEIA- minded and culturally competent professional development training for faculty. Trainings should focus specifically on nurturing the development of culturally responsive pedagogy and classroom practices for disproportionately impacted and underrepresented communities. The Mission College CRPP Grant, Equity-Minded Transformation through Sustained Faculty Learning, is designed to develop a sustained model of faculty professional learning opportunities grounded in culturally responsive classroom practices. This two-year grant will build upon Mission Colleges existing Equity Pedagogy Community of Praxis model and will further expand faculty professional learning opportunities through Universal Design for Learning with a Culturally Responsive Teaching lens, Equity-based Design Labs, and a new Equity Data Coaching Program. Outcomes of the grant will help increase student success and wellness for our Latinx, African American, DSPS, first generation college students and our LGBTQ+ the student groups who are most disproportionately impacted in terms of outcomes at the College by creating sustained faculty professional learning that focuses on equity pedagogy. The assignment will include both virtual and in-person/on-campus presence to address the need for in-person workshops, office hours, and permit some level of flexibility. The West Valley College CRPP Grant, Inclusive and Equitable Teaching Community of Practice, is designed to create an ongoing faculty CoP (community of practice) integrating the colleges and the districts student equity initiatives with proven training in designing and teaching incl usive and equitable classes. These existing initiatives include our Caring Campus faculty behavior commitments ; our Anti- Racism and Inclusion Action Plan ; the 23-24 faculty CoP Racial Equity in Education facilitated by faculty member Rebecca Cisneros-Diaz focusing on inequities in education from an ethnic studies perspective ; and the districts faculty practicum on Universal Design Learning. We will begin the grant period by hosting three CoPs based on the Association for College and University Educators Inclusive Teaching for Equitable Learning course.
