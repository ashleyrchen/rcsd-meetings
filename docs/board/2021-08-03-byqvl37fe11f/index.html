Presented by: Dr. Ray Gamba, Dean of Social Sciences, West Valley College President Stephanie Kashima introduced Dr. Ray Gamba, stating that Dr. Gamba has led the way in transitioning to a 100% online format for the past 18 months. APPROVE &quot;DOCUSIGN&quot; PROFESSIONAL SIGNATURE AND DOCUMENT MANAGEMENT SERVICES CONTRACT - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve a contract with &quot;DOCUSIGN&quot; for contract and internal authorization signature and legal records services, for an amount not to exceed $279,000, effective July 1, 2021 through June 30, 2024.

Prepared By: Dan Borges Reviewed By: Michael Robins Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact This contract will be funded by the General Fund. Reference(s) The District adopted the &quot;Docusign&quot; platform for remote signing and recording of contract signatures and internal authorizations on documents starting in 2017. By entering into a three-year agreement at $93,000 per year, the District will save 4.7% annually and keep costs flat. BP and AP 6340 require prior board approval to contract with an independent contractor or consulting for an amount more than $100,000. Government Code Section 53060 allows the District to contract for special professional services without competitive bidding; the executive director of general services deems these services as &quot;professional and specialized&quot; due to the complex legal and cyber protections built into the Docusign signing and records management platform. Background/Alternatives Electronic signing platforms have become a routine requirement of conducting the District&#39;s business, particularly its contracting and maintenance of legal records functions. While other electronic signing platforms exist, Docusign provides some unique features, including ease of use across platforms and enterprises. Further, once an organization selects an electronic signing and record-keeping platform, provided it fulfills its functions well, it is important to maintain that platform for consistency, staff training, and ease of access to records. Docusign has been an excellent platform in terms of function, ease of use, and efficiency, particularly in response to the Covid-19 pandemic driven need for remote operations. Docusign has been configured as a Single-Sign-On (SSO) application on the WVM network such that user identity and authentication is carried forward when connecting to the application which provides greater ease of use and improved cybersecurity. Coordination The associate vice chancellor of information & educational technology and the executive director of general services have negotiated with Docusign and reviewed the contract. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the associate vice chancellor of finance & administration will sign a contract with Docusign in the amount of $279,000 for three years of service. §
