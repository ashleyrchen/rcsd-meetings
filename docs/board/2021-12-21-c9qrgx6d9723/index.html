Special Board Meeting - Patrick Henry Drive Project (Tuesday, December 21, 2021) Generated by Rebecca Alvarez on Tuesday, January 4, 2022 1. Call to Order-Public Session The meeting was called to order at 6:07 pm by President Kepner. 1.1 Roll Call Members present Susan Fish, Randi Kinman, Robert Owens, Jack Lucas, Anne Kepner, Adrienne Grey Student Trustee: Gabrielle Wilson 1.2 Pledge of Allegiance The pledge of allegiance was recited. 1.3 Oral Communications from the Public on Items on the Agenda. Pursuant to Government Code 54954.3(b) Oral Communication is limited during special meetings to items listed on the published agenda. Comments were made by the following members of the public: Mary Grizzle Brian Goldenberg Yun Lin Maria Rodriguez Steve Kelly - email received 1.4 Patrick Henry Drive Briefing - Daniel Peck, Mission College President President Peck provided a briefing with a PowerPoint presentation, stating that details and full plan is available on the City of Santa Clara Planning Division website. Highlights of the project include: Approximately 74 acres of land are bounded by Calabazas Creek, Mission College, Great America Parkway, and the SFPUC right of way. Transform several existing office parks into a cohesive, high-density mixed-use community Multiple individual landowners and developers have worked jointly with the City towards this plan. Between 10.000 - 12,000 new housing units. Mixed-use with some retail primarily aimed at community residents. Public use facilities such as parks, a community room, and space for a public library. Mission College Boulevard is currently owned and maintained by the District, requires the permission of the District to proceed. Access road would include a traffic light. Summerhill has proposed a 9.250 square foot park area. Updated Plan: There are now plans for green space across from the campus. President Peck stated his recommendation of the project, which was not to move forward with the road due to safety concerns, potential traffic impacts, and unknowns of future costs. He believes that there are other ways that Mission College can engage with the City. Chancellor Davis echoed President Peck's sentiments stating that he does not believe that the District should move forward with this project. He expressed that the project boils down to a traffic issue. Discussion ensued with members of the Board expressing their concerns. 1.5 Consideration and possible action regarding Patrick Henry Drive Project Motion that the Board advises the City of Santa Clara that the Board does not agree nor will the Board participate in granting access to the road as proposed and as listed in the Patrick Henry Drive Project presentation shown on the evening of Tuesday, December 21, 2021. Motion by Randi Kinman, second by Adrienne Grey Final Resolution: Motion passes unanimously Aye: Susan Fish, Adrienne Grey, Jack Lucas, Robert Owens, Anne Kepner, and Randi Kinman Student Trustee advisory votes: Aye: Gabrielle Wilson 2. Adjournment The meeting was adjourned at 7:15 PM
