Some of the Community Health Partner (CHP) clinics include Gardner Health Services, Ravenswood Family Health Network, Northeast Medical Services, School Health Centers of Santa Clara County, Peninsula Health Care Connection, Marmonte Community Clinics, Planned Parenthood, and the Indian Health Center of Santa Clara Valley. In response to that need, Ann Cowel and Julie Barba used Mission College's credit CHW programto develop a three course, non-credit CHW program with externships at the clinic partners. The courses could be accomplished in 12 weeks (162 hours), when students would earn their certificate of completion. The CHW acts as the link between communities and health care services, with strong focus on low-income individuals with chronic conditions, mental illnesses and substance abuse. CHWs worked with medical doctors, nurses and mental health care professionals and provided outreach, care management and coordination, home-based support, health promotion and coaching, and navigation of the health care system for their clients while acting as community/cultural liaison. During spring and summer 2020, Julia Barba and Ann Cowels determined the course content, lectures, activities, class materials and externship guidelines and secured six externship sites for the first student cohort. Santa Clara County Supervisor Susan Ellenberg championed this pilot CHW accelerated program and recommended they receive a one-time $85,000 grant, which was granted by the SCC Board of Supervisors. The one-year project included: &bull; Curriculum Development &bull; Identification of Student Cohort &bull; Established Agreements with Participating Community Health Center Sites &bull; Implementation of Curriculum & Successful Completion of Student Cohort &bull; Placement of Students at Community Health Centers to Complete Internship &bull; Evaluation and Tracking &bull; Plan for program sustainability and supports for completing students in collaboration with local health care providers and potential CHW employers Twenty-one students participated in the first cohort comprised of community health center staff and Mission College students. The three courses included: 1) Introduction to Community Health Worker, 2) Introduction to Chronic Disease Management, and 3) Externship for Community Health Workers. Student Melanie Zamora talked about her externship at Mayview Health Center, which is part of Ravenswood Family Health Network. The Primary Care Access Program where she was working increased enrollment through outreach and designed bilingual educational materials that improved access to medical health services for the uninsured and undocumented population in our community. Her externship outcomes included the following: Personal Development: strengthen listening and communication skills, strengthen technical and artistic skills, and strengthen leadership and team work skills. Professional Development: Obtained information about health coverage options for uninsured and undocumented patients, screened and referred patients for PCAP, successfully enrolled about 30 uninsured patients in PCAP, and created an educational brochure in English and Spanish. The program was a big success, with all 21 students in the cohort on track to completion. After the presentation, members of the Board spoke highly of this program and hoped that it could continue and expand.
