AGREEMENT BETWEEN WEST VALLEY COLLEGE AND LOS GATOS-SARATOGA UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT AND CAMPBELL UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT FOR A MIDDLE COLLEGE PROGRAM - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approves the agreement between Los Gatos-Saratoga Union High School District (LGSUHSD), Campbell Union High School District (CUHSD), and West Valley-Mission Community College District regarding Middle College at West Valley College.

Prepared By: Anthony Djedi Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact West Valley College will utilize existing resources and facilities to support the Middle College High School Program. Reference(s) The California Education Code (EDC) section 11300, part of Chapter 14 of Title 1, Division 1, Part 7, defines Middle College High Schools (MCHS) as a collaboration between community colleges and local school districts. MCHS are secondary schools on college campuses that offer challenging academic programs for high-potential and high-risk students. The goal is to help students achieve their academic potential through a nurturing environment integrating college preparation, career education, work experience, and community engagement. MCHS includes a curriculum that focuses on college and career preparation, a reduced adult-student ratio, flexible scheduling, as well as opportunities for internships, work apprenticeships, and community service. Background/Alternatives Middle College High School was developed at LaGuardia Community College in New York City in 1974 and was designed to proactively address the large number of students who were dropping out of high school. The unique collaborative model of a high school located on and integrated into the community college environment was developed to lower high school dropout rates among what were termed high-ability, at-risk youth. This program enables students to obtain a quality high school education while concurrently receiving direct and invaluable access to college courses and services. Coordination Mark Manrose, Director of Continuing Education, will serve as WVC lead administrator in support of the Middle College High School Program. CUHSD will provide an on-site administrator. LGSUHSD will identify an administrative designee to support the MCHS students that it sponsors and coordinate with the CUHSD on-site administrator. Follow-up/Outcome Upon approval by the Board of Trustees, West Valley College will continue to serve as a Middle College High School site for the students of Los Gatos, Saratoga, and Campbell. The contract shall be reviewed annually by April 1. If all parties agree, the agreement shall remain in effect as signed thereafter. §

ACCEPT CALIFORNIA APPRENTICESHIP INITIATIVE NEW AND INNOVATIVE GRANT FROM THE CALIFORNIA WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT DIVISION AWARDED TO MISSION COLLEGE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees authorize the administration to accept the California Apprenticeship Initiative New and Innovative Grant totaling $947,565 awarded to Mission College from the California Workforce Development Division to be distributed over three-year period (2024-2027).

Prepared By: Jeffrey Pallin Reviewed By: Lorrie Ranck and Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The California Workforce Development Division is awarding this three-year grant, $947,565 in total. The grant begins on August 1,2024 through December 31, 2027. Reference(s) In alignment with college planning, goals and action steps, Mission College successfully completed its 2023-2024 Mechatronics Apprenticeship Planning Grant. The College has registered an Apprenticeship Program for Hardware Technician with the Department of Labor and the California Division of Apprenticeship Standards. Applied Materials of Santa Clara is the employer sponsor. The College is embarking on a significant expansion of apprenticeship programs in Advanced Manufacturing, targeting manufacturing organizations that supply the Chip Manufacturing Industry. This aligns with the State Chancellor’s Office and Department of Labor initiatives around workforce development. The college has secured this new grant to expand the number of employers and apprentices in the Mission College Mechatronics Certificate Program. Employers will either hire college students as apprentices or enroll current employees as students. Background/Alternatives The college used the 2023-2024 $120,000 CAI Planning Grant to register for the Mechatronics Certificate Program with the Federal Department of Labor and the California Division of Apprenticeship Standards. This new grant gives the college four years of ongoing financial support to expand the college’s apprenticeship portfolio in Advanced Manufacturing. Mission College is working with both Workforce Development Boards in Santa Clara County, work2future of San José, and NOVA Works of Sunnyvale, who will assist in recruitment efforts from the community to enroll individuals seeking education and gainful employment to improve their lives. The College is working with several workforce intermediaries, including the Silicon Valley Leadership Group, the SEMI Foundation, the National Institute for Innovation and Technology, along with well-established firms specializing in assisting community colleges in Apprenticeship, Workforce Development, and employer engagement. The Biden Administration and the Departments of Labor and Defense have a keen interest in the semiconductor industry through the Chips and Science Act of 2022. The Mechatronics Certificate at Mission College is aligned with this initiative, as many employers in Silicon Valley working in the Chip Industry have been visiting Mission College lecture and lab classrooms to learn more about the college’s students and our program. Coordination Jeff Pallin, Dean of Workforce Innovation, oversees apprenticeship, internship, work experience, on-the-job training, and career/workforce industry partners, and manages the college’s two CTE Grants – Strong Workforce and Perkins. The Workforce Innovation team will be responsible for collecting all data necessary for timely and accurate reporting to the state, as the college is required to submit ongoing, regular reporting of progress in the successful application for all grant-supported programs to gauge the success of the effort and its impact on students and the region. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the grant will go into effect September 11, 2024, through December 31, 2027. Quarterly reports will be provided to the California Workforce Development Board with actual expenditures against the budget. §

Prepared By: Susan Victoria Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Fundin g Source / Fiscal Im p act Prior to adoption of its final budget, each state governmental entity must calculate its appropriations limit. The limit that was first calculated in 1979 may be modified each year by a factor composed of a change in student population (FTES) and the lesser of the change in the Consumer Price Index or California per capita income. West Valley-Mission CCD’s appropriations limit, as calculated according to the provisions of Article XIII-B, is much larger than the District’s actual appropriations. The District's 2024-2025 appropriations subject to limitation is $177,825,822. The District's appropriations limit, based on population growth/decline, has been determined to be $193,778,290 (worksheet attached). The District’s appropriations subject to the limit for 2024-2025 does not exceed the appropriations limit. Reference(s) Article XIII-B of the California Constitution, commonly referred to as the Gann Limit, was approved by the voters in 1979. Proposition 4 was passed by the voters of the state of California in November of 1979. Later, implementing legislation in the form of SB 1352 was passed in the California legislature and signed by the Governor. SB 1352 requires the following: "Government Code Section 7910: Each year the governing board of each local jurisdiction shall by resolution establish its appropriations limit for the following fiscal year pursuant to Article XIII-B at a regularly scheduled meeting or at a special meeting. Fifteen (15) days prior to such meeting, documentation used in the determination of the appropriations limit shall be available to the public. The determination of the appropriations limit is a legislative act." Back gr ound / Alternatives In accordance with the provisions of Government Code Section 7910, any action to set aside or annul the establishment of the appropriations limit must be filed with the State Chancellor within forty-five (45) days of the adoption of the resolution.
