EXECUTION OF DOCUMENTS ASSOCIATED TO THE SALE OF QTS - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve and execute estoppels and a non-disturbance attornment agreement related to the sale of QTS, 2805 &amp; 2807 Mission Blvd., Santa Clara, CA.

Prepared By: Christina Booth Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact associated with this item. Reference(s) Pursuant to the Ground Lease between the Mission-West Valley Land Corporation and Quality Investment Properties Santa Clara LLC (QIP Santa Clara) for the property located at 2805 and 2807 Mission College Blvd, the West Valley-Mission Community College District and Mission-West Valley Land Corporation boards are required to execute estoppels and a non-disturbance agreement for the benefit of QIP Santa Clara&rsquo;s lenders from time to time. Background/Alternatives The West Valley-Mission C ommunity College District (WVMCCD) owns 60 acres of real property located by Mission College. The WVMCCD has a master lease agreement with the Mission &ndash;West Valley Land Corporation which grants them the rights to sublease the land. The three ground leases of the Land Corporation includes Sobrato, QTS, and Mercado Shopping Center. QTS is being bought out by Blackstone Group. The tenant and ground lease remains with QIP. In order for QIP to complete the financing transactions involving the sale of QTS, the estoppel agreements and the non-disturbance attornment agreement need to be executed by the Mission-West Valley Land Corporation Board and the WVMCCD Board. The estoppels serve to memorialize, for the benefit of the lenders, the current status of the QIP Santa Clara Ground Lease and the Master Ground Lease between the District and the Land Corporation. The NDA protects the interests of QIP Santa Clara and the lenders in the event that there should occur a default under the Master Ground Lease or Ground Lease, respectively. Coordination This item has been reviewed by the legal counsel and the Associate Vice Chancellor of Finance and Administration. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the estoppels and non-disturbance attornment agreement will be executed and forwarded to legal counsel. §
