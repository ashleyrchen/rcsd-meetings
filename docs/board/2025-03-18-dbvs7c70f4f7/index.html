Prepared By: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed By: Cade Story-Yetto Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The district’s community support (Basic Aid) funds will be allocated to support the development and ongoing costs of the universal meal program, in alignment with the board’s commitment to removing hunger as a barrier to student success. These efforts include the elimination of tuition, parking, healthcare, textbook, childcare, and other miscellaneous fees. Data indicate that a substantial portion of the district’s students experience food insecurity; by offering no-cost meals, the district aims to ensure all students—especially those in greatest need—have reliable access to nutritious food. Coordination The chancellor and chief of staff drafted this resolution in coordination with the executive management team. Follow-up/Outcome Upon adoption, the chancellor will oversee the creation of a universal meal program in collaboration with district finance, relevant college departments, and Thomas Cuisine, the newly selected dining services provider. Each year, the district will reassess its fiscal position and present the Board of Trustees with a comprehensive report on the program’s effectiveness, along with any recommended enhancements or additional support. §
