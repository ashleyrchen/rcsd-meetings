CONSIDER APPROVAL AND AUTHORIZE THE EXPANSION OF THE MISSION EARLY COLLEGE HIGH SCHOOL (MECHS) PROGRAM TO NINTH GRADE Recommendation: That the Board of Trustees authorize the update to the MOU agreement with Santa Clara Unified School District (SCUSD) to allow for the expansion of the Mission Early College High School (MECHS) program to include SCUSD ninth grade students.

Prepared By: Omar Murillo and Viola Smith Reviewed By: Daniel Peck Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact If approved, Mission College would allow the Mission Early College High School (MECHS) program to expand to ninth grade. Currently, the MECHS program serves 10 th -12 th grade students on the Mission College campus. MECHS will continue to utilize the current facility spaces and classroom computers provided when the initial MOU was approved. The college agrees to continue providing appropriate access to classrooms, offices, and classroom computers to accommodate the MECHS program. It is important to note that the Mission College Academic Senate approved the expansion to ninth grade during the Spring 2021 semester. Funding for dedicated staffing and faculty and all direct program costs for MECHS is provided and managed by SCUSD. No permanent personnel would be assigned from Mission College or the District on a full-time or part-time basis. As part of the MECHS program, MECHS students enroll in Mission College classes at a rate of approximately two three-unit classes per student each term. Mission College receives normal fees and tuition (with the exception of parking and VTA smart pass fees) along with generation of FTES for the enrollment in Mission College classes. Currently, the program generates around 90 FTES over the primary Fall and Spring terms. Since Fall 2017, MECHS has served 284 students with 100 students being served in Fall 2019 and Spring 2020. Reference(s) This agreement was reviewed in consultation with college administration, the District Director of General Services, the Vice Chancellor for Administrative Services, and the District&rsquo;s Attorney, Liebert Cassidy Whitmore. Background/Alternatives MECHS serves a student population that is rich in diversity academically, culturally, and socio-economically, which provides MECHS students with a rich educational experience. MECHS encourages the participation of students who are considered at-risk, low-income, underrepresented ethnic minorities, or first generation to attend college, and believe our unique design helps to decrease the high school dropout rate while increasing student access to postsecondary education. The MECHS program model further allows SCUSD to provide a more comprehensive set of services for students within the program which are legislatively not allowed within the existing model. Students will be able to pursue specific areas of study leading to a higher likelihood of completion of a Mission College certificate or degree simultaneous to completion of high school graduation requirements. Finally, MECHS removes logistical and structural challenges within SCUSD, making administration of the program more effective and efficient. SCUSD is committed to providing the program with more resources and has already assigned a dedicated Vice Principal and additional staffing. Amending the current MOU to included ninth grade students will have a positive impact on the college readiness of MECHS students and will strengthen the relationship between Mission College and SCUSD. Both parties look forward to continuing this important work in an effort to impact students&rsquo; lives for the better by affording them direct access to a post-secondary education, and setting them on a trajectory that improves and benefits them and their families for generations to come. Coordination The operational and instructional aspects of the MOU agreement will continue to be monitored by the Vice President of Student Services, in coordination with the Vice President of Administrative Services and Vice President of Instruction. The Vice President of Student Services will continue to serve as the formal liaison to SCUSD for MECHS and the administration of Mission College would continue discussion and further coordination with the administration of SCUSD. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, Mission College will notify SCUSD of the outcome. The SCUSD Board will be voting on the MOU update at their June 2021 board meeting. A full review of the impact for students, faculty, and staff along with the fiscal impact to the college will be conducted at the conclusion of each academic year to identify needed adjustments or requests for changes to the agreement. §

EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY FUND MULTIPLE METHOD ALLOCATION MODEL CERTIFICATION FORM APPROVAL, FISCAL YEAR 2020-2021 - Recommendation: That the Board of Trustees adopt the Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) Fund Multiple Method Allocation Model Certification Report.

Prepared By: Ryan Ng Reviewed By: Eric Ramones Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Funds for the continuation of District efforts to promote EEO and diversity will be provided to the District by the State Chancellor&rsquo;s Office pursuant to the provisions set forth below. Pending the State Chancellor&rsquo;s Office approval of the West Valley-Mission Community College District model certification form, the District&rsquo;s allocation from the State is projected to be comparable to the allocation of 2020-2021, in the tens of thousands of dollars. Reference(s) Title 5 regulations, section 53000 et seq.; WVMCCD BPs 3410, 3420, 3430; Education Code 87100 et seq.; and Board of Governors Standing Order 374 Background/Alternatives The District Board of Trustees revised and approved the current District EEO Plan at its regularly scheduled meeting on May 2, 2017. Under the State Chancellor&rsquo;s authority to &ldquo;represent the Board of Governors in monitoring the use of the Equal Employment Opportunity Fund for the purpose of promoting equal employment opportunities in hiring and promotion at community college districts,&rdquo; the Chancellor&rsquo;s Office implemented a new fund allocation model a few years ago. The Multiple Method allocation model was developed by the State Chancellor&#39;s Office&#39;s longstanding Equal Employment Opportunity and Diversity Advisory Committee and the CCCCO&rsquo;s Legal Division Office over the course of six months in 2016-17. The West Valley-Mission CCD Associate Vice Chancellor of Human Resources is a member of the State Chancellor&rsquo;s EEO Diversity Advisory Committee. Purpose In the past, districts received money from the EEO Fund, which was allocated proportionally to the FTES of each district each year. The purpose of implementing a new model was to increase compliance with legal EEO requirements and provide our students with the educational benefits of a diverse workforce. The State Chancellor&rsquo;s Office&#39;s Equal Employment Opportunity and Diversity Advisory Committee and the Legal Affairs Division identified nine best-practice areas for &ldquo;success in promoting&rdquo; EEO. These nine areas serve as the &ldquo;multiple methods&rdquo; for allocating the EEO Fund: District&rsquo;s EEO Advisory Committee and EEO Plan Pre-Hiring Board policies and adopted resolutions Incentives for hard-to-hire areas/disciplines Focused outreach and publications Hiring Procedures for addressing diversity throughout hiring steps and levels Consistent and ongoing training for hiring committees Post-Hiring Professional development focused on diversity Diversity incorporated into criteria for employee evaluation and tenure review Grow-Your-Own programs Multiple-Method Allocation Model According to the model, a district&rsquo;s EEO Diversity advisory committee, chief human resources officer, chief executive officer, and governing board must certify that the district meets the requirements. The form must be submitted to the California Community Colleges Chancellor&rsquo;s Office, Legal Affairs Division by June 1 of each year. Multiple Method 1: Role of District EEO Advisory Committee and EEO Plan Under the Multiple Method allocation model, districts must minimally have an operational EEO Advisory Committee and an updated EEO Plan in order to qualify for receipt of the EEO Funds (Multiple Method 1). Districts are required to submit a board-adopted EEO Plan every three years to the Chancellor&rsquo;s Office. (Title 5, Section 53003). Districts are also required to establish an EEO Advisory Committee to assist in developing and implementing the EEO Plan. (Title 5, Section 53005). Multiple Methods 2-9: To receive funding for a particular year&rsquo;s allocation amount, districts are also required to meet a minimum of six of the remaining eight Multiple Methods. Each district that meets the requirements of the Multiple Method allocation model will receive diversity funding for use in the upcoming year. Previously, the fund allocation has been approximately $50,000. Use of the EEO Funds Under Education Code, Section 87108 and T itle 5, Section 53030, EEO Funds received by the districts may be used for the following: Outreach and recruitment; In-service training on equal employment opportunity; Accommodations for applicants and employees with disabilities pursuant to Title 5, Section 53025; Activities designed to encourage students to become qualified for, and seek, employment as community college faculty or administrators; and O ther activities to promote equal employment opportunity. Resources The certification form requires districts to report the various activities that they are implementing or have implemented to promote EEO for each of the nine multiple methods. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval, the Board President will sign the certification report on behalf of the Board, and the Associate Vice Chancellor of Human Resources will promptly submit the fully completed signature page and report form to the State Chancellor&rsquo;s Office in compliance with the deadline of June 1, 2021. §
