Assembly Bill 2449 Remote Teleconference Attendance Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may be eligible to attend a Board of Education meeting remotely with Just Cause or with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance. To ensure compliance with the notice requirements of AB 2449, the Board will maintain an AB 2449 standing agenda item for each meeting. Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Just Cause submissions received for the current Board meeting. Consider and take action on Emergency Circumstance requests, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance and Board approval. Emergency Circumstance means a physical or family medical emergency that prevents the trustee from attending in person. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. Disclosure of individuals present at remote locations, if any, pursuant to AB 2449 . A trustee attending a Board meeting remotely under AB 2449 must publicly disclose whether any other individuals 18 years of age or older are present in the room at the remote location with the trustee, and the general nature of the members relationship with any such individuals. Any trustee attending the current Board meeting remotely under 2449 will make this public disclosure.

CONSIDER APPROVAL OF CURRICULUM CHANGES AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees approve the curriculum changes at West Valley College as presented.

Prepared By: Kristen Jackanich, Luis Portillo, Doonu Barife, Chris Dyer and Joshua Moon-Johnson Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The general fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. These costs are offset, to varying degrees, by apportionment received for credit-based instruction and other associated fees. Reference(s) Curriculum development and recommendation is an ongoing activity overseen by the West Valley College Academic Senate, and its subordinate curriculum committee, and the office of instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the West Valley College program review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per district policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and recommended by the curriculum committee and the articulation officer. Each of the curriculum changes and the foregoing recommendations has been further reviewed by the chief instructional officer, the college president, and the district chancellor. The office of instruction further certifies that the programs and courses described herein satisfy all applicable requirements of Title 5 and have also been submitted to the Associated Students Government of West Valley College. Degrees developed in compliance with state-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the district chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees, and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between West Valley College and Mission College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the college catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the college catalog and from the state curriculum inventory. §

APPROVAL OF MISSION COLLEGE TO ATTEND THE ANNUAL SHPE SOCIETY OF HISPANIC PROFESSIONAL ENGINEERS CONFERENCE - Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees approve up to ten (10) student leaders, one (1) student services administrator, and up to one (1) classified staff from the sponsoring departments to attend the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE) National Conference in Anaheim, CA from October 30, 2024 to November 3, 2024.

Prepared By: EDRINA RASHIDI & INEZ BARRAGAN Reviewed By: SEHER AWAN Approved By: BRADLEY DAVIS Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Below is the estimated cost of attendance for the SHPE National Conference for twelve attendees: Registration fees: $3,700 Airfare: $8,000 Ground transportation: $400 Lodging: $5,000 Meals: $3,840* Membership: $230 Total $21,170 Funding will be provided by the Mission College MESA Program, with support from the HSI-STEM Grant in the case non-MESA qualified students are selected to attend. The program budgets have funding allocated specifically for qualifying student travel. Reference(s) AP6311 states: “Prior Board approval must be obtained for any travel at District expense if the total travel expense will exceed $10,000 for any one event or purpose, without regard to the number of individuals traveling." Background/Alternatives MESA and HSI-STEM are dedicated to supporting Latinx, first-generation, and/or low-income students towards graduation, transfer, and success with the goal of creating tomorrow’s doctors, engineers and scientists. The programs have identified several goals to increase enrollment and retention within the programs for 2024-2025, including re-integrating our students into the greater STEM community. Building a team of transfer-pathway STEM scholars, specifically focusing on support for Latinx STEM students, will better equip our student leaders for success. This also supports the achievement of Mission College equity goals aligned with increasing internship attainment and transfer rates for Latinx students. The SHPE Conference is the largest student and professional conference dedicated to elevating Latinx Engineers in the country. Over the course of the conference, Mission College students will be immersed in cutting-edge STEM technology, professional development sessions, motivational keynote speakers, and possibly the largest engineering career fair dedicated to Latinx students and early professionals. The SHPE Conference also includes skill-based competitions where students engage in activities such as multi-day hackathons where they can gain both scholarships and internships. Mission College students successfully achieved this in 2017 when they received 1 st place in the SHPE National Hackathon, bringing recognition to Mission College. By attending SHPE, Mission College’s Latinx and/or first-generation college student STEM majors will gain tools to successfully progress towards their ultimate goals of professional development, internships, transfer, baccalaureate degree completion, graduate school, and successfully finding a job. Coordination The co-coordinators for this opportunity are Edrina Rashidi and Inez Barragan. The co-coordinators will create an application for students to apply for the SHPE conference and will also hold a pre-orientation for selected students. We have created a program budget and are awaiting board approval to recruit students and proceed with securing conference registration, airfare and other expenses. This coordination and support will continue, pending Board approval, with Edrina Rashidi serving as chaperone for the event. Follow-up/Outcome On approval of this agenda item, the MESA and HSI-STEM offices will begin to make travel arrangements; potential scholar participants will be notified that a total of ten (10) Mission College student leaders and up to (2) chaperones have been approved to attend the SHPE National Conference in Anaheim, CA from October 30-November 3, 2024. §

Consider Selection of a Representative and Alternate from West Valley College to Serve on the Steering Committee of the South Bay Consortium for Adult Education - Recommended Action: In accordance with the Adult Education Program (formerly Adult Education Block Grant), it is the recommendation of the Chancellor that the Board of Trustees appoint Mark Manrose from West Valley College as the representative to the Steering Committee of the South Bay Consortium for Adult Education (SBCAE) and Eric Grabiel as alternate to that same committee.

Prepared By: Joshua Moon-Johnson Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Funds for activities associated with South Bay Consortium for Adult Education (SBCAE) are provided through the California Adult Education Program (CAEP), in accordance with AB 104. For fiscal year 2024/2025, West Valley College will receive $276,146. Reference(s) The applicable authority and provisions of this contract are AB 104 and the California Community College Chancellor's Office. Background/Alternatives In June 2015, through AB 104 the Governor proposed, and the Legislature approved, the Adult Education Block Grant , which was later renamed the California Adult Education Program. AB - 104 requi res that all member institutions participate in the decision-making process through public meetings with formal representatives from each member institution. In addition, each institution’s governing board must approve their representatives. On October 20, 2015, the West Valley-Mission Community College District Board of Trustees appointed Mae Conroy as the West Valley College representative to the Steering Committee for the SBCAE. In January 2020, the Board of Trustees approved Brad Weisberg to assume the role . Eric Grabiel, then Director of the School of Continuing Education, subsequently assumed the role in May 2020. In June 2024, Mark Manrose assumed the role of Director of the School of Continuing Education and will, upon approval by the WVMCCD Board of Trustees, become the College's primary representative. Mr. Manrose’s responsibilities include attending required meetings and planning sessions, as well as participating in the decision-making process as appropriate. Eric Grabiel will serve as the College's alternate representative, working directly with Mark Manrose, and will take an active part in the consortium’s relationship with the college. Coordination The California Adult Education Program mandates that each of the consortia steering committee members be approved by their respective Board of Trustees. Furthermore, the CAEP also requires representation from each college rather than each district. Upon the approval of the Board of Trustees, Mark Manrose (West Valley College) will serve as the primary representative whereas Eric Grabiel (West Valley College) will serve as the College's alternate representative to support the implementation of the CAEP on behalf of the WVMCCD. Follow-up/Outcome This appointment will ensure that West Valley College has consistent representation on the Steering Committee and meets our obligation as a member of the SBCAE. §

CONSIDER APPROVAL FOR CONTRACTING OF ROOFING REPAIR WORK WITH PIONEER CONTRACTORS, INC. – Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the construction contract agreement with Pioneer Contractors, Inc. in the amount of $1,672,500 for the roofing repair work on the Cilker Building at West Valley College.

Prepared By: Jennifer Mahato Reviewed By: Michael Robins and Terrance DeGray Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The funding for this project comes from Community Supported Funds. Reference(s) California Public Contact Code Section 20652 allows the governing board of any community college district, without advertising for bids, to acquire equipment, materials, and supplies by utilizing an existing contract from another public corporation or agency. OMNIA Partners, formerly U.S. Communities, assists Participating Public Agencies to reduce the cost of purchased goods through strategic sourcing that combines the volumes and the purchasing power of public agencies nationwide. This is accomplished through an award of competitively solicited contracts for high quality products and services by large and well recognized public agencies (herein "Lead Public Agencies"). The contracts provide for use by not only the respective Lead Public Agency, but also by other Participating Public Agencies. Utilizing this Agreement will benefit the district in achieving added price savings of more than $145,000.00 through volume discounting. Therefore, this purchase will be procured pursuant to OMNIA Partners Contract R230404, currently active through October 31, 2026. Background/Alternatives The roofing assessment completed in the fall of 2023 showed that the Cilker Building had a poor rating for its roofing condition. During the winter of 2023/2024, water intrusion was observed due to heavy rains. Facilities staff evaluated options but did not include the building in the districtwide roofing projects because of the unknown conditions of the roofing underlayment. The existing roof on the Cilker Building has multiple failures due to incompatible systems and separate contract agreements for additions and repairs over the years, including HVAC systems and roof screens. During investigations to resolve the water issues, the Facilities staff entered into an agreement to perform a moisture study in the spring of 2024 to determine the extent of the damage and assess the roof underlayment materials to determine the best investment for the roof. Based on the results of the moisture study, only about 300 square feet of the roofing underlayment were damaged and needed replacement. Facilities staff worked with Weatherproofing Technologies Inc. to have multiple contractors review the roof and provide proposals in accordance with the Omnia agreement. The lowest proposal was received from Pioneer Contractors, Inc. to provide materials, equipment, and labor to complete the work. The damaged areas of flashing, roofing underlayment, and penetration flashings will be addressed, and a fluid-applied coating will be placed across the entire roof. Additionally, safety barriers will be installed at skylights and areas of the roof that have potential fall hazards for operations staff who access the roof to maintain the building systems. Upon completion of the work, the roof warranty and the life of the roof will be extended for 20 years with preventative maintenance on years 2, 5, 10 and 15. Coordination This item includes the work of the vice chancellor of facilities development and operations, the executive director of general services, and the executive director of facilities maintenance and operations. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, a contract will be issued to Pioneer Contractors, Inc. in the amount of $1,672,500.00 for the District Roofing project on the Cilker Building at West Valley College. §

13.1 · Action · Board

Second Reading - Board Policy 5500 on Standards Of Conduct. Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct a second reading of Board Policy 5500 on Standards of Conduct, close public comment, and adopt the proposed revisions.

Prepared By: Monica Garcia and Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed By: Eric Ramones Approved By: Bradley J. Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) ACCJC Accreditation Standards I.C.8 and 10; Education Code Sections 66300, 66301 , and 76120 Background/Alternatives This policy language was provided by the Community College League of California’s (CCLC) Policy & Procedure Service in partnership with the law firm of Atkinson, Andelson, Loya, Rudd and Romo. This policy is being updated to incorporate recent changes in California Education Code and Title IX changes. Major changes include: Title IX Exception: A notable addition is the exception for conduct constituting sexual harassment under Title IX. Such cases are now addressed separately under AP 3433 and AP 3434, rather than AP 5520. Permission from District Employee: The requirement for obtaining written permission to possess certain objects from the District Police and Vice President of Student Services is replaced with obtaining permission from any District employee, concurred by the college president. Expanded Definitions: The revised statement expands the definitions of prohibited behaviors to include sexual assault, sexual exploitation, harassment based on additional categories (disability, gender identity, gender expression, nationality, race or ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation), and bullying, including cyberbullying. Inclusion of Intimidating Conduct: Intimidating conduct or bullying through various means, including direct physical contact, verbal assaults, social isolation, manipulation, and cyberbullying, is explicitly addressed. Clarification on Smoking: The smoking prohibition is maintained but specifies that it is in areas prohibited by law or college/District regulations. Inclusion of Sexual Assault: Sexual assault is explicitly mentioned as a separate category of misconduct. Clarification of Reporting: The reporting of misconduct involving injury or death now specifies the involvement of District personnel. These changes enhance the clarity, specificity, and inclusivity of the conduct that may lead to disciplinary action while also addressing Title IX cases separately. Coordination The policy went through the District’s participatory governance process and was reviewed by the Academic and Classified Senates and College Councils of both Colleges prior to review by District Council on May 6 and July 8, 2024. Follow-up/Outcome At the August 20, 2024, Board of Trustees meeting, approval of Board Policy 5500 on Standards Of Conduct and the conclusion of the public comment period will be considered on the agenda. If adopted, this policy will be publicly available on the WVMCCD website. Following its refinement by the District Vice President Council and subsequent approval by the Chancellor, the related Administrative Procedure will be incorporated into the websites and catalogs of both Mission College and West Valley College. §
