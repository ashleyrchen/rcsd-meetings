Special Board of Trustees Meeting, Enrollment Discussion (Saturday, May 21, 2022) Considered unapproved until the June 21, 2022 Board of Trustees Meeting Generated by Rebecca Alvarez on Monday, May 23, 2022 The Public Session Meeting was called to order at 10:00 AM Roll Call Susan Fish, Robert Owens, Karl Watanabe, Jack Lucas, Anne Kepner, Adrienne Grey and Randi Kinman Student Trustees: Nicholas Hsieh, Gabrielle Wilson Absent: Trustee Susan Fish 1. Opening Items Pledge of Allegiance The pledge of allegiance was recited. 2. Regular Order of the Agenda Chancellor Davis welcomed all and introduced the presentation. 2.1 Mission College and West Valley College - Enrollment Growth Strategies: Norma Ambriz-Galaviz, Interim President, Mission College and Stephanie Kashima, President, West Valley College Presidents Kashima and Ambriz-Galaviz presented and covered the following highlights while showing a PowerPoint presentation: 2022-2024 Strategies Embedding college advisors - Presented by: Omar Murillo and Joe McDevitt Middle school partnerships - Presented by: Omar Murillo and Joe McDevitt Older adult expansion - Presented by: Danny Nguyen and Marilyn Morikang Job goal focus - high demand/high wage careers - Presented by: Stacy Gleixner Identifying untapped populations (some college/no degree; leavers) - Presented by: Stephanie Kashima and Norman Ambriz-Galaviz Direct marketing/outreach to "great resignation" - Presented by Tina Vossugh Marketing examples were shown by Tina Vossugh 2.2 Trustee Comments: Karl Watanabe stated that when he enrolled in classes, he found that he was paying miscellaneous fees. Stephanie stated that the goal is to make attending classes as accessible as possible. In addition, he stated that he would be interested in taking more classes in the near future, however, would like a vast variety to choose from. Lastly, he asked how we will complete with the Santa Clara Senior Center. Danny Nguyen stated that part of their goal is re-branding as many folks are not aware that taking a class could enrich their lives. Norma and Danny stated that they would like to complement what the Senior Centers are already doing. Trustee Grey thanked the group for the presentation, she asked the following questions: She requested the script that went along with the presentation. This will be sent. Enrollment increase estimates, she asked if this had been done. A: It was been mapped out as broad estimates, however yet to do the analysis described in her question. Will the demographic consultants consider all different types of buckets, such as newly retirees, trade, etc? A: At the state-wide level, apprenticeship is now one of the largest trends. The goal is to catch them early to draft the curriculum. The stigma on working adults in going back to school - looking at our own college websites to see how welcoming they are to adult learners. Mission College looks good in this regard, less so with West Valley. Trustee Kinman: Saratoga and Santa Clara were mentioned as touch points for service areas. She wanted to ensure that all communities are being targeted, including the service area that she represents. A: Currently, the Saratoga area is planning on expanding its service area. As far as the feeder schools, will students be encouraged to attend one campus or another? Joe McDevitt stated that it is a challenge to encourage them to attend West Valley College. Relationships are key in this. There are some students who do attend from outside of the feeder schools. We are currently reaching out to Regarding the demographic breakdown - how will it be broken down? A: It will look at our current students and service area in general and all types of categories. Chancellor Davis stated that he wants to follow up with Trustee Kinman directly in regard to the transportation routes and how we think more intentionally about this moving forward. Trustee Lucas thanked the group for the presentation, it is a good reminder of the aggressive effort being taken: In regard to light rail. What can the Board do to help in the pursuit to increase enrollment? A: Already have helped by approving funding for the demographer with the land corp support. The flexibility of thought as we will have to change the status quo. What can be done individually? College leadership will reach out to the board as an incredible resource. As they need information before they craft their program. Trustee Owens stated that his question proceeds the presentation. Why are we currently losing FTES that has been going on for 5-6 years. He does not believe it is due to the pandemic. Perhaps, not realizing the aging population, cost of living, such as gas. There is an increase in offsite learning. He believes we need to look toward maintenance rather than growth. He believes there is an opportunity here. Trustee Grey asked about services for parenting adults. This is currently being looked at. Numerous conversations are taking place around the structure of the childcare center. Trustee Kinman stated that the childcare centers must be accommodating to the student-parent needs. Trustee Kinman The only time that she used to hear from both campuses was when it was bond season, she would like to ensure that folks hear from the colleges other than that time of year. The demographer will help to create a service area address list. Trustee Kinman thanked the group for the work that went into this. Trustee Kepner thanked the group for the presentation and offered her support moving forward. This is the beginning of an ongoing conversation. 2.3 Oral Communications from the Public on Items on the Agenda. Pursuant to Government Code 54954.3(b) Oral Communication is limited during special meetings to items listed on the published agenda. Public Comment was made from: Howard Randall, Aram Shepherd, Gretchen Ehlers, Leigh Burrill, and Ellen Yu Costa 3. Meeting Closing 3.1 Adjournment The meeting was adjourned at 12:12 PM

Attendees may either request to speak by raising their hand at the meeting or via the zoom raise hand feature. Please include the following information in your public comment request: SUBJECT: COMMENTS FOR THE MEETING DATE OF: TOPIC: __________________________ NAME: ___________________________________________________ TITLE: ___________________________________________________ ORGANIZATION (optional): ___________________________________ ADDRESS (optional): ________________________________________ How to address the Board (Speaking): When it is your turn to address the Board, the Board Clerk will call your name based on the information provided above in the Public Comment Request. Please note: Per Board Policy 2345, three minutes may be allotted to each speaker with a maximum of 30 minutes per subject. No action or discussion shall be undertaken on any public comments except that members of the Board or its staff may briefly respond to or ask a question for clarification regarding statements made or questions posed by persons addressing the Board under this agenda item.
