County Health also noted that they are moving away from their current focus on PCR testing results, due to the public&rsquo;s increased use of at home Antigen tests. For this reason, the County will be focusing on wastewater testing results to predict Covid levels in the community more accurately. For the time being, Chief Rolen will continue to report both the State and County PCR figures, but I wanted to introduce the wastewater testing component which will likely be incorporated in future reports. The state&rsquo;s 7-day positive case rate is 5,745. In Santa Clara County, the 7-day rolling average is 281, and this number has been relatively stable for the last month. Omicron variants continue to be the most dominant strains comprising approximately 90% of all cases. There are no new state or county health orders to report. During today&rsquo;s County Health meeting it was mentioned that if California&rsquo;s Covid -19 State of Emergency expires on February 28 th as expected, the County has no plans to make any significant changes to their recommendations or requirements. CONSIDER APPROVAL OF CURRICULUM CHANGES AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the curriculum changes at West Valley College as presented.

Prepared By: Kristen Jackanich, Stacy Gleixner, Chris Dyer, Luis Portillo and Doonu Barife Reviewed By: Stephanie Kashima Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The general fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. These costs are offset, to varying degrees, by apportionment received for credit-based instruction and other associated fees. Reference(s) Curriculum development and recommendation is an ongoing activity overseen by the West Valley College Academic Senate, and its subordinate curriculum committee, and the office of instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the West Valley College program review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per district policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and recommended by the curriculum committee and the articulation officer. Each of the curriculum changes and the foregoing recommendations has been further reviewed by the chief instructional officer, the college president, and the district chancellor. The office of instruction further certifies that the programs and courses described herein satisfy all applicable requirements of Title 5 and have also been submitted to the Associated Students Government of West Valley College. Degrees developed in compliance with state-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the district chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees, and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between West Valley College and Mission College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the college catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the college catalog and from the state curriculum inventory. §

These classes will be in addition to course sections offered on the West Valley College campus and will generate full-time equivalent students (FTES). Reference(s) Education Code 76004 (Assembly Bill 288) establishes the College and Career Access Pathways Act to authorize California Community College districts to enter into formal partnership agreements with local school districts. The goal is to create easier pathways from high school to community college and to offer or expand dual enrollment opportunities for under-represented higher education students or for students who may not be college bound. This partnership aligns with the goals of West Valley College&#39;s Educational and Facilities Master Plan and its Student Equity Plan related to improving the student pathways to degrees and transfer, strengthening collaborative partnerships with K-12 institutions, and increasing access and success for underrepresented groups. On October 4, 2019, the Governor signed Assembly Bill 30 and Senate Bill 5861 both of which amended the law in the same way to make pupil participation in the College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) program and the CCAP agreement process less burdensome for colleges. These changes include: The elimination of the requirement that each district present the proposed CCAP agreements to their respective governing boards twice. Each district is now only required to present the proposed CCAP agreement once to their respective governing boards. This agreement has been reviewed by the West Valley College president, West Valley College vice president of academic affairs, West Valley College vice provost, West Valley College vice President of student services, diversity, and inclusion, superintendent of the Los Gatos-Saratoga Union High School District and the assistant superintendent of the Los Gatos-Saratoga Union High School District. This CCAP template has been reviewed by the District&rsquo;s legal counsel, Liebert Cassidy Whitmore. This agreement is also being presented to the LGSUHSD Board and may undergo changes before it is returned. Background/Alternatives Students who complete college credit while enrolled in high school, are more likely to earn high school diplomas, to enroll in community colleges and four-year colleges, to attend post-secondary education on a full-time basis, and to complete degrees in those institutions, than students without these experiences. LGSUHSD is a public-school district serving grades 9-12 with students whose residences are located within the regional service area of WVMCCD and these students deserve an opportunity to participate in an accelerated process that can further ensure academic success. This CCAP Agreement allows students to pursue this dual enrollment opportunity. Courses offered as part of this CCAP Agreement shall be community college courses acceptable towards a career technical education credential or certificate, or preparation for transfer, or appropriate to improve high school graduation rates or help high school pupils achieve college and career readiness. Coordination The operational and instructional aspects of this agreement will be administered and monitored by the West Valley College, vice provost. An educational administrator to be specified in the appendix to the CCAP agreement will serve as point of contact to facilitate coordination and cooperation between the college and LGSUHSD, in conformity with WVMCCD policies and standards. Follow-up/Outcome After the proposed Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Dual Enrollment Partnership Agreement is approved by the WVMCCD Board of Trustees and the LGSUHSD Board, West Valley College will submit the agreement to the CCC Chancellor&rsquo;s Office for review and coordinate with LGSUHSD staff to implement the pathway in preparation for the next cohort in the fall 2023 semester . §

SILICON VALLEY HIGHER EDUCATION ANCHOR NETWORK COLLABORATION - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the awarding of one-time grant funds from the Silicon Valley Community Foundation as a result of West Valley College and Mission College’s membership with the Silicon Valley Higher Education Anchor Network.

Prepared By: VIRGINIA MARQUEZ, OMAR MURILLO, STACY NOJIMA, RENEE PAQUIER & KEN SONGCO Reviewed By: SEHER AWAN AND STEPHANIE KASHIMA Approved By: BRADLEY DAVIS Funding Source/Fiscal Impact West Valley College and Mission College will each receive a one-time grant of $30,000. The Silicon Valley Community Foundation is currently fundraising to be able to offer an additional grant to subsidize baseline data collection and analysis costs for member institutions in Spring of 2023. Reference(s) Silicon Valley Higher Education Anchor Network Parameters: A $30,000 grant will be awarded to each member institution to subsidize BIPOC (Black, Indigenous, People of Color) engagement and staffing participation. Background/Alternatives The vision of the Silicon Valley Higher Education Anchor Network is as follows: This is a collective collaboration to learn about and put into practice a greater number of institution-level policies and practices that center equitable economic development in Silicon Valley (specifically San Mateo and Santa Clara Counties in California) using the anchor mission framework. The anchor mission is defined as &ldquo;a commitment to intentionally apply an institution&rsquo;s place-based economic power and human capital in partnership with community to mutually benefit the long-term well-being of both.&rdquo; With the Silicon Valley Community Foundation and Stanford Impact Labs acting as backbone organizations for this network, West Valley College and Mission College have joined the inaugural Silicon Valley Higher Education Anchor Network to discuss and establish a shared vision for the role of higher education institutions in cultivating equitable economic prosperity in San Mateo and Santa Clara counties. The Network will bring together regional public and private colleges and universities to deepen their anchor institution commitments to: a. Establish a shared vision for equitable economic prosperity in Silicon Valley. b. Incorporate a community engagement process with students, staff, and faculty who identify as Black, Indigenous, and/or People of Color in order to identify initial priorities. c. Carry out baseline data collection to inform 1-3 policy and/or practice change objectives at each institution. d. Develop and pilot an initial round of anchor commitments. The process will unfold over a series of full-network in-person and virtual convenings and self-organized working groups to take deep dives on specific areas of interest and action such as Procurement, Hiring and Leadership Development, Local Infrastructure, Sound Community Investment, and Healthy Community Residents. The plan is for this work to take place between January 2023 and May 2024. Anchor Network Member Organizations include De Anza College, Foothill College, Gavilan College, Menlo College, Mission College, Santa Clara University, San Jose City College, San Jose State University, Stanford University, and West Valley College. Coordination At West Valley College, interim vice president Paquier will act as the leadership point of contact. Virginia Marquez and Stacy Nojima will act as the grant administration and project manager contacts. At Mission College, vice president Murillo will act as the leadership point of contact. Ken Songco will act as grant administration and project manager contact. Each college will form a core team of faculty, staff, and administrators in Spring 2023 to support this project on their respective campuses. Follow-up/Outcome Each college will identify and implement a project that is related to &ldquo;addressing the racial wealth divide.&rdquo; These projects will be designed to both enhance and propel the equity/anti-racism work at West Valley College and Mission College. Metrics and outcomes will be incorporated into the design of each of the college&rsquo;s projects to monitor/assess activities while aligning with the strategic goals of West Valley College and Mission College. The Silicon Valley Community Foundation and Stanford Impact Labs have designed this grant as a fully flexible, unrestricted set of resources to support each Network member institution to be able to participate in the activities and objectives outlined in the Silicon Valley Higher Education Anchor Network MOU. This means that there are no formal reporting or timeline requirements for the grant, and it will be at each college&rsquo;s discretion when and how to specifically utilize funds. The ethos here is to practice power sharing and a trust-based approach among member institutions as the Network sets up its infrastructure to tackle the racial wealth divide and critically examine its intersection with higher education in San Mateo and Santa Clara counties. §

Prepared By: Monica Garcia Reviewed By: Eric Ramones Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The 2022-2023 Budget included funding for expenditures for the WVMCEA as provided in the proposed agreement. Reference(s) Collective Bargaining Agreement between WVMCEA and WVMCCD, July 1, 2021 - June 30, 2024, and Board Policy 7140: Collective Bargaining. Background/Alternatives The District and WVMCEA entered into successor negotiations in December 2022. The District and WVMCEA had sunshined and held public hearings on successor proposals at the Board of Trustees meetings of October 18, November 15, and December 6, 2022. On December 13, 2022, WVMCEA and the District successfully concluded successor negotiations for 2022-2023. Summary of the proposed agreement: Article 9 – Holidays Added language to clarify Juneteenth as an official District holiday. Added language to clarify scheduled holidays will be taken as a full day off work. Article 16 – Health and Welfare Benefits Increased benefits cap from $12,480 to $13,734 for single coverage, $23,580 to $26,027 for 2 party coverage, and $30,240 to $33,402 for 3 or more coverage . Removed specific plan information for dental coverage. Coordination The Associate Vice Chancellor of Human Resources and the negotiations team: HR Director, HR Supervisor, HR Specialists, and the Executive Assistant to the Associate Vice Chancellor, worked with WVMCEA to achieve this tentative agreement. WVMCEA leadership informed the District that the membership approved the tentative agreement on January 6, 2023. Follow-up/Outcome Human Resources will coordinate with the WVMCEA, Payroll, and District and College management to implement and administer all provisions of the agreement. §
