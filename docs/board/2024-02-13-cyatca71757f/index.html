Reference(s) Curriculum development and recommendation is an ongoing activity overseen by the West Valley College Academic Senate, and its curriculum committee, and the office of instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Likewise, it is regulated through Title 5 and WVMCCD District Policy 4020. Background/Alternatives As per district policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and recommended by the curriculum committee and the articulation officer. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between West Valley College and Mission College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. Courses which are archived will be removed from the college catalog; courses which are deleted will be removed both from the college catalog and from the state curriculum inventory. §

14.1 · Action · Board

Filling Vacancy on Board of Trustees – Area 5. The Board will consider whether to fill the seat on the Board of Trustees vacated due to the passing of Trustee Jack Lucas (Area 5) by provisional appointment or by special election. In the event that the Board chooses to fill the seat by provisional appointment, the Board may also adopt an appointment process and schedule.

Prepared By: Cade Story-Yetto and Rebecca Alvarez Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The estimated cost of a special election for Trustee Area 5, based on the number of registered voters, is approximately $600,000. The estimated cost of a provisional appointment for Trustee Area 5 is under $5,000. Each year, the District has budgeted $200,000 for special elections. Reference(s) California Education Code sections 5091 et seq. and BP 2110 set forth the procedures to fill a Board vacancy. Education Code 72103 stipulates who is eligible to serve as an elected or appointed member of a governing board of a school district. Background/Alternatives A vacancy has been created on the West Valley-Mission Community College District Board of Trustees due to the passing of Trustee Jack Lucas on January 24, 2024. Per the Education Code, the Board has 60 days from the effective date of the vacancy (January 24, 2024) to either call for a special election or make a provisional appointment. Should the board elect to hold a special election, the Santa Clara County Department of Elections has indicated that a Resolution must be submitted to their office by April 24, 2024, and the special election would occur on August 27, 2024. A new Board member would, therefore, not be seated until the next regularly scheduled meeting following certification of the election. A candidate elected via special election would remain in role to the end of the previous Trustee's term, ending December, 2026. Should the board decide to make a provisional appointment, residents of Trustee Area 5 who wish to be considered, will be invited to fill out a standard application form, providing their resume, two letters of recommendation, a list of professional references, and a personal statement expressing their reasons for seeking the board seat. This application process will be kept open for a period of 3 weeks. A special meeting will then be held with a committee of no less than a quorum of the Board to screen applications and/or interview potential candidates. The Board will then publicly appoint the selected candidate at the regular March 2024 Board of Trustees meeting. See Proposed Timeline for Provisional Appointment below. Proposed Timeline for Provisional Appointment: February 13, 2024 - Regular Governing Board meeting — Board to discuss and approve the vacancy timeline and process; determine whether to make a provisional appointment or call a special election. February 14, 2024 - District to notify residents of Trustee Area 5 of the vacancy via announcement in a newspaper of general circulation within the District, posting on the District website, and other means and methods to recruit a robust and diverse applicant pool. March 9, 2024 - Special Governing Board meeting — Board to screen applications and/or interview the finalists in public forum and make a final selection (copies of applicants’ documents available at the meeting). The Board may elect to hold an additional special Governing Board meeting on March 14, 2024, for purposes of completing the interview process. The District will then post and publish notice of provisional appointment. March 19, 2024 - Regular Governing Board meeting — Board to appoint the provisional Area 5 Board member and administer the oath of office. A Trustee appointed through provisional appointment shall hold office until the next governing board member election, which will occur at the Statewide General Election in November 2024. Coordination The Board President and Chancellor shall oversee coordinating the implementation of the option selected by the Board. In collaboration with the Santa Clara County Department of Elections and County Council, staff will work under the Chancellor's guidance to ensure the board vacancy is filled in accordance with the law. Follow-up/Outcome In the event that the Board makes a provisional appointment, it must post notices of the appointment and publish it in a newspaper of general circulation in Trustee Area 5 within 10 days. In the event that the Board fails to make a provisional appointment or order a special election, the Santa Clara County Superintendent of Schools shall order a special election to fill the vacancy. §
