Assembly Bill 2449 Remote Teleconference Attendance Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may be eligible to attend a Board of Education meeting remotely with Just Cause or with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance. To ensure compliance with the notice requirements of AB 2449, the Board will maintain an AB 2449 standing agenda item for each meeting. Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Just Cause submissions received for the current Board meeting. Consider and take action on Emergency Circumstance requests, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance and Board approval. Emergency Circumstance means a physical or family medical emergency that prevents the trustee from attending in person. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. Disclosure of individuals present at remote locations, if any, pursuant to AB 2449 . A trustee attending a Board meeting remotely under AB 2449 must publicly disclose whether any other individuals 18 years of age or older are present in the room at the remote location with the trustee, and the general nature of the members relationship with any such individuals. Any trustee attending the current Board meeting remotely under 2449 will make this public disclosure.

Prepared By: Kristen Jackanich, Luis Portillo, Doonu Barife, Chris Dyer and Joshua Moon-Johnson Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The general fund is the major source for the cost of instruction, which pertains to the offering of approved courses. These costs are offset, to varying degrees, by apportionment received for credit-based instruction and other associated fees. Reference(s) Curriculum development and recommendation is an ongoing activity overseen by the West Valley College Academic Senate, and its subordinate curriculum committee, and the office of instruction as per District Policy 4020. It is integrated into the West Valley College program review process. It is emphasized in many sections of Standard II of the Accreditation Standards of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. Background/Alternatives As per district policy, the curriculum changes to programs and courses described herein have been considered and recommended by the curriculum committee and the articulation officer. Degrees developed in compliance with state-approved Transfer Model Curricula (TMC) pursuant to SB-1440 (Padilla, 2010) have already been approved by the district chancellor acting as designee for the Board of Trustees, and are being presented here as information items. Coordination Offerings have been coordinated between West Valley College and Mission College through a process of consultation. Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. ACTUARIAL STUDY OF OTHER POST-EMPLOYMENT BENEFITS (OPEB) LIABILITIES FOR RETIREE HEALTH BENEFITS UNDER GASB 74/75 - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees accept the actuarial study of Other Post-Employment Benefits (OPEB) liabilities for retiree health benefits under GASB 74/75.

Prepared By: Ngoc Chim Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The recent valuation shows the district’s total OPEB liability (TOL) rose modestly from $55.8 million to $58.1 million; the district's OPEB trust holds enough assets to fully offset this liability. Reference(s) The Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) Statements 74 and 75 establish the accounting and financial reporting requirements for OPEB liabilities. This study was prepared in full compliance with these standards by Total Compensation Services, Inc. Background/Alternatives To effectively manage retiree health benefit costs, the district must periodically examine the total liability for retiree health benefits as well as future annual expected premium costs. GASB 74/75 requires a full valuation every other year, with an option to perform a roll-forward valuation in the intervening year. For June 30, 2025, the district selected the roll-forward valuation method to calculate the district's TOL. The report uses a measurement date of June 30, 2024, with a valuation date of June 30, 2023. For the FY 2025-26 statements, the district will prepare a full valuation with a measurement date of June 30, 2025. Coordination The vice chancellor of finance and administration worked with human resources staff and Total Compensation Services, Inc. to complete the report; it was then presented to the Board Audit and Budget Oversight Committee on May 6, 2025. Follow-up/Outcome No additional action is required. §

The district will continue to monitor cash flow monthly to ensure fiscal stability . Section III, Column 1 is the Adopted Budget for FY 2024-25, which was approved by the Board of Trustees on September 10, 2024. Annual Current Budget, Column 2, is the adjusted budget which includes the third quarter budget adjustments. The adjustments include the current allocations, major object budget transfers, and carryover balances that have been approved by the Board of Trustees. Year-to-date actuals, Column 3, are revenues and expenses posted in the general ledger as of March 31, 2025. The bottom section of the report addresses employee contract settlements and significant fiscal events. There were no employee contract settlements or significant fiscal events during the third quarter. The district does not have any known fiscal problems that must be addressed for FY 2024-25 or FY 2025-26. Coordination This report was prepared by the vice chancellor of finance and administration and reviewed by the chancellor. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the vice chancellor of finance and administration will certify and submit the CCFS-311Q Report to the State Chancellor’s Office. §

All budget transfers reflect either: Increases/decreases in expenditures that correspond to associated revenue increases/decreases; or Movement of equal budget amounts from one account to another; or Increases/decreases in expenditures or revenues that directly impact Fund Balance. Allocations of carry-overs result in a reduction of the beginning fund balances. Reference(s) Education Code Section 84040 provides that the governing board of a community college district, as required by regulations of the Board of Governors, shall periodically report information to the Board of Governors regarding the fiscal condition of the district. This assessment includes a comparison of budget to actual amounts. Once a budget is adopted, the total designated for each major expenditure classification is the maximum expenditure allowed. Second Reading: Presentation of the new West Valley College Mission, Vision and Values Statement - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct a second reading of the new West Valley College Mission, Vision and Values Statement, close public comment, and adopt the new statement.

Prepared By: Shusaku Horibe Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) Periodically, the college evaluates its mission to ensure alignment with Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges (ACCJC) requirements. The ACCJC requires each college to maintain and regularly review a clearly defined mission that reflects the institution’s character, values, and unique student demographics, while ensuring equitable educational opportunities and outcomes. Additionally, colleges must document formal procedures for this periodic review, engage stakeholders in any revisions, and secure governing board approval of the final mission. Background/Alternatives In Spring 2024, the College Council formed a Mission, Vision, and Values Review Taskforce comprising representatives from all constituency groups. The Taskforce was charged with reviewing the existing mission statement and developing a new mission, vision, and values statement that aligns with the district mission, the California Community Colleges Chancellor’s Office mission, and the updated 2024 ACCJC Standards. This process included ample opportunities for all stakeholders to provide input and feedback. Following multiple stakeholder engagement sessions, draft statements were circulated among the Academic Senate, Classified Senate, and Student Government. On March 13, 2025, the College Council approved the final statements and recommended them to the Board of Trustees. The new Mission comprises three key elements: the Mission Statement, the Vision Statement, and the Values Statement. The Vision Statement itself has two distinct parts: the Aspirational Goal and the Vivid Description. The Aspirational Goal provides a clear, compelling endpoint that unifies the college's efforts, while the Vivid Description offers a vibrant, engaging picture of what it looks and feels like to achieve that goal. Mission Statement West Valley College provides inclusive, accessible, transformative education to empower students to create positive change in their lives and communities. Values Statement WVC values inclusivity, actively cultivating a vibrant community that is not only welcoming but also fully accessible to everyone. WVC values compassion and encourages the community to lead with kindness, empathy, and care. WVC values excellence and creates an environment where high-quality teaching and learning thrive. WVC values service to our community while holding ourselves accountable to excellence and continuous growth. Vision Statement Vision – Aspirational Goal Empower generations of learners through education and service to community. Vision – Vivid Description West Valley College stands as a premier institution of higher learning in the heart of Silicon Valley. Our dynamic, rich learning environment relies on three primary components: Fiscal Leadership, Responsive Programming, and Proactive Community Relations. As one of 116 California's community colleges, we ensure access and create pathways to success for all. Our commitment to students is manifest in the following: Fiscal Leadership: Through responsible fiscal stewardship, we produce economic prosperity to create the conditions for students' success. The college is embedded in the economic and social fabric of the community. Responsive Programming: The college operations function at a high level to promote innovation and adapt to the needs of the community. We cultivate cutting-edge programs that respond to evolving local industry, social, and economic needs, preparing students for tomorrow's challenges. Responsive programming transforms individuals and communities of all nationalities and cultural backgrounds, contributing to awareness, civic responsibility, and social advancement. Proactive Community Relations: We forge vital partnerships with the communities of Saratoga, Campbell, Los Gatos, and surrounding cities, weaving together K-12 schools, universities, non-profits, corporations, businesses, philanthropic initiatives, and comprehensive student services into a tapestry of opportunity. Acknowledging and building on our rich heritage - beginning with our profound respect for the Muwekma Ohlone people, the original stewards of this land – we commit to being a catalyst for positive transformation in our community. For centuries, this land has witnessed transformation from tanneries and mineral springs to agricultural abundance and technological innovation. This legacy of adaptation and growth guides our vision for the future. In an era of profound social, political, and technological change, West Valley College stands ready to meet the moment. It requires us to come together as elected officials, trustees, entrepreneurs, executives, non-profits, elders, faculty, students, classified professionals, managers, coaches, counselors, and librarians to enact systemic change and progress for the benefit of our students. Together, we build more than a college; we cultivate a community where learning empowers, where individuals from varied backgrounds, identities, life experiences, perspectives, beliefs, and values are fully embraced, and where every student finds their path to excellence and prosperity. The college is committed to leading with love, empathy, and service as we remedy injustice and promote academic excellence. We will achieve the vision when generation to generation of students prosper. Coordination Development of the new West Valley College Mission, Vision, and Values Statements was coordinated by the Mission, Vision, and Values Review Taskforce. Input was received from internal and external stakeholders, and the Taskforce members partnered with their constituents to identify language and themes to be included. The final statements were drafted by the Taskforce and the college executive leadership team, and formally approved by College Council on March 13, 2025. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, the new college Mission will be published in all required locations, including the college catalog and website, and broadly disseminated throughout the campus community. The updated Mission, Vision, and Values Statements will also guide the college’s integrated planning processes. §
