AGREEMENT BETWEEN WEST VALLEY COLLEGE AND CAMPBELL UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT FOR A COLLEGE AND CAREER ACCESS PATHWAYS DUAL ENROLLMENT PARTNERSHIP 2025-2026 - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees receive, review, and approve the College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Dual Enrollment Partnership Agreement with Campbell Union High School District (CUHSD) as required by the California Education Code (EC § 76004(b)).

Prepared By: Chris Dyer Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact If approved, West Valley College will continue to offer community college pathway courses exclusively to CUHSD students at the West Valley College and Campbell Union High School District campuses. West Valley-Mission Community College District will claim apportionment for enrolled students under this CCAP Agreement. These classes will be in addition to course sections offered on the West Valley College campus and will generate full-time equivalent students (FTES). Reference(s) Education Code section 76004 (Assembly Bill 288) establishes the College and Career Access Pathways Act to authorize California Community College districts to enter into formal partnership agreements with local school districts. The goal is to create easier pathways from high school to community college and to offer or expand dual enrollment opportunities for under-represented higher education students or for students who may not be college bound. This partnership aligns with West Valley College’s Educational and Facilities Master Plan and Student Equity Plan goals: improving pathways to degrees and transfer, strengthening K-12 collaboration, and increasing access and success for underrepresented groups. On October 4, 2019, the Governor signed Assembly Bill 30 and Senate Bill 586, which amended the law to require that a proposed CCAP agreement be presented only once to each governing board. This agreement has been reviewed by the West Valley College president; vice president of academic affairs; vice provost; vice president of student services, diversity, and inclusion; and by the Campbell Union High School District superintendent and assistant superintendent. It is also being presented to the CUHSD Board and may undergo changes before final execution. Background/Alternatives Students who complete college credit while in high school are more likely than their peers to graduate, enroll in post-secondary education full-time, and complete degrees. CUHSD is a public-school district serving grades 9-12 with students whose residences are located within WVMCCD’s regional service area; its students deserve the accelerated pathway this CCAP agreement provides. Courses offered as part of this CCAP Agreement shall be community college courses acceptable towards a career technical education credential or certificate, or preparation for transfer, or appropriate to improve high school graduation rates or help high school pupils achieve college and career readiness. Coordination The operational and instructional aspects of this agreement will be administered and monitored by the college director of continuing education. An educational administrator to be specified in the appendix to the CCAP agreement will serve as point of contact to facilitate coordination and cooperation between the college and CUHSD, in conformity with WVMCCD policies and standards. Follow-up/Outcome After approval by both boards, West Valley College will submit the agreement to the State Chancellor’s Office for review and coordinate with CUHSD staff to implement the pathway in preparation for the next cohort in the fall 2025 semester . §

ADOPT RESOLUTION NO. 25061701 ENDORSING ASSEMBLY BILL 648 (ZBUR) — COMMUNITY COLLEGES: HOUSING: LOCAL ZONING REGULATIONS: EXEMPTION (2025-2026) – Recommendation: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 25061701 endorsing Assembly Bill 648, which authorizes California community college districts to construct student, faculty, and staff housing on district-owned or -leased land without being subject to municipal zoning ordinances.

Prepared By: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed By: Bradley Davis Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) Board Policies 6600 (Capital Construction) and 6910 (Housing) and Education Code Sections 70902 and 81330; Government Code Sections 53094, 65300 et seq., 65850, and 65860; and Title 5 Sections 57150 et seq. Goal 2 of the 2024-25 Annual Board of Trustees Goals, focusing on the management of district assets to meet student needs, with championing student housing solutions identified as a key measurement of success. Background/Alternatives California faces an acute shortage of affordable housing, with the Legislative Analyst’s Office reporting that fifty-eight percent of community-college students experience housing insecurity and nearly one quarter experience homelessness. AB 648 removes a core barrier—local zoning approval—that currently adds twelve to twenty-four months and significant litigation risk to campus housing timelines. By placing land-use authority solely with elected community-college governing boards, the bill de-risks project financing, aligns with the district 2024-25 Annual Board of Trustees Goals, and supports statewide student success by helping to streamline housing efforts which stabilize basic needs. Coordination Resolution No. 25061701 was reviewed by District Council on June 10, 2025, and by the Legislative Policy & Oversight Committee on June 11, 2025.
