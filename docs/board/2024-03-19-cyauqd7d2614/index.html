Prepared by: Rebecca Alvarez & Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed by: Bradley Davis Approved by: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The appointed Provisional Trustee will receive $400.00 per month and health benefits as afforded to elected Board members. Reference(s) Board Policies 2110 (Vacancies On The Board), 2725 (Board Member Compensation), and 2730 (Board Member Health Benefits); Government Code Sections 53201 and 53208.5; California Education Code sections 5091 et seq., 72024, and 72103. Back g round/Alternatives A vacancy has been created on the West Valley-Mission Community College District Board of Trustees due to the passing of Trustee Jack Lucas on January 24, 2024. In accordance with California Education Code sections 5091 et seq. and Board Policy 2110, the Board has 60 days from the effective date of the vacancy (January 24, 2024) to either call for a special election or make a provisional appointment. At the regular governing board meeting on February 13, 2024, the Board decided to proceed with a provisional appointment to fill this vacancy. Coordination The Board President and Chancellor oversaw the coordination and implementation of the provisional appointment. The Board of Trustees interviewed candidates at a Special Governing Board meeting on March 9, 2024. In collaboration with the Santa Clara County Department of Elections and County Council, staff worked under the Chancellor's guidance to ensure the board vacancy was to be filled in accordance with the law. Follow-up/Outcome Upon board approval, the Provisional Trustee will serve on the Board of Trustees until the next governing board member election, which will occur at the Statewide General Election in November 2024. §

APPROVE THE 2024-2025 NONRESIDENT TUITION RATE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees establish a nonresident tuition fee of $406 per credit unit and no foreign citizen capital outlay fee. In addition, the Board of Trustees approve a five-year phased implementation plan as allowed by California Education Code to meet the mandated fee amount with “gradual, moderate, and predictable” increases.

Prepared By: Sandy Dinh Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact In FY 2022-23, the District received $2,365,564 in nonresident tuition revenue. For FY 2023-24, the Board approved a nonresident tuition rate of $358 per unit with projected revenue of approximately $2,900,000. The estimated revenue for nonresident tuition for FY 2023-24 increased from FY 2022-23 by $534,436. This increase was primarily the result of an overall increase in nonresident students and fees. For FY 2024-25, the District is proposing to increase the nonresident tuition rate to $367 per unit, which is the statewide average. The estimated revenue for FY 2024-25 will be approximately $3,000,000. Reference(s) Education Code Section 76140 requires each district governing board to establish the nonresident tuition fee for the succeeding fiscal year. Pursuant to Education Code Section 76141, a district may also charge any nonresident student a capital outlay fee and pursuant to Education Code Section 76142, a processing fee may be charged to students from foreign countries. Additionally, the California Education Code establishes the methods available to community college districts to calculate nonresident tuition fees so that increases in these fees “shall be gradual, moderate, and predictable,” presumably to mitigate the potential for financial hardships disrupting education progress. In the event that the nonresident tuition fee calculation would result in a significant year-over-year increase, districts may adopt the fee along with a reasonable, phased implementation plan to meet the “gradual, moderate, and predictable” requirements. Background/Alternatives Education Code section 76140 specifies seven options to determine the nonresident tuition fee. The options are as follows: Nonresident Tuition Fee Options Per Credit Unit A.1 – District Average Cost $545 A.2 – District Average Cost with 10 percent or more noncredit FTES N/A B.1 – Statewide Average Cost $406 B.2 – Highest Statewide Average Cost $414 C – Contiguous District $406 to $545 D – Between Statewide Average Expense of Education and District Expense of Education $406 to $545 E – Comparable States Average $432 Nonresident students served by the colleges are not supported with state apportionment funds. Districts are allowed and required to collect tuition fees from those students who do not meet the residency requirements for the State of California. The amount charged for nonresident tuition is determined using information provided by the state. Districts are allowed to select from seven options in determining the amount to be charged for nonresident tuition. Revenue collected from nonresident students is retained by the District and does not affect the amount of state funding that is received. A summary of the options is provided below. Option A.1 – District Average Cost. This option allows the District to charge an amount equal to the cost of providing educational services to students. This is the full cost method, which recognizes that the District is providing services on a long-term basis and that nonresident students are factored into each of the college’s base operations. A disadvantage to this option is that some of the contiguous districts may charge less for tuition than West Valley - Mission’s actual cost of education. Option A.1 is not recommended for the West Valley-Mission Community College District because it would significantly increase the fees for nonresident students. ADOPT RESOLUTION NO. 24031902 ENDORSING ASSEMBLY BILL 3158 (BERMAN) COMMUNITY COLLEGES: WEST VALLEY-MISSION COMMUNITY COLLEGE DISTRICT (2023-2024) - Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 24031902 endorsing Assembly Bill 3158, which would authorize the West Valley-Mission Community College District to waive tuition and enrollment fees and use existing local funds to reduce costs for students furthering their education.

Prepared by: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed by: Bradley Davis Approved by: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact California Education Code § 76300 requires community college districts to charge students an enrollment fee of $46 per unit per semester. In 2022, the San Mateo County Community College District (SMCCCD) worked with legislative partners to enact SB 893, amending the education code to allow SMCCCD to use local unrestricted general funds to provide fee waivers to students with the greatest financial need. The West Valley-Mission Community College District endeavors to support AB 3158, introduced by Assemblymember Marc Berman, which would permit the District to do the same. Upon the change of the law, the District would be permitted to waive (or charge reduced) tuition and enrollment fees and use its local funds to provide students with free community college courses on our campuses. This change to the California Education Code would also reconcile any concerns relating to potential gifts of public funds. This program would not increase state funding for the District or commit the state to allocating additional resources. The legislation grants the District flexibility to determine all eligibility requirements. Reference(s) California Education Code 76300, Board Policies 2200 (Board Duties and Responsibilities), 2210 (Officers), 2220 (Committees of the Board), 2305 (Annual Organizational Meeting), 2310 (Regular meetings of the Board), 2340 (Agendas), 2345 (Public Participation at Board Meetings), 2360 (Minutes), 2365 (Recording), 2410 (Board Policies and Administrative Procedures), 2430 (Delegation of Authority to the Chancellor), 2710 (Conflict of Interest), 2716 (Political Activity), 2720 (Communications Among Board Members), Title 5 sections 53000 et seq., and 59300 et seq.; and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and Senate Bill 893. Back g round/Alternatives The resolution would authorize the Chancellor to work closely with state legislators on championing Assembly Bill 3158. This bill aims to allow the District to utilize unrestricted general funds, in addition to funding received under the California College Promise, to help students with the total cost of attendance by providing fee waivers to residents of the district service area . There are several additional transparency requirements and safeguards in the resolution, including: The West Valley-Mission Community College District Board of Trustees must establish a framework and adopt policies to determine eligibility. Students must demonstrate financial need (as determined by the District). Students must reside within the West Valley-Mission Community College District's boundaries, thus preventing any real or perceived competition with neighboring community college districts. West Valley-Mission Community College District must provide financial impact information at a public Board meeting showing that the District has the funds to reduce or waive student fees and is prohibited from receiving reimbursement from the state. The resolution incorporates the U.S. Department of Education’s definition of a student’s Total Cost of Attendance (COA). COA is the estimate of tuition and fees, cost of room and board (or living expenses), books, supplies, transportation, loan fees, and miscellaneous expenses (including a reasonable amount for the documented cost of a personal computer), allowance for childcare or other dependent care, costs related to a disability, and reasonable costs for eligible study-abroad programs. Existing law authorizes a community college that receives funding under the program to, among other things, waive some or all fees for up to 2 academic years for first-time students who are enrolled in 12 or more semester units or the equivalent at the college and complete and submit either a Free Application for Federal Student Aid or a California Dream Act application. Coordination Resolution No. 24031902 was reviewed by District Council on March 11, 2024, and by the Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on March 13, 2024. AB 3158 would require the West Valley-Mission Community College Board of Trustees to adopt appropriate and necessary policies regarding the qualifications of students eligible for reduced or waived fees. It would also require the District to prepare and submit a report to the Chancellor’s Office of the California Community Colleges, the Department of Finance, and the appropriate committees of the Legislature on the implementation of these provisions, including a 3-year projection of the fiscal impact of the fee waiver on the District. Follow-up/Outcome If adopted, the resolution will be publicly posted on each campus and available on the West Valley-Mission Community College District website. In addition, the approved Resolution No. 24031902 will be provided to the Chancellor’s Office of the California Community Colleges, the Department of Finance, and the appropriate members of the Legislature . §

ADOPT RESOLUTION NO. 24031903 ENDORSING SENATE BILL 895 (ROTH) COMMUNITY COLLEGES: BACCALAUREATE DEGREE IN NURSING PILOT PROGRAM (2023-2024) - Recommended Action: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 24031903 endorsing Senate Bill 895, which would require the Chancellor of the California Community Colleges to develop a pilot program that authorizes select community college districts to offer a Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree.

Prepared by: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed by: Bradley Davis Approved by: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) Board Policies 4020 (Curriculum Development), 4021 (Hours and Units), 4022 (Curriculum Approval), 4050 (Articulation), 4260 (Prerequisites and Co-Requisites) and Education Code Sections 66030, 66250 et seq., 87100 et seq., and 87100 et seq.; Title 5 sections 53000 et seq., and 59300 et seq.; and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964. Background/Alternatives Lawmakers recognize that California is confronted with a critical shortfall in the nursing workforce, exacerbated by the recent pandemic and anticipated retirements. In response to this and the increasing demand for Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) qualifications, Senate Bill 895 would require the Chancellor of the California Community Colleges to develop a Baccalaureate Degree in Nursing Pilot Program that authorizes select community college districts to offer a BSN degree. This bill aims to offer students a cost-effective pathway to secure the necessary qualifications for gainful employment while addressing the acute shortage of registered nurses. The pilot program shall be limited to 15 community college districts statewide. The State Chancellor shall identify eligible community college districts that apply based on the following criteria: There is equitable access between the northern, central, and southern parts of the state to the pilot program. Priority is given to community college districts in underserved nursing areas. The community college district has a nationally accredited nursing program. Each district’s pilot program shall limit the number of participants to 25 percent of the size of its Associate Degree in Nursing class, or 35 students, whichever is greater. The Legislative Analyst’s Office shall evaluate the pilot program to determine its effectiveness and the need to continue or expand it. Coordination Resolution No. 24031903 was reviewed by District Council on March 11, 2024, and by the Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on March 13, 2024. In the event of the bill's passage and the District's selection for the pilot program, the District will reassess the logistics and financial implications of establishing a baccalaureate program in nursing. Following this evaluation, the District will present its findings and propose the next steps to the Board at a subsequent Regular Governing Board meeting. Follow-up/Outcome If adopted, the resolution will be publicly posted on each campus and available on the West Valley-Mission Community College District website. The approved resolution will also be provided to the Chancellor’s Office of the California Community Colleges, the Department of Finance, and the appropriate members of the Legislature. §
