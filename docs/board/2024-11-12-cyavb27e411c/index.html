Follow-up/Outcome Following Board approval of the curriculum changes, updates to catalogs and class schedules will be made, and campus marketing communications will reflect the changes, as appropriate. First Reading - BOARD POLICY 2715 ON CODE OF ETHICS/STANDARDS OF PRACTICE. Recommendation: The Board of Trustees conduct a first reading of Board Policy 2715 on Code Of Ethics/Standards Of Practice and consider public comment.

Prepared By: Cade Story-Yetto Reviewed By: Cade Story-Yetto Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) ACCJC Accreditation Standard IV.C.11 Background/Alternatives This policy language was provided by the Community College League of California’s (CCLC) Policy & Procedure Subscription Service in partnership with the law firm of Liebert Cassidy Whitmore. This policy update establishes clearer standards for Trustee conduct, especially in communications and social media use, ensuring alignment with open meeting laws and civility expectations. It also provides protocol for managing potential conflicts of interest and outlines procedures for the censure of Trustees to uphold accountability and integrity within the Board. Coordination The policy was reviewed by the Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on October 30, 2024, and by District Council on November 4, 2024. This policy language was revised by legal counsel. Follow-up/Outcome At the December 4, 2024, Board of Trustees meeting, approval of this policy and the conclusion of the public comment period will be considered on the agenda. If adopted, this policy will be publicly available on the WVMCCD website. Following its refinement by the district vice president council and subsequent approval by the Chancellor, the related administrative procedure will be incorporated into the websites and catalogs of both Mission and West Valley College. §
