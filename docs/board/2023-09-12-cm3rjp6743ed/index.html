Assembly Bill 2449 Remote Teleconference Attendance Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may be eligible to attend a Board of Education meeting remotely with Just Cause or with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance. To ensure compliance with the notice requirements of AB 2449, the Board will maintain an AB 2449 standing agenda item for each meeting. Identify Just Cause submissions, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Just Cause. Just Cause includes a childcare or caregiving need, a contagious illness that prevents a trustee from attending in person, a need related to a qualifying physical or mental disability, and travel while on official business of the Board or another state or local agency. The Board will identify any Just Cause submissions received for the current Board meeting. Consider and take action on Emergency Circumstance requests, if any . Pursuant to 2449, a trustee may attend the Board meeting remotely with a qualifying Emergency Circumstance and Board approval. Emergency Circumstance means a physical or family medical emergency that prevents the trustee from attending in person. The Board will identify any Emergency Circumstance requests received for the current Board meeting, provide a general description of the circumstances, and take action to approve or deny the request. Disclosure of individuals present at remote locations, if any, pursuant to AB 2449 . A trustee attending a Board meeting remotely under AB 2449 must publicly disclose whether any other individuals 18 years of age or older are present in the room at the remote location with the trustee, and the general nature of the members relationship with any such individuals. Any trustee attending the current Board meeting remotely under 2449 will make this public disclosure. §

Prepared By: Patricia Nguyen Reviewed By: Terrance DeGray Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The funding for this project comes from Measure W, line items MC-06 of the Mission College project priority list. Reference(s) This item is associated with the implementation of the WVC Facilities Master Plan, the WVMCCD 5-Year Construction Plan, and the Measure W Project Priority List. Background/Alternatives At the July 18, 2023 Board meeting, the Board was presented with information and details by the Facilities team on alternative delivery methods including Design-Build. In a Design-Build project, the contractor and architect form a single design-build entity (DBE) to deliver a complete project based upon a conceptual plan provided by the District. This method allows for greater control over the schedule, quality of work, and the efficiency of the project, as conflicts between design and construction are significantly reduced. The District holds one contract with the DBE, and the DBE holds all subcontractor contracts. Due to the nature of the Mission College STEM Center project, the design-build delivery method is considered the best process to maintain District input during the design process, manage costs, control quality, and deliver the project on an expedited schedule. APPROVAL OF BID CA01-2324 AV BID PACKAGE FOR THE LRC RENOVATION PROJECT AT WEST VALLEY COLLEGE - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees approve the lowest responsive and responsible bid received from Avidex Industries in the amount of $2,774,082.54 and reject all other bids.

Prepared By: Patricia Nguyen Reviewed By: Terrance DeGray Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact The funding for this project comes from the Measure W project WV-03 Learning Resource Center Renovation of the District project priority list. The bid came in slightly over budget, staff will rebase at a later board meeting. Reference(s) This item is associated with the implementation of the WVC Facilities Master Plan, the WVMCCD 5-Year Construction Plan, and the Measure W bond program. Background/Alternatives The award of the contract is based on the base bid plus allowance and alternates as follows: Contractor Base Bid Allowance Award Avidex Industries $2,699,082.54 $75,000 $2,774,082.54 The District received two other bids: EIDIM Group $2,499,540.25 $75,000 $2,574,540.25 Integrated Communication Systems $2,511,116.00 $75,000 $2,585,116.00 A notice to bidders was advertised and bids were solicited from several contractors. Ten companies attended the pre-bid meeting; three bids were received. Bids were submitted through PlanetBids and publicly opened and read on August 17, 2023, at 2:00 PM. After a thorough review of the bidder’s qualifications documents, the District found that Avidex Industries was the only responsive bidder that met the personnel qualifications. Coordination This item includes the work of the associate vice chancellor of facilities development and operations, the executive director of general services, and the executive director of construction. Follow-up/Outcome Upon Board approval, a two phase contract will be issued to Avidex Industries in the amount of $2,774,540.25 for the LRC Renovation project at West Valley College. §
