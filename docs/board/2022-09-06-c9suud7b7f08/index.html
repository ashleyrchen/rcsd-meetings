5.3 · Action (Consent) · Consent Agenda

AGREEMENT BETWEEN WEST VALLEY-MISSION COMMUNITY COLLEGE DISTRICT AND THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA FOR THE 2022 MISSION COLLEGE CALIFORNIA NEW AND INNOVATIVE GRANT—THE CALIFORNIA APPRENTICESHIP INITIATIVE —FOR TRANSIT APPRENTICESHIP. Recommended Action: The Governing Board accept the $499,905 Agreement covering the period of October 2022 through June 2025.

Prepared By: Jeff Pallin and Lorrie Ranck Reviewed By: Seher Awan Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact Funding will come from the CAI Grant provided by the state. These funds are specifically designated for curriculum development, project administration, employer-based instruction, supplies and materials, and related indirect costs. The budget over the grant period has been approved by the state in an award dated February 4, 2022, and an MOU signed by the district executive director of general services, Michael Robbins, on August 3, 2022. Reference(s) This grant will support two of the college&rsquo;s local vision goals: Goal 4 is to &ldquo;Expand Interconnectedness with the Community;&rdquo; and Goal 1B is to &ldquo;Increase All Students Who Earn a Chancellor&rsquo;s Office Approved Certificate.&rdquo; Additional benefits of this grant will be providing a more diverse pool of students as VTA continues to recruit employees from the local area. It will also enable Mission College&rsquo;s new Transit Hub to work with other transit agencies and colleges to develop their own Apprenticeship Partnerships and Programs in Public Transportation. Background/Alternatives This grant enables the college to continue supporting the efforts of the JWI in addressing workforce development issues in Santa Clara County for Public Transportation. The work in this grant will help the college increase student success metrics and show a demonstrative increase and improvement in the earnings of students completing the program. The High Roads Training Partnership Grant of 2022 (ending on September 30, 2022) has enabled the college to research and establish the Transit Hub. This CAI Grant is the culmination of many years of the successful JWI Partnership. It positions the college to meet the needs of the local and regional economies. California is calling on public transit to help lead the transition to a more sustainable transportation future, e.g., phasing in zero emission buses (ZEBs) in transit fleets, with a goal of 100% deployment of clean-energy transit vehicle fleets by 2040 (Innovative Clean Transit Rule, 2018). All public transit therefore will need to adapt their operations and workforce to the changes in technology and skills that accompany deployment of ZEBs, which include battery-powered and fuel cell buses. VTA needs to train employees in the skills necessary to maintain and repair the new electric vehicles and to create a culture of continuous learning to keep up with rapidly changing technology. The Apprenticeship Program at Mission College and the JWI partnership are focused on improving diversity, equity, inclusion, and belonging in the Public Transportation Industry Sector. The Santa Clara Valley Transportation Authority (VTA) has partnered with Amalgamated Transit Union (ATU), Local 265, California Transit Works (CTW), and Mission College to support the Joint Workforce Investment (JWI), in our innovative transit operations partnership. We will expand the public transit apprenticeship offerings, first created in 2016 and funded by the California Apprenticeship Initiative (CAI), to include a new Light Rail Operator Apprenticeship Certificate program and transform the existing Public Transit Leadership Certificate into a formal apprenticeship at Mission. Project partners are part of the existing California High Road Training Partnership (HRTP), awarded for $125,000 in September 2021, accepted by the Board in October 2021 to cover the period from January 1, 2022, to September 30, 2022. HRTP represents foundational work for this larger, multi-year grant. Mission College currently runs a nationally recognized, successful, transit apprenticeship where more than 400 apprentices have completed their learning and on-the-job training to date, transitioning to new careers at VTA in positions such as Coach Operator and Overhead Line Worker. On August 3, 2022, Mission College enrolled 22 new employees of VTA into the Coach Operator Apprenticeship Program. On August 22, 2022, Mission College will begin a new Overhead Line Apprenticeship program. This grant covers two new apprenticeship programs. The Public Transit Leadership Program will prepare incumbent workers who are currently peer mentors with professional skills and lead to a Certificate of Achievement in Public Transit Leadership. Upon successful completion of 18 units of coursework, graduates will be qualified as Community College Apprenticeship Instructors in Transit. The Light Rail Operator Apprenticeship includes 10 weeks of full-time classroom training followed by 15.5 months of on-the-job training, complemented by mentoring and additional credit-bearing training integrated into the on-the-job component provided through JWI. Upon completion, light rail apprenticeship graduates will become Journey Level Light Rail Operators with a Certificate of Completion of Light Rail Operator Apprenticeship. Both programs will be a continuation of the highly successful JWI partnership and will provide equitable opportunities for higher wages, promote professionalism, and encourage education and certification for all transit workers. Moreover, both programs will incorporate a set of supports to accelerate advancement. In addition to partner support, future funding sources will include Related Supplement Instruction (RSI) and Full-Time Equivalent Student (FTES) funding. Coordination Mission College will use this CAI Grant in Transportation to prototype and test a framework for coordinating programs in Work Experience, Internship, and Apprenticeship to achieve faculty engagement and student success. Follow-up/Outcome The dean of business, technology, and kinesiology, tasked with managing this grant and the partnership with the JWI, will provide regular progress reports to the Mission College Executive Cabinet. §

RESOLUTION NO. 22090601 TO ESTABLISH THE 2022-2023 APPROPRIATION LIMIT - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 22090601 to establish the 2022-2023 appropriations limit at $200,747,671.

Prepared By: Susan Victoria Reviewed By: Ngoc Chim Approved By: Bradley Davis Fundin g Source / Fiscal Im p act Prior to the adoption of its final budget, each state governmental entity must calculate its appropriations limit. The limit that was first calculated in 1979 may be modified each year by a factor composed of a change in student population (FTES) and the lesser of the change in the Consumer Price Index or California per capita income. Although the state is approaching its Gann Limit, few school districts or community colleges have approached theirs. West Valley-Mission CCD&rsquo;s appropriations limit, as calculated according to the provisions of Article XIII-B, is much larger than the District&rsquo;s actual appropriations. The District&#39;s 2022-2023 appropriations subject to limitation is $150,577,054. The District&#39;s 2022-2023 appropriations limit, based on population growth/decline, has been determined to be $200,747,671 (worksheet attached). The District&#39;s appropriations subject to the limit for 2022-2023 does not exceed the appropriations limit. Reference(s) Article XIII-B of the California Constitution, commonly referred to as the Gann Limit, was approved by the voters in 1979. Proposition 4 was passed by the voters of the state of California in November of 1979. Later, implementing legislation in the form of SB 1352 was passed in the California legislature and signed by the Governor. SB 1352 requires the following: &quot;Government Code Section 7910: Each year the governing board of each local jurisdiction shall by resolution establish its appropriations limit for the following fiscal year pursuant to Article XIII-B at a regularly scheduled meeting or at a special meeting. Fifteen (15) days prior to such meeting, documentation used in the determination of the appropriations limit shall be available to the public. The determination of the appropriations limit is a legislative act.&quot; Back gr ound / Alternatives In accordance with the provisions of Government Code Section 7910, any action to set aside or annul the establishment of the appropriations limit must be filed with the State Chancellor within forty-five (45) days of the adoption of the resolution. Coordination According to law, the &quot;documentation used in the determination of such limit shall be made available to the public fifteen (15) days prior to the actual adoption of such resolution.&quot; In compliance with the requirement, the documentation was made available to the public on August 22, 2022 . Follow-u p/ Outcomes Upon Board approval, the Gann Limit information will be reported on the CCSF-311 Report, which is due on or before October 10, 2022. §
