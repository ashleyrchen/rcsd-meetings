WEST VALLEY-MISSION COMMUNITY COLLEGES DISTRICT RESOLUTION 23051601 SUPPORTING ASSEMBLY BILL 358, THE COMMUNITY COLLEGE HOUSING ACT INTRODUCED BY ASSEMBLY MEMBER ADDIS – Recommendation: The Board of Trustees adopt Resolution No. 23051601 supporting Assembly Bill 358 expediting the development of community college district student housing projects important for addressing high levels of housing and basic needs insecurity for students.

Prepared by: Manny Cappello Reviewed by: Bradley Davis Approved by: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact There is no fiscal impact. Reference(s) Board Policies 3410 (Nondiscrimination), 5030 (Fees), 5050 (Student Success and Support Program), 5130 (Financial Aid), 5150 (Extended Opportunity Programs and Service), 5300 (Student Equity), and Education Code Sections 66030, 66250 et seq., 87100 et seq., and 87100 et seq.; Title 5 sections 53000 et seq., and 59300 et seq.; and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964. Background/Alternatives The severity of the housing crisis is greatly visible across California. Although the cost of housing and the shortage of supply has affected millions of Californians, it has created unique challenges for California&rsquo;s students who often lack the financial resources and support to compete in today&rsquo;s housing market. In the case of low-income students without parental support, the cost of housing and other basic needs will be the most expensive barrier to higher education, even when the cost of tuition is covered. Although some students may qualify for traditional affordable housing, students themselves are not designated as a Special Needs Population and are not included in the Regional Housing Needs Assessment, which quantifies the need for housing within each jurisdiction during specified planning periods. Therefore, students suffer from the lack of protections from the state or their local governments, relying on their campuses to provide housing. Campuses, themselves however, suffer from increasing on-campus housing shortages, leading to escalating housing costs and extensive waiting lists. This is especially the case at the California Community Colleges, which originally did not provide housing as they catered to &ldquo;commuter&rdquo; students. As a result, many community colleges are scrambling to erect housing amongst a dire landscape, all while experiencing limited land availability, high costs, and long completion delays. The Field Act requires the Department of General Services to supervise the design and construction of any school building, including both school district and community college district buildings, or, if the estimated cost exceeds $100,000, the reconstruction or alteration of or addition to any school building, to ensure that plans and specifications comply with the rules and regulations adopted pursuant to the act and with relevant building standards, and to ensure that the work of construction has been performed in accordance with the approved plans and specifications. Existing law requires the Department of General Services, for purposes relating to access and use by persons with disabilities, to issue a written approval of the plans and specifications of certain buildings and facilities, as provided. Existing law defines &ldquo;school building&rdquo; for these purposes, and excludes from that definition any building that serves or is intended to serve as residential housing for school district and community college district teachers and employees, and their families. This bill would additionally exclude from these requirements any building used as a residence for students attending a campus of a community college district, except upon a request by the community college district, as specified. Coordination Resolution No. 23051601 was reviewed by District Council on May 8, 2023 and the Legislative Policy and Oversight Committee on May 10, 2023. Follow-up/Outcome The Chancellor will publicly post this resolution on each campus and the WVMCCD websites, and email the approved Resolution 23051601 to the office of Assembly Member Addis and the California Community College League. §
