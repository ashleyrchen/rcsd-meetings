AGREEMENT BETWEEN WEST VALLEY COLLEGE AND LOS GATOS-SARATOGA UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT FOR A COLLEGE AND CAREER ACCESS PATHWAYS MIDDLE COLLEGE PARTNERSHIP 2025-2026 - Recommendation: The Board of Trustees receive, review, and approve the College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Middle College Partnership Agreement with Los Gatos-Saratoga Union High School District (LGSUHSD) as required by the California Education Code (EC § 76004(b)).

Prepared By: Anthony Djedi Reviewed By: Jennifer Taylor-Mendoza Approved By: Bradley Davis Funding Source/Fiscal Impact If approved, West Valley College will continue to offer community college pathway courses exclusively to LGSUHSD students at the West Valley College and Los Gatos-Saratoga Union High School District campuses. West Valley-Mission Community College District will claim apportionment for enrolled students under this CCAP Agreement. These classes will be in addition to course sections offered on the West Valley College campus and will generate full-time equivalent students (FTES). Reference(s) Education Code section 76004 (Assembly Bill 288) establishes the College and Career Access Pathways Act to authorize California Community College districts to enter into formal partnership agreements with local school districts. The goal is to create easier pathways from high school to community college and to offer or expand dual enrollment opportunities for under-represented higher education students or for students who may not be college-bound. This partnership aligns with West Valley College’s Educational and Facilities Master Plan and Student Equity Plan goals: improving pathways to degrees and transfer, strengthening K-12 collaboration, and increasing access and success for underrepresented groups. On October 4, 2019, the Governor signed Assembly Bill 30 and Senate Bill 586, which amended the law to require that a proposed CCAP agreement be presented only once to each governing board. This agreement has been reviewed by the West Valley College president, vice president of academic affairs, vice provost, vice president of student affairs, vice president of strategy and equity, and by the Los Gatos-Saratoga Union High School District superintendent and assistant superintendent. It is also being presented to the LGSUHSD Board and may undergo changes before final execution. Background/Alternatives Students who complete college credit while in high school are more likely than their peers to graduate, enroll in post-secondary education full-time, and complete degrees. LGSUHSD is a public school district serving grades 9-12 with students whose residences are located within WVMCCD’s regional service area; its students deserve the accelerated pathway this CCAP agreement provides. Courses offered as part of this CCAP Agreement shall be community college courses acceptable towards a career technical education credential or certificate, or preparation for transfer, or appropriate to improve high school graduation rates, or help high school pupils achieve college and career readiness. Coordination The operational and instructional aspects of this agreement will be administered and monitored by the college director of continuing education. An educational administrator specified in the appendix to the CCAP agreement will serve as the point of contact to facilitate coordination and cooperation between the college and LGSUHSD, in conformity with WVMCCD policies and standards. Follow-up/Outcome After approval by both boards, West Valley College will submit the agreement to the State Chancellor’s Office for review and coordinate with LGSUHSD staff to implement the pathway in preparation for the next cohort in the fall 2025 semester. §
